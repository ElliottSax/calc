## Understanding the Basic Dividend Income Formula

The fundamental calculation for annual dividend income is:

```
Annual Dividend Income = Number of Shares × Dividend Per Share × Payment Frequency
```

For example:
- 100 shares of Johnson & Johnson (JNJ)
- $4.52 annual dividend per share
- Quarterly payments (4x per year)
- Result: 100 × $4.52 = $452 annual dividend income

### Step 2: Determine Portfolio Size Requirements

To calculate required portfolio size:
```
Required Portfolio Size = (Annual Income Needed ÷ Average Dividend Yield) × 100
```

Using common dividend yield scenarios:
- 3% average yield: $1,600,000 portfolio needed ($48,000 ÷ 0.03)
- 4% average yield: $1,200,000 portfolio needed ($48,000 ÷ 0.04)
- 5% average yield: $960,000 portfolio needed ($48,000 ÷ 0.05)

## Tax Considerations for Dividend Income

### Different Tax Treatments

1. Qualified Dividends
- Taxed at long-term capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20%)
- Must meet holding period requirements

2. Non-qualified Dividends
- Taxed as ordinary income
- Higher tax rates apply

### Tax-Advantaged Accounts

Consider using:
- Traditional IRA
- Roth IRA
- 401(k)
- Health Savings Account (HSA)
