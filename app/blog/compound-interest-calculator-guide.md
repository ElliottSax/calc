# Compound Interest Calculator: How to Calculate Investment Growth

## What Is Compound Interest and How Do You Calculate It?

Compound interest is interest earned on both principal and previously accumulated interest, calculated using the formula A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt) where A = final amount, P = principal, r = annual rate, n = compounding frequency, t = years. Example: $10,000 invested at 10% annual return compounding monthly for 20 years = $10,000 × (1 + 0.10/12)^(12×20) = $67,275 (versus $30,000 simple interest). Key insight: monthly compounding yields 5-8% more than annual compounding over 20+ year periods due to reinvestment effect—$10,000 at 10% for 20 years = $67,275 monthly vs $67,275 annually = $61,159 difference.
