### Example Calculation

Let's run through a practical example:

* Annual Salary: $80,000
* Contribution Rate: 10%
* Employer Match: 50% up to 6% of salary
* Age: 45

**Step-by-Step Calculation:**
1. Employee Contribution: $80,000 × 10% = $8,000
2. Employer Match: $80,000 × 6% × 50% = $2,400
3. Total Annual Contribution: $8,000 + $2,400 = $10,400

## Strategic Planning for Maximum Benefits

### 1. Optimal Contribution Strategies

#### For High-Income Earners
* Contribute early in the year to maximize compound growth
* Consider front-loading contributions while ensuring you don't miss employer matches
* Example: $24,000 contribution spread across first 6 months = $4,000/month

**Related**: [401k Contribution Limit 2026: Calculator & Strategy](/401k-contribution-limit-2026:-calculator-&-strateg)


#### For Mid-Range Income
* At minimum, contribute enough to get full employer match
* Increase contributions by 1% annually
* Example: $60,000 salary, 6% contribution = $3,600/year + employer match

### 2. Catch-up Contribution Strategy

For those 50 and older:
* Plan to maximize both standard and catch-up contributions
* Total potential contribution: ~$32,000 (projected 2026)
* Consider splitting additional contributions between 401k and Roth IRA
