## Basic Dividend Growth Rate Formula

**Related**: [How To Calculate Dividend Growth Rate_2](/how-to-calculate-dividend-growth-rate_2)

The simplest way to calculate dividend growth rate between two periods is:

```
Growth Rate = (Ending Value - Beginning Value) / Beginning Value × 100
```

### Example Calculation

Let's say Company XYZ paid:
- 2022 dividend: $2.00 per share
- 2023 dividend: $2.20 per share

Growth Rate = ($2.20 - $2.00) / $2.00 × 100 = 10%

## Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)

For longer periods, the Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) provides a more accurate picture of dividend growth. The formula is:

```
CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/n) - 1
```
Where 'n' is the number of years

### CAGR Example

Consider Johnson & Johnson's dividend history:
- 2018 annual dividend: $3.54
- 2023 annual dividend: $4.76
- Time period: 5 years

CAGR = ($4.76 / $3.54)^(1/5) - 1
= 1.0610 - 1
= 0.0610 or 6.10%

## Rolling Average Growth Rate

To smooth out short-term fluctuations, many investors use a rolling average growth rate.

**Related**: [How To Calculate Dividend Growth Rate_4](/how-to-calculate-dividend-growth-rate_4)

### 3-Year Rolling Average Example

Company ABC's dividend increases:
- 2021: 8%
- 2022: 12%
- 2023: 7%

3-year rolling average = (8% + 12% + 7%) / 3 = 9%

## Advanced Methods for Calculating Dividend Growth

### Geometric Mean Method

This method is useful when dealing with varying growth rates over multiple periods.

Formula:
```
Geometric Mean = ((1 + r1) × (1 + r2) × ... × (1 + rn))^(1/n) - 1
```

Where r1, r2, etc. are individual period growth rates

**Related**: [How To Calculate Dividend Growth Rate_10](/how-to-calculate-dividend-growth-rate_10)

### Example:
Growth rates:
- Year 1: 5%
- Year 2: 8%
- Year 3: 6%

Calculation:
((1.05 × 1.08 × 1.06)^(1/3)) - 1 = 6.32%

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using Too Short a Time Period**
- Solution: Analyze at least 5-10 years of dividend history

2. **Ignoring Payout Ratio**
- Solution: Check if growth is sustainable based on earnings

3. **Not Considering Special Dividends**
- Solution: Focus on regular dividend payments only

4. **Overlooking Currency Effects**
- Solution: Convert all dividends to same currency before calculating

## Practical Applications

### Portfolio Analysis

1. Calculate weighted average dividend growth:
```
Portfolio Growth = Σ(Stock Weight × Individual Growth Rate)
```

2. Compare against benchmarks:
- S&P 500 dividend growth rate
- Industry averages
- Inflation rate

### Investment Screening

Create minimum criteria:
- 5-year dividend growth > 5%
- 10-year dividend growth > 3%
- Payout ratio < 75%

## Case Study: Dividend Aristocrat Analysis

Let's analyze Procter & Gamble (PG):

Historical Dividends:
- 2013: $2.29
- 2018: $2.79
- 2023: $3.76

5-year CAGR (2018-2023):
= ($3.76 / $2.79)^(1/5) - 1
= 6.15%

10-year CAGR (2013-2023):
= ($3.76 / $2.29)^(1/10) - 1
= 5.08%
