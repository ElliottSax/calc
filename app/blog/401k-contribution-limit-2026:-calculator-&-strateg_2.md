## 401k Contribution Calculator for 2026

Use this framework to calculate your optimal contribution strategy:

### Basic Calculation Formula
```
Annual Salary × Contribution Percentage = Annual Contribution
Example: $100,000 × 15% = $15,000 annual contribution
```

### Maximum Contribution Timeline Calculator
```
(Contribution Limit - Current Contributions) ÷ Remaining Pay Periods = Per-Paycheck Contribution Needed
Example: ($24,000 - $5,000) ÷ 18 remaining pay periods = $1,055.56 per paycheck
```

## Strategic Planning for 2026 Contributions

### 1. Employer Match Optimization

Most employers offer a matching contribution, typically structured as follows:

- **Common Match**: 50% or 100% of employee contributions
- **Match Limit**: Usually up to 3-6% of salary

**Example:**
For a $80,000 salary with 100% match up to 5%:
- Annual contribution needed for full match: $4,000
- Employer match: $4,000
- Total retirement savings: $8,000

### 2. Contribution Timing Strategies

#### Even Distribution
- Divide annual goal by pay periods
- Example: $24,000 ÷ 24 pay periods = $1,000 per paycheck

#### Front-Loading
Benefits:
- Earlier market exposure
- Guaranteed maximum contribution
- Protection against job changes

Caution: Ensure you don't miss out on employer matches later in the year

### 3. Age-Based Strategies

#### Under 50
- Focus on reaching standard contribution limit
- Prioritize Roth vs. Traditional based on tax bracket
- Consider supplemental IRA contributions

#### Over 50
- Maximize catch-up contributions
- Total potential contribution: $32,000-$33,000 (projected 2026)
- Consider tax implications of increased contributions
