## Standard 401k Contribution Limits

For 2025, the IRS has announced the following contribution limits:

- Base employee contribution limit: $23,000 (increased from $22,500 in 2024)
- Catch-up contribution limit (age 50+): $7,500
- Total maximum contribution (employee + employer): $69,000
- Combined maximum with catch-up: $76,500

Learn more: [DRIP strategy guide](/guides/drip-investing)

### Special Catch-Up Provisions for 2025

For employees with annual earnings of $145,000 or more:
- Standard catch-up contributions must be made as Roth contributions
- This applies to both 401k and 403(b) plans
- Lower-income employees maintain choice between traditional and Roth catch-up contributions

## 401k Contribution Calculator

Use this formula to determine your optimal contribution rate:

```
Monthly Contribution = (Annual Goal ÷ 12) ÷ Monthly Gross Income
```

### Example Calculation:

Annual Salary: $80,000
Desired Contribution: 15%

```
Monthly Contribution = ($12,000 ÷ 12) = $1,000 per month
Percentage of Income = ($1,000 ÷ $6,667) = 15%
```

## Strategic Planning for 2025 Contributions

### 1. Maximize Employer Match

Most employers offer a matching contribution structure similar to:
- 100% match on first 3% of salary
- 50% match on next 2% of salary

Example with $80,000 salary:
- First 3%: $2,400 (100% matched = $2,400)
- Next 2%: $1,600 (50% matched = $800)
- Total employer contribution: $3,200

### 2. Contribution Timing Strategies

#### Front-Loading vs. Dollar-Cost Averaging

**Front-Loading Benefits:**
- Earlier market exposure
- Maximizes time in market
- Ensures full contribution before unexpected changes

**Dollar-Cost Averaging Benefits:**
- Reduces timing risk
- Maintains consistent cash flow
- Ensures year-round employer matching

### 3. Tax Optimization Strategy

Consider this three-tier approach:

1. **Tier 1: Employer Match**
   - Contribute minimum needed for full match
   - Typical target: 5-6% of salary

2. **Tier 2: Tax Bracket Management**
   - Contribute enough to lower taxable income
   - Example: Reduce from 24% to 22% bracket

3. **Tier 3: Maximum Contribution**
   - Fill remaining space up to annual limit
   - Consider Roth vs. Traditional split

## Advanced Contribution Strategies

### 1. Mega Backdoor Roth

If your plan allows after-tax contributions:

1. Contribute maximum $69,000 (2025 limit)
2. Convert after-tax portion to Roth
3. Benefit from tax-free growth

### 2. Age-Based Contribution Strategy

#### Under 40:
- Focus on Roth contributions
- Aggressive investment allocation
- Target 15-20% total savings rate

#### 40-50:
- Mix of Traditional and Roth
- Moderate allocation
- Target 20-25% savings rate

#### Over 50:
- Maximize catch-up contributions
- Conservative-moderate allocation
- Target 25%+ savings rate

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Missing Employer Match

### 2. Poor Investment Selection
- Average return loss: 1.3% annually
- Solution: Review fund expenses and asset allocation

### 3. Improper Contribution Timing
- Risk: Missing full employer match
- Solution: Spread contributions throughout year
