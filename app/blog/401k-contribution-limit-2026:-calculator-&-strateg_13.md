## Strategic Planning for Maximum Benefits

### 1. Employer Match Optimization

Most employers offer a matching contribution. Here's how to maximize it:

- **Common Match Structure:** 50% up to 6% of salary
- **Strategy:** At minimum, contribute enough to get full employer match
- **Example:** On $80,000 salary with 6% match
  - Your contribution: $4,800
  - Employer match: $2,400
  - Total annual benefit: $7,200

### 2. Age-Based Strategies

#### Under 50:
- Focus on reaching maximum standard contribution
- Consider additional retirement vehicles (IRA, HSA)
- Build consistent contribution habits

#### Over 50:
- Utilize catch-up contributions
- Accelerate savings as retirement approaches
- Consider tax implications of increased contributions

## Advanced Contribution Strategies for 2026

### 1. Dollar-Cost Averaging

Instead of trying to time the market, spread contributions throughout the year:

- **Monthly Contribution:** Annual limit ÷ 12
- **Example:** $23,500 ÷ 12 = $1,958 monthly

### 2. Front-Loading vs. Back-Loading

#### Front-Loading Benefits:
- Earlier market exposure
- Potentially higher returns
- Guaranteed maximum contribution

#### Back-Loading Benefits:
- More consistent cashflow throughout year
- Better for variable income earners
- Easier budgeting

## Tax Implications and Benefits

### Traditional 401k Benefits:

### Example Tax Savings:

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Missing Employer Match**
   - Cost of missing 6% match on $80,000 salary = $2,400/year

2. **Incorrect Contribution Timing**
   - Reaching limit too early and missing employer matches
   - Solution: Spread contributions across the year

3. **Ignoring Catch-up Contributions**
   - Additional $7,500+ potential savings for age 50+
