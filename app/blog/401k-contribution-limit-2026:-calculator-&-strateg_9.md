## 401k Contribution Calculator for 2026

### Basic Calculator Formula

To determine your optimal contribution, use this formula:

```
Annual Contribution = (Annual Salary × Contribution %) + Employer Match
```

### Example Calculation

Let's consider an employee earning $80,000 annually:

- Salary: $80,000
- Contribution Rate: 10%
- Employer Match: 50% up to 6% of salary

```
Employee Contribution: $80,000 × 10% = $8,000
Employer Match: $80,000 × 6% × 50% = $2,400
Total Annual Contribution: $10,400
```

## Strategic Planning for 2026 Contributions

### 1. Maximize Employer Match

**Strategy**: Always contribute at least enough to get the full employer match - it's free money!

### 2. Age-Based Strategies

#### For Workers Under 50
- Aim to contribute at least 15% of gross income
- Consider increasing contributions by 1% annually
- Focus on long-term growth investments

#### For Workers 50 and Older
- Take advantage of catch-up contributions
- Consider more conservative investment allocations
- Max out contributions if possible

### 3. Income-Based Contribution Strategies

| Annual Income | Recommended Contribution |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Under $50k | 10-12% |
| $50k-$100k | 12-15% |
| $100k-$200k | 15-20% |
| $200k+ | Maximum allowed |

## Optimizing Your 401k in 2026

### 1. Regular Contribution Adjustments

- Review contribution levels quarterly
- Adjust for salary increases
- Consider automatic escalation programs

### 2. Investment Allocation Strategy

- Diversify across asset classes
- Consider target-date funds
- Rebalance annually

### 3. Tax Considerations

#### Traditional vs. Roth 401k Options
- **Traditional**: Pre-tax contributions, taxed at withdrawal
- **Roth**: After-tax contributions, tax-free withdrawals
- Consider splitting contributions between both types

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How are dividends taxed?

Qualified dividends are taxed at capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20%). Non-qualified dividends are taxed as ordinary income.

### What is DRIP investing?

Dividend Reinvestment Plans automatically use dividends to buy more shares, compounding your returns over time.

### How much do I need to retire?

A common rule is 25x your annual expenses, though individual needs vary based on lifestyle and healthcare costs.
