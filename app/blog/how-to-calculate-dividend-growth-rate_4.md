## Basic Dividend Growth Rate Formula

The simplest way to calculate dividend growth rate between two periods is:

```
Growth Rate = (Ending Value - Beginning Value) / Beginning Value × 100
```

### Example:
If Company XYZ paid $1.00 per share in 2021 and $1.10 in 2022:

Growth Rate = ($1.10 - $1.00) / $1.00 × 100 = 10%

## Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)

For longer periods, the Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) provides a more accurate picture of dividend growth. The formula is:

```
CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/n) - 1
```
Where n = number of years

### Practical Example:
Let's say Johnson & Johnson paid dividends of:
- 2018: $3.54
- 2023: $4.76
- Time period: 5 years

CAGR = ($4.76 / $3.54)^(1/5) - 1
     = 1.0610 - 1
     = 0.0610 or 6.10%

## Methods for Calculating Dividend Growth Rate

### 1. Historical Average Method

Calculate year-over-year growth rates and take the average.

Example using Procter & Gamble's dividends:
- 2019: $2.98
- 2020: $3.16 (6.04% growth)
- 2021: $3.32 (5.06% growth)
- 2022: $3.52 (6.02% growth)
- 2023: $3.76 (6.82% growth)

Average growth rate = (6.04% + 5.06% + 6.02% + 6.82%) / 4 = 5.99%

### 2. Linear Regression Method

This method uses statistical analysis to find the best-fit line through historical dividend data points. While more complex, it can provide more accurate results by reducing the impact of outliers.

### 3. Rolling Average Method

Calculate growth rates using rolling periods (e.g., 3-year or 5-year windows) to smooth out short-term fluctuations.

### 1. Using Too Short a Time Period
- Minimum 5-year history recommended
- Longer periods provide more reliable trends
- Consider full business cycles

### 2. Ignoring Special Dividends
- Focus on regular dividend payments
- Exclude one-time special dividends
- Account for stock splits

### 3. Not Considering Sustainability
- Check payout ratio trends
- Analyze free cash flow coverage
- Evaluate earnings growth

## Practical Application: Dividend Growth Analysis

Let's analyze a real-world example using Microsoft (MSFT):

Annual Dividends per Share:
- 2019: $1.84
- 2020: $2.04
- 2021: $2.24
- 2022: $2.48
- 2023: $2.72

### Step-by-Step Analysis:

1. Calculate year-over-year growth rates:
- 2019-2020: 10.87%
- 2020-2021: 9.80%
- 2021-2022: 10.71%
- 2022-2023: 9.68%

2. Calculate 5-year CAGR:
CAGR = ($2.72 / $1.84)^(1/4) - 1 = 10.26%

3. Evaluate sustainability:
- Strong free cash flow coverage
- Low payout ratio (~30%)
- Consistent earnings growth
