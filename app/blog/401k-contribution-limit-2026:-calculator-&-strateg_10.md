## 401k Contribution Calculator for 2026

### How to Calculate Your Maximum Contribution

Use this simple formula to determine your per-paycheck contribution:

```
Annual Contribution Goal ÷ Number of Pay Periods = Per-Paycheck Contribution
```

**Example Calculation:**
- Annual Goal: $23,000
- Bi-weekly Pay (26 periods)
- $23,000 ÷ 26 = $884.62 per paycheck

## Maximizing Your 401k Strategy for 2026

### 1. Employer Match Optimization

Always aim to capture the full employer match. Here's a practical example:

- Annual Salary: $80,000
- Employer Match: 5% (dollar-for-dollar)
- Minimum Contribution Needed: $4,000
- Annual Employer Match: $4,000
- Total Annual Investment: $8,000

### 2. Strategic Contribution Timing

Consider these approaches:

- **Front-loading**: Contributing heavily early in the year
- **Dollar-cost averaging**: Consistent contributions throughout the year
- **Back-loading**: Increasing contributions in later months

#### Front-loading Benefits:
- Earlier market exposure
- Guaranteed maximum contribution
- Protection against unexpected job changes

#### Dollar-cost Averaging Benefits:
- Reduced market timing risk
- Consistent budgeting
- Steady employer matching

## Advanced 401k Strategies for 2026

### 1. Roth vs. Traditional 401k Planning

Consider your tax situation:

**Traditional 401k Benefits:**
- Immediate tax deduction
- Higher take-home pay
- Better for high-income earners

**Roth 401k Benefits:**
- Tax-free withdrawals in retirement
- No required minimum distributions
- Better for expected higher future tax rates

### 2. Mega Backdoor Roth Strategy

If your plan allows after-tax contributions:

1. Maximize standard 401k contribution
2. Contribute after-tax dollars up to total limit
3. Convert after-tax portion to Roth
4. Potential additional $40,000+ in Roth contributions
