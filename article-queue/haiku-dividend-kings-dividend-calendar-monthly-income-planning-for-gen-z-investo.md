### Rebalancing Strategy

Annual rebalancing maintains your target allocation:

1. **Identify drift:** Calculate current percentages
2. **Set thresholds:** Rebalance when ±5% from targets
3. **Execute trades:** Sell overweight, buy underweight
4. **Harvest losses:** During rebalancing, harvest any losses for tax benefits

**Rebalancing Example:**
- Target VOO: 20%, Current: 23% (drift +3%)
- Target BND: 15%, Current: 12% (drift -3%)
- Action: Sell $15,000 VOO, buy $15,000 BND
- Tax impact: Only if VOO has unrealized gains

### The 4% Rule for Withdrawals

The 4% rule suggests withdrawing 4% of your portfolio in year one, adjusting annually for inflation:

**Example:**
- Portfolio: $500,000
- Year 1 withdrawal: $500,000 × 4% = $20,000
- Year 2 (3% inflation): $20,000 × 1.03 = $20,600
- Year 3 (3% inflation): $20,600 × 1.03 = $21,218

This approach has a 90%+ success rate for 30-year retirements.

### Emergency Fund Allocation

Even with a dividend portfolio, maintain cash reserves:

**Recommended Reserve:**
- 12-24 months of expenses in cash/short-term bonds
- Prevents forced sales during market downturns
- Provides flexibility for unexpected costs

**Example:**
- Annual expenses: $60,000
- Reserve needed: $60,000 × 1.5 = $90,000
- Keep in high-yield savings or short-term treasuries

## FAQ

**Q: What's the minimum portfolio size for sustainable dividends?**
A: $300,000-$500,000 for $1,000-$1,500/month sustainably, depending on yield and allocation.

**Q: Should I buy individual dividend stocks or dividend ETFs?**
A: ETFs provide diversification. SCHD holds 50+ Dividend Kings. Start with SCHD, add individual stocks for tax efficiency later.

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**
A: Annually, typically in December. Use the opportunity to harvest losses for tax efficiency.

**Q: Are dividend stocks safer than growth stocks?**
A: They're different risk types. Dividend stocks have lower volatility but slower capital growth. Growth stocks have higher volatility but capital appreciation potential.

**Q: Can I live on dividends if I have $1 million?**
A: Yes. At 2.5% yield, $1M generates $25,000/year or $2,083/month.

**Q: What about inflation impact on my portfolio?**
A: Dividend growth stocks historically grow dividends at inflation rates. A portfolio with 5% dividend growth outpaces 3% inflation.

**Q: Should I use margin to amplify dividend income?**
A: No. Margin adds risk and interest costs. Stick to unleveraged ETFs for stable long-term income.
