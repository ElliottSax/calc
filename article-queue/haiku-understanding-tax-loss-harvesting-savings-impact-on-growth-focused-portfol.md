## Tax-Loss Harvesting: Your Secret Wealth Multiplier

Most growth-focused investors focus exclusively on market returns while ignoring the 15-37% tax drag eroding wealth. Tax-loss harvesting transforms market declines from pure losses into tax reduction opportunities, potentially adding 0.5-1.5% annual returns through tax efficiency.

## What is Tax-Loss Harvesting?

Tax-loss harvesting involves deliberately selling securities trading below cost basis to realize losses, offsetting capital gains and up to $3,000 of ordinary income annually. Harvested losses carry forward indefinitely.

## The Tax-Loss Harvesting Formula

$$	ext{{Tax Savings}} = 	ext{{Realized Loss}} 	imes 	ext{{Effective Tax Rate}}$$

A $10,000 loss for a 37% federal + 3% state (40% total) taxpayer saves:
$$	ext{{Tax Savings}} = \$10,000 	imes 0.40 = \$4,000$$

## Why Tax-Loss Harvesting Works

Growth portfolios experience frequent volatility, creating recurring opportunities:

### Annual Harvesting Cycles
- Declines of 10%+: Multiple positions available
- Market corrections (20%+ decline): Abundant opportunities
- Sideways markets: Selective opportunities

### Growth Portfolio Characteristics
- High turnover potential
- Low dividend income (minimal offset)
- Significant unrealized gains
- Frequent loss opportunities

## FAQ: Tax-Loss Harvesting

**Q1: When should I start?**
A: Once portfolio exceeds $50,000 with embedded gains. Smaller portfolios rarely justify effort.

**Q2: Works in 401(k) or IRA?**
A: No. Tax-loss harvesting only applies to taxable accounts.

**Q3: What happens to carry-forward losses at death?**
A: Losses die with you. Heirs receive stepped-up basis, eliminating both gains and losses.

**Q4: How many positions needed?**
A: 6-10 enables optimal flexibility while remaining manageable.

**Q5: Should I use automated services?**
A: For portfolios under $250,000, manual harvesting is free. Larger portfolios may justify 0.20% fee for automation.
