## FAQ

**Q: How much should I start with?**
A: Start with whatever you can invest long-term (5+ years). Minimum $10,000 works if you scale position sizes down. Maximum is unlimited.

**Q: What if a company cuts its dividend?**
A: Analyze the reason. Cyclical cuts during recessions often recover. Structural changes may require replacement. Always maintain a watch-list of alternatives.

**Q: Should I use individual stocks or ETFs?**
A: ETFs provide instant diversification (50-500+ holdings). Individual stocks offer tax-loss harvesting. A 70/30 ETF-to-stock split balances both.

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**
A: Quarterly works well. Annual reduces costs; monthly increases them. Only rebalance when positions drift 5%+ from targets.

**Q: Is this suitable for IRAs/401(k)s?**
A: Yes. Tax-free growth makes dividend strategies more efficient in retirement accounts. Higher yields work well here.

**Q: What happens in a crash?**
A: Use dollar-cost averaging to buy at lower prices. A 30% decline means you invest at 30% discounts, improving long-term returns.

**Q: Can I use margin/leverage?**
A: Margin amplifies losses equally. Most dividend investors avoid it. The forced-liquidation risk during downturns exceeds benefits.
