# How to Calculate Tax-Loss Harvesting Savings for Municipal Bonds in 2026

Tax-loss harvesting for municipal bond investors offers unique advantages: tax-free income preserved, capital losses realized and deducted at full value. This guide teaches you to calculate tax-loss harvesting savings specific to tax-exempt investing.

## The Municipal Bond Tax Harvesting Advantage

Municipal bonds create perfect tax-loss harvesting opportunities:

1. **Tax-free income preserved**: Harvesting loses nothing; gains stay tax-free
2. **Capital losses fully valued**: Unlike stocks, no preferential tax rate dilutes loss value
3. **High tax brackets benefit most**: 32%+ federal + state brackets create substantial savings
4. **Abundant substitutes**: Multiple issuer/maturity options enable easy harvesting
5. **Quality selection**: Forced substitution often improves credit quality

Muni investors in 39.6% combined tax brackets save $3,960 per $10,000 loss realized.

## The Core Tax Savings Formula

**Tax Savings = Realized Loss × Your Marginal Tax Rate**

For municipal bond investors, marginal rate includes:
- Federal rate: 24% to 39.6%
- State rate: 3% to 12%
- Combined: 27% to 51.6%

**Example:**
- Muni position: $100,000 purchase
- Current value: $95,000
- Loss: $5,000
- Federal rate: 32%
- State rate: 8%
- Combined: 40%
- Tax savings: $5,000 × 0.40 = $2,000

The $2,000 represents direct tax liability reduction.

## Avoiding Wash Sales with Munis

Municipal bonds offer excellent substitution options:

**Substantially identical (wash sale applies):**
- Same issuer, same maturity, same coupon
- Example: California State General Obligation Bond A vs Bond A duplicate

**NOT substantially identical (harvest safely):**
- Different issuer: California vs Texas munis
- Different maturity: 5-year vs 7-year
- Different coupon: 3.8% vs 4.2% coupon bonds
- Different call features: Callable vs non-callable
- Different sectors: General obligation vs revenue bonds

**Harvesting plan:**
Sell California State 5-year at loss, immediately buy Texas State 5-year. Different issuers prevent wash sale complications.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I harvest muni losses and reinvest in Treasury bonds?**
A: Yes, though this shifts from tax-free to taxable income. Only harvest when loss tax benefit exceeds forgone tax-free income.

**Q: Should I harvest muni losses even if they hurt my portfolio yield?**
A: Yes, if harvesting into higher-yielding munis. If harvesting into lower-yielding positions, only harvest if tax savings exceed yield reduction.

**Q: What muni loss harvest amount makes sense?**
A: Harvest any position down $500+ or when total harvestable losses exceed $1,000. Smaller amounts create minimal tax benefit relative to trading friction.

**Q: Can I harvest muni losses, then buy the same muni back after 31 days?**
A: Yes, after waiting 31 days from sale date. The wash sale rule applies during the 30-day window but doesn't prevent repurchase on day 31+.

**Q: How do I handle muni interest that's been accrued and not yet paid?**
A: Accrued interest isn't part of the loss calculation. Cost basis includes purchase price only; accrued interest at sale adjusts your net proceeds but doesn't change loss recognition.
