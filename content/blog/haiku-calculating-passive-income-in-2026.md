# Calculating Passive Income in 2026

Passive income from dividend-paying investments provides financial flexibility and compounds into substantial wealth. Calculating income accurately requires understanding dividend mechanics, tax treatment, and reinvestment strategies. In 2026, strategic dividend positioning creates reliable income streams even during market volatility.

## Defining Passive Dividend Income

Passive dividend income includes quarterly dividend payments from stocks and ETFs, plus distributions from dividend-focused mutual funds. This distinguishes from earned income (wages), capital gains (selling for profit), and active business income. The passive classification affects tax treatment and reporting requirements.

Dividend income in 2026 divides into two categories:
- **Qualified dividends**: Taxed at preferential rates (0%, 15%, or 20% depending on income)
- **Non-qualified dividends**: Taxed as ordinary income (10% to 37%)

Most US stock dividends qualify for preferential treatment if held 60+ days around the ex-dividend date. This makes dividend investing tax-efficient relative to interest income.
