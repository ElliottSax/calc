# Dollar-Cost Averaging vs Income Investing: Which Builds More Wealth?

Income investing and dollar-cost averaging represent two distinct wealth-building philosophies. Income investors prioritize dividend yield and current cash flow. DCA practitioners focus on compounding growth. This analysis reveals which strategy builds more wealth over time.

## Understanding Income Investing

Income investing emphasizes securities generating substantial current yield through dividends. Income investors select stocks like KO, JNJ, and SCHD, prioritizing 3-5% annual yields.

**Income Investing Characteristics:**
- Dividend yield focus (3-5%+ annually)
- Monthly/quarterly income distributions
- Lower volatility (defensive stocks)
- Tax inefficient (frequent distributions)

## Understanding Dollar-Cost Averaging

DCA involves systematic investment at fixed intervals, emphasizing capital appreciation through compound growth.

**DCA Characteristics:**
- Capital appreciation focus
- All dividends reinvested
- Higher volatility acceptance
- Tax efficient (long-term gains)
