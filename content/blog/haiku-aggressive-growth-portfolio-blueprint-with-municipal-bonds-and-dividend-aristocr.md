## Building Your Portfolio Blueprint

A portfolio blueprint ensures systematic asset allocation and diversification aligned with your investment objectives.

## Core Allocation Principles

Successful portfolios follow key principles:

1. **Asset class diversification:** Reduce concentration risk
2. **Geographic diversification:** Access multiple economies
3. **Rebalancing discipline:** Maintain target allocations
4. **Cost minimization:** Use low-cost index funds and ETFs
5. **Tax efficiency:** Minimize unnecessary tax drag

## Rebalancing and Maintenance

### Quarterly Review

1. Calculate current allocations
2. Identify drift >5% from target
3. Rebalance using new contributions or tactical selling

**Tax-Efficient Rebalancing:**
- Direct new contributions to underweighted positions
- Use dividend income to rebalance
- Sell overweighted with smallest unrealized gains only

### Annual Actions

| Month | Action |
|-------|--------|
| January | Review allocation |
| April | Tax planning |
| July | Rebalance if drifted |
| October | Update contributions |
| December | Harvest losses |

## FAQ

**Q: Should I hold individual stocks or use ETFs?**
A: Core holdings should be diversified ETFs/index funds. Individual stocks can be 5-10% for conviction positions.

**Q: What's the right rebalancing frequency?**
A: Quarterly review, annual rebalancing. More frequent creates unnecessary costs.

**Q: How much should I allocate to international?**
A: 15-30% of equity allocation. Currently 25% is reasonable.

**Q: Should I include commodities?**
A: Only if hedging inflation. For most, 10% works; conservative investors might skip it.

**Q: What if my portfolio outperforms significantly?**
A: Rebalance aggressively to lock gains. Don't let positions dominate.
