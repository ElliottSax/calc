## Understanding Dividend Portfolio Foundations

A successful dividend portfolio begins with core principles: diversification, correlation analysis, and risk management. Modern portfolio theory demonstrates that combining uncorrelated assets reduces idiosyncratic risk while maintaining growth potential.

Harry Markowitz proved portfolio risk does not equal the sum of individual asset risks. Carefully selecting assets with low correlation coefficients achieves superior risk-adjusted returns.

**Key Principles:**
- Diversification reduces concentration risk across sectors and geographies
- Correlation reveals asset movement relationships
- Rebalancing maintains target allocations
- Tax efficiency minimizes returns drag
- Dollar-cost averaging reduces timing risk

## Mathematical Framework

### Expected Return

**Expected Return = (W1 × R1) + (W2 × R2) + ... + (Wn × Rn)**

Example: 60% VTI (9%) + 40% VOO (8%) = 8.6%

### Sharpe Ratio

**Sharpe Ratio = (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Std Dev**

Example: (8.6% - 4.5%) / 12% = 0.34

Above 1.0 indicates strong risk adjustment.

### Portfolio Volatility

**σp = √[(W1²σ1²) + (W2²σ2²) + 2(W1W2ρ12σ1σ2)]**

VTI (σ=15%, W=60%) + BND (σ=5%, W=40%), ρ=-0.15:
σp = 8.9%
