## Tax Efficiency in 2026

Tax efficiency significantly impacts your investment returns. Understanding how investments are taxed helps you keep more of what you earn.

## Understanding Your Tax Brackets

Your tax situation determines which investments work best for your situation:

- Short-term capital gains taxed as ordinary income (up to 37%)
- Long-term capital gains taxed at preferential rates (0%, 15%, 20%)
- Qualified dividends taxed at capital gains rates
- Tax-loss harvesting creates deductions to offset gains

### Key Tax Dates for 2026

| Event | Date | Importance |
|-------|------|-----------|
| Tax Year End | December 31 | Deadline for loss harvesting |
| Wash Sale Period | 30 days | Can't claim losses if repurchasing |

## Growth Vs. Income Strategies

### Growth Investing Approach

Growth investors benefit from lower turnover and capital gains treatment. A $100,000 investment returning 10% annually with 15% long-term capital gains tax yields 8.5% after-tax return.

**Key tax points:**
- Index funds minimize internal capital gains distributions
- ETFs offer better tax efficiency than mutual funds
- Hold positions 366+ days for preferential rates

### Income Investing Approach

Income investors face higher tax burdens from dividends. However, qualified dividends receive preferential treatment:

- Qualified dividends: 15% tax (or 0% for lower earners)
- Non-qualified dividends: ordinary income rates (up to 37%)
- Municipal bonds: federal tax-free income

**Strategy:** Place high-dividend investments in tax-advantaged accounts when possible.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting

Harvesting losses against gains reduces tax liability. The key is avoiding wash sales—selling at a loss then repurchasing within 30 days.

**Example:**
- Sold position at loss: $1,000 deduction
- Tax benefit at 24% bracket: $240 saved
- Must wait 31 days before repurchasing same security

### The 30-Day Wash Sale Window

| Days Relative to Sale | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| -30 to sale | Extends wash period if purchasing |
| Sale day | Loss potentially deductible |
| +1 to +30 | Repurchase violates wash sale |
| +31+ | Can repurchase without violation |

## Rebalancing Tax Efficiently

Minimize taxes when rebalancing your portfolio:

1. Direct new contributions to underweighted positions
2. Use dividend income to rebalance rather than selling
3. Sell overweighted positions with smallest unrealized gains
4. Consider hold-until-stepped-up-basis for heavily appreciated positions

**Example:** $100,000 portfolio
- Target: 60% stocks, 40% bonds
- Current: 70% stocks, 30% bonds
- Action: Put new $10,000 contribution entirely into bonds

## FAQ

**Q: Can I harvest losses in December and repurchase in January?**
A: Yes. Wait until January 1 due to 30-day lookback provision. Repurchase on January 1 or later.

**Q: How long must I hold investments for preferential rates?**
A: 366 days (more than one year). Short-term gains are taxed as ordinary income.

**Q: Should I harvest losses every year?**
A: Yes, if your portfolio has unrealized losses. Pair with gains when possible to maximize benefit.

**Q: What's the difference between qualified and non-qualified dividends?**
A: Qualified dividends (15-20% tax) meet holding period requirements. Non-qualified taxed as ordinary income.

**Q: Does DRIP trigger wash sale violations?**
A: Yes. Automatic reinvestment within 30 days of harvesting the same stock violates wash sale rules.

## Conclusion

Tax efficiency doesn't require complex strategies—awareness and planning matter most. Implement straightforward techniques like tax-loss harvesting, maintain low-turnover core holdings, and place high-tax investments in retirement accounts.
