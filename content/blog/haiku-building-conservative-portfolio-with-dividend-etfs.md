## Introduction

Conservative investors near or in retirement need stable income and capital preservation, not growth. Dividend-focused conservative portfolios using quality dividend ETFs (SCHD, VYM, DVY) combined with bonds generate 4-5% yields with 6-8% maximum drawdowns. This guide provides exact allocations for conservative dividend portfolios, withdrawal strategies, and 30-year sustainability analysis.

## Conservative Dividend Portfolio Principles

**Goals:**
- Generate $20,000-$40,000 annual income from $500,000-$1,000,000 portfolio
- Limit volatility to <8% annual drawdowns
- Preserve capital (no portfolio depletion)
- Compound slowly (portfolio grows despite withdrawals)

**Asset allocation rules:**
- 40-50% dividend stocks (SCHD, VYM)
- 40-50% bonds (BND, TLT)
- 5-10% cash/alternatives
- 0-10% growth stocks (optional)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is 4% withdrawal rate safe for conservative portfolios?**
A: Yes. With dividend yield already 4%, safe withdrawal rate matches yield. Very sustainable.

**Q: Should I avoid stocks entirely if very conservative?**
A: No. 25-35% dividend stocks still important for dividend growth. Helps inflation protection.

**Q: Can I live on dividends alone without touching principal?**
A: Yes, after 15-20 years. Dividend growth means distributions eventually exceed needs.

**Q: Are conservative portfolios suitable for 30-year retirements?**
A: Yes. Dividend growth compounds over time. After 20 years, dividends alone often cover needs.

**Q: What's the maximum safe yield?**
A: 5%+ on bonds is risky (usually means lower quality). 4-4.5% reasonable. Above that requires concentration in higher-risk assets.

**Q: Should I reinvest dividends in conservative portfolio?**
A: Yes, during accumulation phase. Once retired, take half as income, reinvest half for growth.
