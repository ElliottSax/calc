## Why Dividend Investing Works

### Income Generation
Dividend stocks provide reliable cash flow for current income needs and portfolio stability during market volatility.

### Compound Growth
Reinvested dividends create exponential wealth growth over decades through compounding effects.

### Inflation Hedge
Dividends that grow annually provide inflation protection as purchasing power increases.

## Building Your Dividend Portfolio

### Strategy 1: Dividend Growth
Focus on companies increasing dividends 5-10% annually with modest current yields (2-3%).

### Strategy 2: Current Income
Target high-yield stocks (5-8%) for immediate income generation.

### Strategy 3: Balanced Approach
Combine dividend growers with high-yield stocks for total return optimization.

## Tax-Efficient Strategies

### Account Placement
- Qualified dividend stocks in taxable accounts
- REITs and MLPs in tax-advantaged accounts
- High-yield stocks in Roth IRAs

### Tax Loss Harvesting
Sell underwater positions to realize losses that offset dividend income taxes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much capital do I need?
For $30,000 annual income: $1M at 3% yield, $750K at 4%, or $600K at 5%.

### Should I reinvest dividends?
Yes, for 10+ year time horizons. DRIP accelerates compounding dramatically.

### What payout ratio is safe?
40-65% of earnings indicates sustainable growth capacity.

### How often should I monitor?
Quarterly reviews after dividend announcements and annual portfolio deep-dives.

### Individual stocks or ETFs?
Blend both - ETFs for diversification, individual stocks for customization.
