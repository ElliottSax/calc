# 2026 Guide: Growth Investing for Generational Wealth

Generational wealth through growth investing maximizes compounding over 50-100+ years. With such extreme time horizons, bonds are irrelevant. Pure growth allocations compound to extraordinary wealth.

## 100% Growth Allocation for Dynasty Trusts

Unlike retirement portfolios needing income, dynasty trusts can be 100% equities.

### Formula: Pure Growth Over 100 Years

```
Future Value = Initial Investment × (1 + Growth Rate)^Years
With 100% stocks, Growth Rate ≈ 8% annually
```

**Worked Example:**

Dynasty trust funded with $1,000,000 in VTI/VXUS (100% stocks)

**50-year projection (Gen 1 lifetime):**
$1,000,000 × (1.08)^50 = $46,900,000

**100-year projection (Gen 1 + Gen 2):**
$1,000,000 × (1.08)^100 = $2,199,761,000

**Your $1M becomes $2.2 BILLION over 100 years with pure growth allocation.**

Even with $500,000 annual distributions to beneficiaries:
- First 50 years: Compound with minimal distributions
- Last 50 years: Distributions funded by previous growth
- Result: Still $500M+ remaining after 100 years

## Growth Investing Discipline Across Generations

Dynasty trusts require extreme discipline: never sell, never adjust allocation, never panic.

### Multi-Generational Discipline Rules

**Rule 1: Set Allocation Once, Never Change**
- Allocate 40/35/15/10 (VTI/VXUS/growth/small-cap)
- Lock in for 100 years
- No rebalancing, no adjustments
- Buy-and-hold forever

**Rule 2: Never Sell Except for Distributions**
- Sell only when distributions needed (once annually)
- Sell worst-performing holding (rebalance through distributions)
- Minimize taxes through stepped-up basis at each generation

**Rule 3: Reinvest All Dividends**
- Compounding accelerates with reinvestment
- $1M × 1.4% dividend = $14,000 dividend reinvested
- Over 100 years, dividend reinvestment adds billions

**Rule 4: Add Capital Only When New Wealth Created**
- If trustee earns income, reinvests in trust
- Otherwise, trust runs on its own
- Generational wealth is self-perpetuating

**Rule 5: Document Allocation in Trust Document**
- Write allocation into irrevocable trust
- Prevents emotional changes by future trustees
- Ensures discipline across generations
