# 2026 Guide: Growth Investing for Wealth Preservation

Wealth preservation often incorrectly emphasizes bonds. For 30+ year retirements, moderate growth allocation (50-60% stocks) provides inflation protection while maintaining stability.

## The Inflation Challenge in Preservation

Fixed-income preservation fails against inflation.

### Formula: Purchasing Power Decline

```
Purchasing Power = Initial Amount / (1 + Inflation Rate)^Years
30-year retirement, 3% inflation
Purchasing Power = 1 / (1.03)^30 = 0.41 (41% remaining)
```

**Worked Example:**

$80,000 annual withdrawal (today's purchasing power)
30-year retirement, 3% inflation

**Year 1:** $80,000 (100% purchasing power)
**Year 10:** $80,000 nominal = $59,700 in today's dollars (74% purchasing power)
**Year 20:** $80,000 nominal = $44,600 in today's dollars (56% purchasing power)
**Year 30:** $80,000 nominal = $33,300 in today's dollars (42% purchasing power)

**With inflation adjustment (withdrawing more each year):**

**Year 1:** $80,000 (nominal)
**Year 10:** $107,500 (adjusted for 3% inflation)
**Year 20:** $143,850 (adjusted)
**Year 30:** $193,200 (adjusted)

This requires portfolio growth to fund increasing withdrawals.

## Glide Path: Preservation Growth to Conservative

As you age (especially 75+), gradually reduce equity allocation.

### Age-Based Glide Path

**Ages 60-70:**
- 55% stocks / 45% bonds
- Target 6% return
- Accept 10% volatility

**Ages 70-80:**
- 45% stocks / 55% bonds
- Target 5.5% return
- Accept 8% volatility

**Ages 80+:**
- 35% stocks / 65% bonds
- Target 5% return
- Accept 6% volatility

This provides inflation protection when young, stability when old.

## Tax-Efficient Growth Preservation

Growth portfolios in preservation phase benefit from tax-efficient positioning.

### Tax Efficiency Strategy

**Tax-deferred accounts (401k, IRA):**
- Place bonds here (ordinary income taxation)
- Example: $250K BND in Traditional IRA

**Taxable accounts:**
- Place growth stocks here (qualified dividend/LTCG rates)
- Example: $550K VTI/VXUS/SCHD in taxable account
- Dividends taxed at 15-20% (vs. 24%+ on bonds in taxable)

**Tax benefit:** Saves $50,000-$100,000+ over 30-year retirement through tax location optimization.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Isn't 50% stocks too risky for retirement?**
A: Not for 30+ year retirements. Any crash recovers within 5-7 years. Time horizon allows recovery.

**Q: Should I reduce stocks as I age?**
A: Yes. Implement glide path: 55% at 60, 45% at 70, 35% at 80. Reduces volatility while maintaining growth.

**Q: What if market crashes in year 2 of retirement?**
A: Portfolio drops 20-30%, but recovers within 5-7 years. Bond allocation (45%) provides stability during crashes.

**Q: Should I increase withdrawals if portfolio grows?**
A: Yes, but cautiously. If portfolio doubles, increasing withdrawals 20-30% is sustainable. Avoid lifestyle inflation spikes.

**Q: Is dividend growth critical for preservation?**
A: Helpful but not essential. 6% total return (capital appreciation + dividends) sustains preservation whether from growth or income.
