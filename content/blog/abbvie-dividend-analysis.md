# AbbVie Dividend Analysis: Pharma Income Stock Deep Dive

AbbVie Inc. (ABBV) stands as one of the pharmaceutical industry's premier dividend stocks, offering a compelling 3.5% yield backed by blockbuster drugs and an aggressive R&D pipeline. Spun off from Abbott Laboratories in 2013, AbbVie has built an impressive dividend track record while navigating patent cliffs and building next-generation revenue streams.

## Key Takeaways

- AbbVie yields 3.4-3.6%, among the highest of quality dividend stocks
- The company has increased dividends for 11 consecutive years since the Abbott spinoff
- Payout ratio of 45-50% balances generous yield with pharmaceutical R&D needs
- Post-Humira patent cliff, new drugs (Skyrizi, Rinvoq) drive revenue growth
- Free cash flow of $20-22 billion supports dividends and strategic acquisitions

## Dividend History Since Spinoff (2013-2026)

**2013**: $1.60 annual (initial post-spinoff)
**2015**: $2.02 annual (+26%)

**2021**: $5.20 annual (+21%)

- Payout Ratio: 48%

## The Humira Challenge and Solution

**Humira Legacy** (1999-2023):
- Peak Revenue: $21 billion (2022)
- U.S. Patent Expiry: 2023
- Revenue Decline: 35-40% (2023-2025)

**New Growth Drivers**:
1. **Skyrizi** (IL-23 inhibitor)
   - Revenue: $9.8B (2025)
   - Growth: 40%+ annually
   - Peak potential: $15B+

2. **Rinvoq** (JAK inhibitor)
   - Revenue: $4.2B (2025)
   - Growth: 50%+ annually
   - Peak potential: $10B+

3. **Allergan Aesthetics**
   - Revenue: $5.8B (2025)
   - Botox leadership
   - Recurring revenue model

**Net Effect**: Post-Humira revenue expected to stabilize 2026-2027, then grow 4-6% annually.
