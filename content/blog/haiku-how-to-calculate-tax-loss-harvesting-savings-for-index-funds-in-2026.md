# How to Calculate Tax-Loss Harvesting Savings for Index Funds in 2026

Tax-loss harvesting for index fund investors offers unique advantages: multiple similar funds with minimal performance divergence, easy execution, and substantial tax savings. This comprehensive guide teaches you to calculate tax-loss harvesting savings specific to index fund investing.

## Index Fund Tax Harvesting Advantages

Index funds present optimal tax-harvesting vehicles:

1. **Multiple substitutes**: VTI vs VOO, SCHD vs SPHD, SPY vs IVV for S&P 500 tracking
2. **Predictable tracking**: Similar funds track identical indices with negligible performance variance
3. **Low costs**: Index fund expense ratios (0.03-0.15%) minimize trading friction
4. **Tax efficiency**: Index funds generate fewer capital gains distributions
5. **Scalability**: Harvesting works across $10,000 to $10,000,000+ portfolios

Unlike harvesting individual stocks (limited alternatives), index fund investors access abundant similar securities.

## The Core Tax Savings Formula

**Tax Savings = Realized Loss × Your Marginal Tax Rate**

For index funds, this calculation remains straightforward:

**Example:**
- VTI position: $75,000 purchase price
- Current value: $69,750
- Loss: $5,250
- Marginal tax rate: 28%
- Tax savings: $5,250 × 0.28 = $1,470

The $1,470 represents direct tax liability reduction.

## Wash Sale Considerations for Index Funds

The IRS considers the following as substantially identical:

Same index, same fund family: VTI (Vanguard) vs VOO (Vanguard) - both track S&P 500, same provider
Different fund providers, same index: VOO (Vanguard) vs SPY (iShares) - both track S&P 500, different providers

Substantially identical (wash sale applies):
- VTI vs VOO (both track total US market, different providers)

NOT substantially identical (harvest without concern):
- VTI (total market) vs QQQ (tech 100)
- VOO (large-cap) vs VB (small-cap)
- BND (broad bonds) vs TLT (long-term Treasuries)

**Tax harvesting strategy:**
Harvest VTI losses by immediately buying VGTSX (Vanguard Total Stock Market Admiral) or VOO (S&P 500). These funds track different indices (total market vs. large-cap only), preventing wash sale complications.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I harvest losses from VOO and immediately buy VTI?**
A: Yes. Both track different indices (S&P 500 vs total US market). IRS considers them substantially different despite both being large-cap focused.

**Q: Does harvesting index funds reduce diversification?**
A: No. By switching between similar funds (VTI to VOO, VXUS to IEMG), you maintain equivalent index exposure while realizing tax losses.

**Q: What's my maximum annual tax-loss harvesting amount?**
A: Unlimited harvesting, but losses offset gains first, then up to $3,000 of ordinary income. Excess carries forward indefinitely.

**Q: Should I harvest all losses or be selective?**
A: Selective harvesting matters. Harvest losses exceeding $1,000 or when losses total 10%+ of position. Small $100 losses create minimal tax benefit relative to trading friction.

**Q: Can I harvest, then immediately buy back the same fund?**
A: No. 30-day wash sale rule applies. Wait 31 days or switch to substantially different fund immediately.
