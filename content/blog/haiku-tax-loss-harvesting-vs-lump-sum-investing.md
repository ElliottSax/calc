## Understanding Both Approaches

Lump sum investing means deploying all available capital immediately into the market. Tax-loss harvesting is a tax optimization strategy applied to existing positions. These are not mutually exclusive—you can deploy a lump sum and then implement harvesting going forward.

The comparison addresses a critical question: if you have a large amount available, should you invest it all at once (lump sum) or gradually (DCA-style) to capture more harvesting opportunities?

## Lump Sum Investing

Lump sum investing involves:
- Investing entire amount immediately
- Capturing full market exposure immediately
- Avoiding timing risk of delayed entry
- Accepting whatever valuations exist at deployment time

**Key Characteristics**:
- Time to full deployment: Immediate
- Emotional difficulty: High (must accept immediate volatility)
- Historical performance: Beats DCA ~65% of the time
- Tax complexity: Lower (fewer transactions)

**Example**: You receive $100,000 bonus. Invest immediately in VOO.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting on Lump Sum

Tax-loss harvesting applied to lump sum portfolio:
- Deploy capital immediately (get full market exposure)
- Wait 1-3 months for positions to move
- Harvest any losses that emerge (some always do)
- Reinvest systematically

**Example**: Deploy $100,000 into VOO today, after 2 weeks it's worth $97,000 (market decline). Harvest $3,000 loss, switch to VTI, then wait 31 days to possibly rebalance.

## Dollar-Cost Averaging Alternative

Instead of lump sum, you could deploy gradually with DCA:

**Alternative**: \$200,000 deployed $10,000 monthly over 20 months

Benefits:
- Lower timing risk (average in gradually)
- Natural harvesting opportunities (buy more when down)
- Tax complexity: Higher (20 separate purchases)
- Final wealth: Similar to lump sum (within 1-2%)

For most investors with stable income, lump sum + harvesting outperforms pure DCA after tax optimization.

## Practical Implementation: Lump Sum + Harvesting

**Step-by-Step Plan**:

1. **Deploy Immediately**: Invest entire lump sum day 1
   - Get full market exposure
   - Accept whatever valuations exist

2. **Wait 1 Week**: Let market settle
   - Small daily fluctuations are normal
   - Don't react to noise

3. **Review Monthly** (or quarterly):
   - Identify any positions down 3-5%+
   - These are harvesting candidates

4. **Execute Harvesting**:
   - Sell losing positions
   - Calculate tax savings: Loss × Your Tax Rate
   - Reinvest in similar-but-distinct security
   - Document for tax return

5. **Resume Holding**:
   - Wait 31+ days before rebalancing back
   - Let harvested security appreciate
   - Capture gains without wash-sale violation

6. **Repeat Quarterly**:
   - Each quarter, review for new harvesting
   - Market volatility creates opportunities
   - Average 1-3 harvesting events per year

## FAQ

**Q: Should I deploy lump sum immediately or use DCA?**
A: Lump sum typically outperforms 65% of the time historically. The emotional difficulty makes DCA preferable for many people. Harvesting makes lump sum + harvesting the optimal math.

**Q: Can I harvest immediately after deploying lump sum?**
A: Yes, if market declines create losses within days/weeks. If immediate deployment is at market peak, wait 2-4 weeks for some normal volatility.

**Q: What if I lump sum deploy and market rises 20%?**
A: Great—no losses to harvest. This happens in bull markets. Harvesting benefits vary by market conditions; some years have minimal opportunity.

**Q: Should I harvest my entire lump sum or just portions?**
A: Harvest portions opportunistically (positions down 3-5%+). Don't force harvesting if nothing is underwater—you'd be realizing gains unnecessarily.

**Q: Does lump sum + harvesting beat DCA?**
A: Mathematically, lump sum + harvesting typically outperforms DCA by 0.5-1.5% over 10+ years, though psychological factors (discipline) may favor DCA for some investors.
