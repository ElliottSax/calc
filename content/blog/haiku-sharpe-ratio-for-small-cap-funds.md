## Calculating Sharpe Ratio for Small-Cap Funds

The Sharpe ratio quantifies excess returns relative to risk taken. For small-cap funds, this metric reveals whether superior growth potential appropriately compensates you for elevated volatility and concentration risk undertaken.

## The Sharpe Ratio Formula

**Sharpe Ratio = (Average Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation of Returns**

## Understanding the Components

**Average Return:** Historical annualized small-cap fund return
**Risk-Free Rate:** Yield of 10-year US Treasury (currently ~4.5%)
**Standard Deviation:** Measure of small-cap price volatility

## Worked Example: Sharpe Ratio Calculation

Calculate Sharpe ratio for a small-cap fund:

**Data Collection (10-year period):**
- Annual returns: 12%, 8%, 22%, 5%, 18%, 15%, 11%, 19%, 9%, 16%
- Average: (12+8+22+5+18+15+11+19+9+16) / 10 = 13.5%
- Calculate standard deviation: approximately 5.8%

**Sharpe Calculation:**
Sharpe Ratio = (13.5% - 4.5%) / 5.8%
Sharpe Ratio = 9.0% / 5.8%
**Sharpe Ratio = 1.552**

## Interpreting Sharpe Ratios for Small-Cap Funds

| Sharpe Value | Assessment | Implication |
|---|---|---|
| < 0.3 | Below-average risk-adjusted returns | Higher risk not justified |
| 0.3-0.5 | Average risk-adjusted returns | Moderate risk-reward tradeoff |
| 0.5-0.8 | Good risk-adjusted returns | Reasonable value proposition |
| > 0.8 | Excellent risk-adjusted returns | Compelling opportunity |

Small-cap funds typically deliver 0.35-0.55 Sharpe ratios.

## Why Small-Cap Funds Have Higher Volatility

| Risk Factor | Impact | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Lower Liquidity | High | Wider bid-ask spreads increase volatility |
| Higher Beta | High | More sensitive to market movements |
| Earnings Volatility | Medium | Less diversified, more uncertain |
| Concentration Risk | Medium | Fewer holdings magnify individual stock moves |

## Volatility Calculation Process

1. Collect 10+ years of monthly small-cap returns
2. Calculate average return
3. Calculate deviation of each return from average
4. Square each deviation
5. Sum squared deviations
6. Divide by number of periods (variance)
7. Take square root (standard deviation)

## Market Cycle Effects on Small-Cap Sharpe Ratios

Economic cycles dramatically affect small-cap performance:

**Expansion Phase:**
- Small-cap Sharpe ratios: 0.60-0.80
- Outperformance continues quarter after quarter
- Valuations expand with growth prospects

**Late Expansion:**
- Small-cap Sharpe ratios: 0.40-0.50
- Valuation expansion ends, earnings growth drives returns
- Growth decelerates

**Contraction:**
- Small-cap Sharpe ratios: 0.10-0.30
- Earnings disappoint, valuations compress
- Worse downside than large-cap

## Comparing Small-Cap Funds Using Sharpe Ratio

When evaluating small-cap allocations:
1. Calculate 5-year and 10-year Sharpe ratios
2. Assess consistency across periods (high variability signals poor strategy)
3. Evaluate against Russell 2000 benchmark
4. Consider fund expense ratios (critical for small-cap)
5. Assess quality (profitable vs. speculative)

## Portfolio Diversification with Small-Caps

Including small-caps in diversified portfolios:
- Adds growth exposure with manageable correlation
- Improves long-term returns if held through cycles
- Increases overall portfolio volatility
- Requires patience and discipline during downturns

## FAQ

**Q: What's a good Sharpe ratio for small-cap funds?**
A: For small-caps, 0.35-0.55 is typical; above 0.55 indicates relative strength.

**Q: Should I allocate heavily to small-caps?**
A: Limited allocation (5-15% of equity) for diversification. Higher exposure increases portfolio volatility substantially.

**Q: Why are small-cap Sharpe ratios lower than large-cap?**
A: Higher volatility typically outweighs additional returns, reducing risk-adjusted value.

**Q: Can small-cap Sharpe ratios predict future performance?**
A: No. Sharpe ratios vary dramatically across cycles, limiting predictive value.

**Q: How do market caps affect small-cap fund volatility?**
A: Smaller cap companies have more volatile earnings and higher business failure risk.

## Conclusion

The Sharpe ratio provides framework for evaluating small-cap fund allocations on risk-adjusted basis. By calculating and comparing Sharpe ratios, understanding cycle variations, and limiting allocation sizes, you integrate small-cap exposure strategically without excessive risk.
