## Tactical Sector Weighting

Adjust based on economic outlook:

**Economic Growth Expected:**
- Overweight: Technology, Industrials, Consumer Discretionary
- Underweight: Utilities, Staples, Healthcare
- Rationale: Cyclicals outperform

**Recession Fears:**
- Overweight: Healthcare, Consumer Staples, Utilities
- Underweight: Industrials, Consumer Discretionary
- Rationale: Defensive sectors hold up

**Inflation Rising:**
- Overweight: Energy, Materials, Financials
- Underweight: Technology, Consumer Staples
- Rationale: These sectors benefit from inflation

## Sector Rotation Case Study

**Starting sector allocation (Jan 2023):**
Market weight (28% tech, 13% healthcare, etc.)

**Q1 2024: Tech momentum strong (+12%), Healthcare weak (-3%)**
- Adjust: Tech 28% → 32%, Healthcare 13% → 10%
- Rebalance: Sell healthcare, buy tech at higher prices

**Q2 2024: Tech crashes (-8%), Healthcare recovers (+4%)**
- Rotate back: Tech 32% → 28%, Healthcare 10% → 13%
- Rebalance: Sell tech losers, buy healthcare winners

**Result:** Sector rotation captured 0.5-1.0% additional return by moving between sectors

**This works IF:**
- You can predict sector rotations (difficult)
- You rebalance disciplined (easy to get wrong)
- You don't chase trends (hard to avoid)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is sector allocation better than broad index funds?**
A: Similar expected returns (7-8%) but with more flexibility. Best if you want tactical positioning.

**Q: How often should I rebalance sectors?**
A: Quarterly. More frequent = higher costs. Less frequent = bigger drifts.

**Q: Which sectors should I always hold?**
A: Healthcare and Utilities for stability. Technology for growth. Financials for income.

**Q: Can I use sector rotation to beat the market?**
A: Theoretically yes, practically difficult. Most outperformance comes from fees saved vs. active managers, not superior sector picks.

**Q: Should I use sector ETFs or individual stocks?**
A: ETFs for balanced portfolios (diversification). Individual stocks only if deep knowledge.

## Conclusion

Sector-based balanced portfolios offer similar 7.5% expected returns as broad index fund portfolios but with greater transparency and tactical flexibility. A $250,000 sector-allocated balanced portfolio generates slightly higher returns (0.3-0.5% annually) if rebalanced quarterly. Sector approach best for investors wanting to understand portfolio composition and willing to actively manage. For hands-off investing, broad VOO/BND still provides excellent outcomes with minimal complexity.
