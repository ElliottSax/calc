# 2026 Guide: Dollar-Cost Averaging for Wealth Preservation

DCA in preservation phase means systematic, discipline-based withdrawals that maintain market exposure and force buying low during downturns.

## Reverse DCA: Systematic Withdrawals

Preservation DCA is inverse accumulation: instead of adding money systematically, withdraw systematically.

### Formula: Systematic Withdrawal DCA

```
Monthly Withdrawal = Annual Expenses ÷ 12
Adjusted annually for inflation (3%)
Maintains market exposure throughout
```

**Worked Example:**

$2,000,000 portfolio, $80,000 annual expenses

Monthly withdrawal: $80,000 ÷ 12 = $6,667

**Year 1:**
- Withdraw: $6,667/month ($80,000 annual)
- Portfolio return: 6% annually = $120,000
- Net portfolio change: +$40,000 despite withdrawals

**Year 5:**
- Withdraw: $6,667 × 1.03^4 = $7,500/month ($90,000 annual)
- Portfolio grows to: $2,249,000
- Portfolio return: $135,000
- Net portfolio change: +$45,000 despite higher withdrawals

**Year 20:**
- Withdraw: $6,667 × 1.03^19 = $11,260/month ($135,100 annual)
- Portfolio grows to: $3,456,000
- Portfolio return: $207,360
- Net portfolio change: +$72,260 despite much higher withdrawals

**Preservation secured: Portfolio grows despite withdrawals for 30+ years.**

## Systematic Withdrawal Discipline

Preservation DCA forces discipline through systematic withdrawals.

### Why Systematic Withdrawals Matter

**Without system (emotional withdrawals):**
- Bull market: Withdraw more (reward seeking)
- Bear market: Withdraw less (panic)
- Result: Sell low, buy high (opposite of optimal)

**With system (mechanical withdrawals):**
- Bull market: Fixed withdrawal amount
- Bear market: Fixed withdrawal amount
- Result: Forced to sell less in downturns, more in bull markets

This is reverse DCA: forced selling in bull markets (locking gains), forced holding in bear markets (allowing recovery).

### Real Example: 2008 Financial Crisis + Preservation DCA

Investor age 60, $2M portfolio, $80K withdrawal plan

**Pre-crash (2007):**
- Portfolio: $2,000,000
- Monthly withdrawal: $6,667
- Market performing well, portfolio stable

**Crash (2008):**
- Portfolio drops to: $1,400,000 (30% decline)
- Monthly withdrawal: $6,667 (unchanged, still $80K annual)
- Forced to continue selling
- Share count increases (buying low)

**Recovery (2010+):**
- Original shares from 2007 at low cost basis
- Additional shares purchased during 2008-2009 at even lower cost
- Recovery gains magnified

**By 2020:**
- Portfolio recovered to $3M+ (beyond original $2M)
- Withdrawals never exceeded $80K
- Maintained market exposure throughout

**Result:** Discipline through systematic withdrawals provided better outcomes than emotional market-timing would have.
