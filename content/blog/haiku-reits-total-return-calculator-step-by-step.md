# REITs Total Return Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

Total return measures all gains from REIT investing: price appreciation plus dividends. A REIT delivering 3% price gain plus 5% dividend yield equals 8% total return. Ignoring dividends dramatically understates REIT performance since distributions form the backbone of REIT investing.

## What Is Total Return for REITs?

Total return captures the complete investor experience, including both capital appreciation and income. Many investors focus only on stock price movement, completely missing that REITs distribute 90% of earnings as dividends. A seemingly flat REIT price hides substantial dividend income when calculated as total return.

## Why Total Return Is Essential for REITs

REITs differ fundamentally from growth stocks. A stock that appreciates 5% annually generates 5% returns, but a REIT appreciating 3% plus yielding 5% generates 8% total return. Comparing only price appreciation severely distorts REIT attractiveness.

## Total Return Formula for REITs

**Total Return % = ((Ending Price - Starting Price + Dividends) / Starting Price) × 100**

Example with Realty Income (O):
- Starting price: $45.00
- Ending price: $48.00
- Dividends received: $2.76
- Total Return = (($48.00 - $45.00 + $2.76) / $45.00) × 100 = 12.8%

## Calculating Total Return Step-by-Step

1. Note starting REIT price when purchased
2. Record all dividends received (reinvested or taken as cash)
3. Check ending price on calculation date
4. Add ending price to total dividends
5. Subtract starting price
6. Divide by starting price
7. Multiply by 100 for percentage

This captures the full investor experience regardless of how distributions were handled.

## Tax-Efficient Total Return Tracking

Track total return accounting for taxes:
1. Tax-advantaged accounts (no tax drag)
2. Taxable accounts (subtract taxes paid on dividends)
3. After-tax return = Pre-tax return × (1 - tax rate)

A 8% return in a taxable account becomes ~6.2% after taxes for a high earner (22% bracket).

## FAQ

**Q: Why does my brokerage show different total return than I calculated?**
A: They may use different start/end dates or exclude specific dividends. Use your own calculation for accuracy in your specific situation.

**Q: Does total return assume dividend reinvestment?**
A: No, unless specified. Calculate both scenarios for comparison to understand true compounding impact.

**Q: How often should I calculate total return?**
A: Quarterly is ideal for monitoring progress. Annual is standard for tax planning and performance assessment.

**Q: Should I include trading fees in total return?**
A: Yes. Subtract broker commissions from your total return for true net performance measurement.

**Q: Is 8% REIT total return good?**
A: Yes. That matches long-term stock returns with lower volatility. Good balanced performance.
