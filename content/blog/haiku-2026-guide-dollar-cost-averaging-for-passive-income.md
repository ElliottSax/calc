# 2026 Guide: Dollar-Cost Averaging for Passive Income

Dollar-cost averaging into dividend-paying stocks builds passive income that grows 5-7% annually, eventually exceeding living expenses.

## DCA to Passive Income Model

Instead of accumulating capital to reach FI number ($1.5M), DCA investors can reach passive income goal when dividends exceed expenses.

### Formula: Dividend Income Target

```
Dividend Income Target = Annual Expenses
Monthly investment goal: (Target ÷ 0.035) ÷ 12
Assuming 3.5% dividend yield on portfolio
```

**Worked Example:**

Annual expenses: $60,000
Monthly dividend DCA target: Sufficient capital to generate $60,000/year in dividends

Required capital: $60,000 ÷ 0.035 = $1,714,286

Monthly DCA needed: $1,714,286 ÷ (7% annual return × 20 years) ≈ $2,500/month

**After 20 years of $2,500 monthly DCA:**
- Portfolio value: ~$900,000
- Dividend income: ~$31,500 (3.5% yield)

**Not yet passive income target. Extend to 25 years:**
- Portfolio value: ~$1,400,000
- Dividend income: ~$49,000 (3.5% yield)

**Still short. Need 28-30 years:**
- Portfolio value: ~$1,800,000
- Dividend income: ~$63,000 (3.5% yield)

**Passive income target reached in 28-30 years.**

## Dividend Growth Impact on Passive Income

Dividend growth significantly accelerates passive income timeline.

### Formula: Yield on Cost

Yield on cost = Current annual dividend / Original investment

**Worked Example:**

$50,000 invested in SCHD, 3.5% yield (= $1,750 dividend Year 1)

**Without dividend growth assumption:**
- Year 20 yield on cost: 3.5% (unchanged)
- Annual dividend: $1,750

**With 10% dividend growth:**
- Year 20 yield on cost: 3.5% × (1.10)^19 = 22.9% (!!)
- Annual dividend: $11,450 (vs. original $1,750)

**This is why dividend growth matters:** Your original investment eventually yields 20%+ annually, not just 3.5%.

In passive income context: Early DCA investments yield higher and higher dividends over time.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long does passive income DCA take?**
A: 20-30 years depending on target income level and dividend yield. $60,000 target ≈ 28 years with $2,500/month DCA.

**Q: Can I increase DCA to accelerate passive income?**

**Q: What dividend yield targets are realistic?**

**Q: Should I DCA in tax-advantaged or taxable account?**
A: Tax-advantaged first (401k, Roth). Overflow to taxable, focusing on qualified dividends in taxable.

**Q: What's the best dividend stock for DCA?**
A: SCHD is ideal for simplicity (100+ dividend stocks, automatic rebalancing). Individual stocks (JNJ, MCD, KO) work but require management.
