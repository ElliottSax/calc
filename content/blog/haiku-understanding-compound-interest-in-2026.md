## FAQ

**Q: How do I calculate compounding with varying annual returns?**

Multiply each year's return sequentially: Year 1 (1 + r1), then multiply by Year 2 (1 + r2), etc. A portfolio returning 8%, 5%, 10%, 7%, 6% over five years: (1.08)(1.05)(1.10)(1.07)(1.06) = 1.498 or 49.8% total return.

**Q: Is 2026's 4.75% Treasury rate guaranteed?**

Yes, Treasury bonds carry U.S. government backing—the safest asset. However, if you sell before maturity and rates rise further, you face capital losses. Hold to maturity for guaranteed 4.75% compounding, or buy Treasury ETFs (BND, SHV) for liquidity with 4.5-4.8% compounding.

**Q: Should I prioritize savings accounts (4.5%) over stock market (8%)?**

Over 20 years, 8% stocks compound to $466,096 vs. 4.5% savings at $251,649. Stocks generate 85% more wealth but with volatility. For emergency funds and short-term needs (under 5 years), prefer savings accounts. For retirement and long-term growth (20+ years), prioritize stocks.

**Q: How does inflation of 2.4% affect my compounding calculations?**

Subtract inflation from nominal returns: 8% stocks minus 2.4% inflation = 5.6% real return. Calculate long-term purchasing power by dividing final amounts by (1.024)^n. A $466,096 portfolio in 20 years represents $297,860 in today's purchasing power.

**Q: What's the best compounding strategy in 2026's high-rate environment?**

Treasuries offer exceptional fixed returns (4.75% safe). Stocks offer growth potential (8% expected). A balanced approach splits between both: 40% Treasuries (safe compounding) and 60% stocks (growth compounding) produces 6.5% blended returns with moderate risk.
