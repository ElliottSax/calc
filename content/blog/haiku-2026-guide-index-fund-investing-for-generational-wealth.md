## Estate Planning for Index Fund Portfolios

### The "Step-Up in Basis" Advantage

**How it works:**
When you die, beneficiaries inherit portfolio at stepped-up cost basis (current market value), eliminating all capital gains taxes.

**Example:**
- You buy VTI at $100, accumulates to $500/share over 40 years
- Your original cost basis: $100
- Unrealized gain: $400/share
- You die; beneficiary inherits VTI at $500 stepped-up basis
- Beneficiary sells immediately: $500 - $500 = $0 capital gains tax
- Your heirs avoid entire tax bill

For a $10M portfolio with 5x gains, step-up saves approximately $1-2M in capital gains taxes.

### Dynasty Trust Strategy

**Setup:**
1. Create irrevocable trust with index fund portfolio
2. Transfer assets to trust (uses estate tax exemption)
3. Trust generates income for current generation
4. Principal preserved for future generations
5. Trust continues indefinitely (perpetual)

**Tax advantage:**
- Portfolio avoids estate taxes after first generation
- Income flows to beneficiaries (they pay income tax)
- Principal preserved entirely
- Compounding continues within trust untouched

**Example:**
- Transfer $5M to dynasty trust at your death (uses estate exemption)
- Trust grows at 7% annually
- In 30 years: Trust worth $37M
- Zero additional estate taxes throughout
- Heirs receive $37M if trust dissolves
