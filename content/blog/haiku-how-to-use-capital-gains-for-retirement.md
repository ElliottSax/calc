# How to Use Capital Gains for Retirement

Capital gains—profits from selling appreciated investments—provide retirement income complementing dividends and interest. Strategic realization of long-term capital gains at favorable tax rates creates tax-efficient retirement income. This guide explains how to systematically harvest gains while managing tax liability.

## Understanding Capital Gains Taxation

Capital gains face preferential tax rates, with long-term gains (held 12+ months) taxed significantly lower than ordinary income:

**2026 Tax rates for single filers:**

| Income Level | Long-Term Gains Rate | Short-Term Gains Rate | Ordinary Income Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| $0-$47,025 | 0% | Ordinary rates | 10-12% |
| $47,025-$518,900 | 15% | Ordinary rates | 24-35% |
| $518,900+ | 20% | Ordinary rates | 37% |

A retiree in the 24% ordinary income bracket pays 15% tax on long-term capital gains—a 9 percentage point advantage. Married filers enjoy larger brackets (0% rate applies to $0-$94,050 income).

## Strategic Gain Realization in Low-Income Years

Retirees can strategically realize capital gains during low-income years to minimize effective tax rates. Example:

**Age 62 retiree before Social Security claiming:**
- Portfolio distributions: $0
- Pension income: $0
- Taxable income: Minimal

Realize $30,000 long-term capital gains:
- Total income: $30,000
- Taxable gains: $0 (within 0% bracket)
- Tax owed: $0
- Effective rate on gains: 0%

By age 70 (after Social Security claiming):
- Social Security: $28,000
- Pension: $20,000
- Other income: ~$10,000
- Total income: $58,000 (exceeds 0% bracket)
- Additional gains taxed at 15%

This strategy harvests gains during low-income years, deferring growth until higher-income retirement years.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting for Gain Offset

Tax-loss harvesting sells losing positions to offset capital gains, reducing net tax liability:

**Example: $50,000 unrealized gain in VOO, $30,000 unrealized loss in VTI**

Harvest loss:
- Sell VTI at loss; realize $30,000 loss
- Simultaneously buy similar fund (VTSAX) avoiding wash-sale violation
- Net gain after harvesting: $50,000 - $30,000 = $20,000
- Tax on $20,000 gain (15% rate): $3,000 (versus $7,500 without harvesting)
- **Tax savings: $4,500**

Continuous tax-loss harvesting throughout retirement captures $500-$2,000+ annually, increasing net retirement income.

## Sequencing Gain Realization by Asset Class

Systematic gain realization prioritizes tax efficiency. Sequence:

1. **Realize gains in 0% bracket years** (maximize bracket utilization at lowest rate)
2. **Harvest losses simultaneously** (offset gains against losses)
3. **Sell low-basis assets first** (minimize gain per share sold)
4. **Hold appreciation-heavy assets longest** (defer highest-gain realization)

**Example: Four holdings with different appreciation:**

| Holding | Basis | Current Value | Gain |
|---------|-------|---|---|
| VOO (held 15 years) | $50,000 | $200,000 | $150,000 |
| SCHD (held 10 years) | $40,000 | $92,000 | $52,000 |
| JNJ (held 8 years) | $30,000 | $48,000 | $18,000 |
| VTI (held 5 years) | $80,000 | $100,000 | $20,000 |

Realization sequence in 0% bracket year (up to $47,025 available):
1. Realize $18,000 gain from JNJ (smallest gain; preserve largest appreciators)
2. Realize $20,000 gain from VTI
3. Realize $9,025 gain from SCHD (partial; leaves room for harvested losses)
4. Defer VOO ($150,000 gain; massive deferred appreciation)

This approach captures $47,000 gains at 0% tax rate while preserving largest appreciators for later realization.

## Roth Conversion Ladder for Gain Management

Converting traditional IRA balances to Roth accounts creates "recharacterization opportunity"—ability to separately realize gains in the converted amount:

**Example: $100K traditional IRA converted to Roth**

Traditional IRA contains:
- $60K cost basis (original contributions)
- $40K gains (investment returns)

Convert to Roth; realize $40K gain immediately:
- Tax on conversion (24% bracket): $9,600
- Benefit: Remaining $100K grows tax-free in Roth

This conversion "costs" $9,600 in taxes immediately but eliminates future tax liability on the $100K+ as it compounds in Roth account.

## Managing Inherited Asset Appreciation

Inherited securities receive "stepped-up basis"—new cost basis at date of death value. This eliminates inherited gains from taxation:

**Example: Parent dies with $100K VOO (cost basis $20K)**

- Original cost basis: $20K
- Value at death: $100K
- Inherited stepped-up basis: $100K
- Heir's cost basis: $100K

If heir later sells for $110K:
- Taxable gain: $10K (not $90K)
- Tax savings: $75K (15% rate on $100K avoided)

For retirees planning legacy wealth transfer, inherited asset stepping-up basis dramatically reduces estate capital gains taxes.

## Qualified Charitable Distributions for Gain Management

Retirees age 70.5+ can distribute traditional IRA funds directly to charities without incurring income tax. This effectively "harvests" the distribution at zero tax rate:

**Example: Age 75 with $500K traditional IRA**

- Withdraw $10,000 from IRA, donating directly to charity
- No income tax on withdrawal
- Deduct charitable donation
- Result: $10,000 directed to charity, $0 tax, $0 AGI increase

This strategy reduces RMD income and AGI, enabling larger capital gain realization at lower tax brackets.

## Harvesting Gains Before Required Minimum Distributions

RMDs (age 73+) force taxable withdrawals, potentially pushing into higher brackets. Proactive gain realization before RMD age prevents forced high-bracket harvesting:

**Age 65-72 (pre-RMD years):**
- Realize $50K gains annually in low brackets (15% rate)
- Harvest losses simultaneously
- Reduce RMD impact through earlier gain realization

**Age 73+:**
- RMDs force distributions but at lower effective rates (pre-harvested gains)
- Remaining portfolio has lower accumulated gains

This strategy spreads gain realization across lower-income pre-RMD years rather than forcing all gains into RMD years.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I harvest gains before or during retirement?**
A: During retirement at lowest income years. Ages 62-70 (pre-Social Security) offer optimal harvesting brackets.

**Q: How do I avoid wash-sale violations when harvesting?**
A: Sell Position A (loss); simultaneously buy similar Position B. Hold position A for 31 days before repurchasing.

**Q: Can I realize long-term gains and remain in the 0% bracket?**
A: Yes. Single filers can realize $47,025 gains at 0% (2026); married filers $94,050. Use this annually to harvest without tax.

**Q: What's the benefit of harvesting gains versus holding forever?**
A: Harvesting during low-income years saves tax; enables better portfolio management (sell underperformers at gains); reduces heirs' stepped-up basis risk.

**Q: Do I have to realize gains in account sequence?**
A: No. Specify which shares sell to optimize basis. Specifically identify FIFO, LIFO, or lowest-cost-basis shares for maximum flexibility.

**Q: Should I sell appreciated stocks or bonds first?**
A: Generally bonds first (lower tax impact; interest taxed as ordinary income anyway). Defer stock gains to optimize capital gains rate timing.
