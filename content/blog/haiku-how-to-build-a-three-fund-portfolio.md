# How to Build a Three-Fund Portfolio

The three-fund portfolio is elegance in simplicity. Three index funds—U.S. stocks, international stocks, and bonds—provide complete market diversification with minimal cost and complexity. You can build a diversified portfolio for less than $1,000 and monitor it in minutes annually.

## Why Three Funds?

Most investors accumulate 20-30 holdings, believing more diversification equals better returns. This is false. Research shows diminishing diversification benefits beyond five holdings.

**Diversification Efficiency:**

| Number of Holdings | Unique Risk Reduction | Marginal Benefit |
|-------------------|----------------------|------------------|
| 1 fund | 0% | - |
| 3 funds | 85% | - |
| 5 funds | 92% | 7% |
| 10 funds | 96% | 4% |
| 20 funds | 98% | 2% |
| 50 funds | 99% | 1% |

Three funds capture 85% of diversification benefits. Adding funds 4 and 5 gets you to 92%, but requires tracking additional holdings. The three-fund portfolio is the optimal efficiency frontier for simplicity.

## Building Your Three-Fund Portfolio: Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Choose Your Broker

Options include Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab, or interactive brokers. All offer the funds you need commission-free.

**Recommendation:** Use the broker offering the lowest-cost version of the funds you want.

**Example:** Vanguard if using VTSAX/VTIAX/VBTLX (ultra-low 0.04% ratio)

### Step 2: Calculate Your Target Allocation

**Example:** Age 40, moderate risk tolerance

Target allocation:
- 50% U.S. Stocks = 50% of portfolio in VTSAX
- 25% International Stocks = 25% of portfolio in VTIAX
- 25% Bonds = 25% of portfolio in VBTLX

If starting with $30,000:
- VTSAX: $15,000
- VTIAX: $7,500
- VBTLX: $7,500

### Step 3: Open Accounts

1. Open brokerage account (3 minutes online)
2. Verify identity and bank account
3. Transfer initial funds (1-3 business days)

### Step 4: Place Orders

**For mutual funds (VTSAX, VTIAX, VBTLX):**
- Search fund ticker
- Enter dollar amount to invest
- Preview purchase, confirm
- Funds settle in 1 business day

**For ETFs (VOO, VTIAX, AGG):**
- Search ticker symbol
- Enter number of shares
- Execute during market hours
- Settlement: next business day

### Step 5: Automate Contributions

Set up monthly automatic transfers:

**Example:** Contribute $1,000/month to three-fund portfolio

**Automatic distribution:**
- $500 to VTSAX (50%)
- $250 to VTIAX (25%)
- $250 to VBTLX (25%)

Let this run for decades. Dollar-cost averaging smooths market volatility and compounds wealth.

## Rebalancing: The Critical Discipline

### Why Rebalance?

Without rebalancing, successful market years drift your allocation away from targets. You gradually become overexposed to stocks.

**Example drift:**
- Starting allocation: 70% stocks, 30% bonds
- After 10 years of strong stock returns: 85% stocks, 15% bonds

This accidental overallocation increases risk beyond your comfort level.

### Annual Rebalancing Process

**Step 1: Calculate current allocation**
- VTSAX value / total portfolio = current stock %
- Repeat for VTIAX and VBTLX

**Step 2: Identify drift**
- Is any allocation more than 5% away from target?

**Step 3: Rebalance**
- Sell overweighted funds
- Buy underweighted funds

**Example:** $100,000 portfolio, 70/30 target

| Fund | Target | Current | Difference |
|------|--------|---------|-----------|
| VTSAX | $35,000 | $42,000 | +$7,000 |
| VTIAX | $35,000 | $30,000 | -$5,000 |
| VBTLX | $30,000 | $28,000 | -$2,000 |

Action: Sell $7,000 of VTSAX, buy $5,000 VTIAX and $2,000 VBTLX

**Tax note:** In tax-deferred accounts (401k, IRA), rebalance freely. In taxable accounts, use new contributions to buy underweighted funds rather than selling winners.

## Three-Fund Portfolio Mechanics: Mathematical Example

**Initial Investment:** $100,000
**Allocation:** 60% VTSAX, 30% VTIAX, 10% VBTLX

| Fund | Amount | Shares (Example Price) | Expense Ratio |
|------|--------|------------------------|----------------|
| VTSAX @ $150/share | $60,000 | 400 | 0.04% |
| VTIAX @ $120/share | $30,000 | 250 | 0.08% |
| VBTLX @ $100/share | $10,000 | 100 | 0.04% |

**Portfolio-weighted expense ratio:** (60% × 0.04%) + (30% × 0.08%) + (10% × 0.04%) = 0.052%

**Annual cost:** $100,000 × 0.052% = $52

Compare to actively managed portfolio at 0.85%: $100,000 × 0.85% = $850 annually—16x higher.

Over 30 years, the expense ratio difference represents $30,000+ in foregone wealth.

## Common Questions

### Should I include a small-cap fund?

No. VTSAX already includes small-caps (about 10% of fund). Adding a separate small-cap fund creates redundancy and concentrates small-cap exposure.

### What about commodities or REITs?

Not necessary. VTI includes REITs (2% allocation). If you want explicit REIT or commodity exposure, add them as a fourth or fifth holding, but they're not essential for basic diversification.

### Should I rebalance quarterly or annually?

Annually is sufficient. Quarterly rebalancing increases trading costs and taxes without meaningful benefit. Rebalance once yearly, ideally using new contributions.

### How do I handle employer stock/options?

Treat them separately. If you hold $50,000 in company stock, reduce stock holdings elsewhere by that amount. Concentrate on diversification across your total wealth, not just accounts.

### What if I want dividend income?

VTI, VTIAX, and BND all provide dividends and distributions. VTSAX pays approximately 1.5-2.0% yield. If you need higher income, substitute VTI (1.7% yield) or add dividend funds, but distributions from all three core funds are taxed efficiently (qualified dividends for stocks).
