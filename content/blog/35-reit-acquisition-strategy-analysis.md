## Key Investment Metrics Explained

### Funds From Operations (FFO)

FFO is the primary earnings metric for REITs, calculated as:

**FFO = Net Income + Depreciation + Amortization - Gains on Property Sales**

Unlike GAAP earnings, FFO removes the distortion of real estate depreciation, which doesn't reflect actual economic performance. Most analysts use FFO as the baseline valuation metric.

**2026 FFO Benchmarks:**
- High-quality assets: FFO growth of 4-7% annually
- Mid-tier properties: FFO growth of 2-4% annually
- Value-play assets: FFO growth of 0-3% annually

### Adjusted Funds From Operations (AFFO)

AFFO provides a more conservative view by accounting for:
- Maintenance capital expenditures
- Tenant improvement reserves
- Leasing commissions
- Structural reserve requirements

AFFO = FFO - Maintenance CapEx - Recurring Reserves

**AFFO Payout Ratios:**
- Conservative REITs: 70-80% AFFO payout
- Growth-focused REITs: 50-70% AFFO payout
- Value REITs: 80-90% AFFO payout

Higher AFFO payout ratios may indicate limited reinvestment capacity or distribution pressure.

### Cap Rate Analysis

Cap Rate = Net Operating Income / Property Value

2026 market cap rates by property type:
- Premium assets: 3.5-4.5% cap rates
- Core assets: 4.5-5.5% cap rates
- Value assets: 5.5-7.0% cap rates
- Distressed assets: 7.0%+ cap rates

Lower cap rates indicate higher valuations and lower relative returns at acquisition.

### Net Asset Value (NAV)

NAV per share = (Total Assets - Total Liabilities) / Shares Outstanding

NAV provides a fundamental value anchor independent of market sentiment. Most REITs trade at:
- Premium to NAV (0-10%) = Market confidence
- Discount to NAV (5-15%) = Market concerns
- Deep discount to NAV (15%+) = Significant value opportunity or distress

### Occupancy & Lease Metrics

**Occupancy Rates:**
- 95%+ occupancy = Pricing power, premium properties
- 90-95% occupancy = Market equilibrium
- 85-90% occupancy = Supply excess, pricing pressure
- Below 85% occupancy = Market distress

**Leasing Spreads:** Year-over-year rent growth on renewal
- Positive 3-5% spreads = Strong market demand
- Positive 5-10% spreads = Exceptional market conditions
- Flat to negative spreads = Market softness

## Valuation Framework

### Relative Valuation Methods

**Price-to-FFO Multiple:**
- Historical average: 13-15x FFO
- 2026 average: 12-14x FFO
- Quality premium: 14-18x FFO
- Value discount: 10-12x FFO

**Price-to-Book Multiple:**
- NAV premium: 0-15% typical
- Market sentiment indicator
- Useful for identifying value opportunities

### Absolute Valuation Methods

**Dividend Discount Model (DDM):**
- Discount rate: 6-8% (based on REIT cost of capital)
- Dividend growth: 2-4% perpetual assumption
- Terminal value significant portion of total value

**Sum-of-Parts (SOTP):**
- Value each property type separately
- Apply different cap rates by asset class
- Account for non-property assets and liabilities
- Compare to REIT market price

## Risk Analysis Framework

### Interest Rate Risk
- REITs highly sensitive to long-term rate environment
- Average REIT duration: 8-12 years
- Each 100 basis point rate increase = 7-10% valuation impact
- Hedging strategies: Fixed-rate debt, interest rate swaps

### Concentration Risk
- Single-tenant concentration: Monitor 15-20% tenant limits
- Geographic concentration: 30-40% in single state not uncommon
- Property-type concentration: Single-asset class REITs more volatile
- Mitigation: Diversified REIT selection or REIT ETFs

### Refinancing Risk
- Floating-rate debt exposure: Evaluate current hedging levels
- Maturity schedule: Review near-term refinancing needs
- Covenant compliance: Monitor debt service coverage ratios
- Market access: Assess REIT's refinancing capability

### Tenant Credit Risk
- Investment-grade tenants: 60-75% concentration typical
- Default history: Monitor last 3 years of tenant losses
- Lease coverage: Ensure NOI coverage of 1.25x+ for each property
- Diversification: Avoid concentration in any single tenant

### Operational Risk
- Management quality: Assess track record and experience
- Capital allocation: Review acquisition, disposition, and dividend strategy
- Development execution: Evaluate pipeline timeline and budget performance
- Maintenance discipline: Monitor property condition and CapEx reserving

## Dividend & Income Analysis

### Dividend Sustainability

Key indicators of sustainable distributions:
- AFFO payout ratios of 70-85%: Sustainable with growth
- FFO payout ratios of 75-90%: Standard for mature REITs
- Balance sheet flexibility: Unencumbered assets or acquisition capacity
- FFO growth sufficient to cover dividend growth

### Dividend Growth Potential

REITs typically grow dividends through:
1. **Organic Growth**: Rent growth from occupancy recovery and rate hikes (2-3%)
2. **Accretive Acquisitions**: Cap-rate accretion from buyer-seller arbitrage (0.5-1.5%)
3. **Development Stabilization**: New projects coming online (0.2-0.5%)
4. **Leverage Adjustment**: Opportunistic deleveraging for growth reinvestment (0-0.5%)

Combined target: 3-5% annual dividend growth for quality REITs.

## FAQ - Common REIT Questions

### What is the REIT structure and tax treatment?

REITs must distribute 90% of taxable income to shareholders. These distributions are taxed as ordinary income (not capital gains), with rates varying by investor tax bracket. This makes REITs ideal for tax-deferred accounts (IRAs, 401(k)s).

### Are REITs suitable for a diversified portfolio?

Yes. REITs provide real estate exposure without property management burden, along with professional oversight and liquidity benefits. Typical allocation: 10-20% of overall portfolio.

### How do REITs compare to real estate crowdfunding platforms?

REITs offer superior liquidity, transparency, and diversification. Crowdfunding platforms offer higher returns but lower liquidity and greater concentration risk. Use REITs for core allocation, crowdfunding for tactical opportunities.

### Should I use REIT ETFs or individual REITs?

REIT ETFs provide instant diversification and lower expense ratios (0.08-0.30%). Individual REITs allow targeted sector exposure and higher dividend concentration. Use ETFs for core allocation, individual REITs for satellite positions.

### How much of my portfolio should be in REITs?

Standard allocation: 10-20% for most investors. Higher allocations (20-30%) appropriate for income-focused portfolios. Lower allocations (5-10%) for growth-focused investors.

### What's the inflation hedge effectiveness of REITs?

REITs provide partial inflation protection through rent escalation clauses. Long-term correlation to inflation: 0.4-0.6. Less reliable than commodity REITs (timber, farmland) but better than bonds.

### How do REITs perform during recessions?

Property-type dependent:
- Defensive REITs (healthcare, storage): -10% to -20% during recessions
- Cyclical REITs (hospitality, retail): -30% to -50% during severe recessions
- Residential REITs: -15% to -25% typical recession decline

Historical data shows faster recovery than broad equity market.

### What are the main risks in REIT investing?

Primary risks: interest rate sensitivity, tenant concentration, refinancing risk, property-market cycles, and management decisions. Mitigation: diversification, quality focus, and financial strength assessment.
