## What Is Compound Interest?

Compound interest is interest earned not only on the initial principal but also on the accumulated interest from previous periods.

**The formula for compound interest:**

$$
A = P \left(1 + \frac{r}{n}\right)^{nt}
$$

Where:
- $ A $ = future value of the investment
- $ P $ = principal amount (initial investment)
- $ r $ = annual interest rate (decimal)
- $ n $ = number of times interest is compounded per year
- $ t $ = number of years

### Simple vs. Compound: The Real Difference

| Investment | Initial Principal | Annual Return | Time (Years) | Final Value (Simple) | Final Value (Compounded Annually) |
|----------|-------------------|---------------|--------------|------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| $10,000  | $10,000           | 7%            | 30           | $31,000               | $76,123                           |
| $10,000  | $10,000           | 10%           | 30           | $40,000               | $174,494                          |

Even at the same rate, compound interest nearly doubles the outcome over 30 years. At higher returns, the gap becomes exponential.

## The 10-Year Fast Track: Building $100K from $0

Can you compound to $100,000 in a decade? Yes—with discipline and the right return.

**Scenario:**
- Start: $0
- Monthly investment: $500
- Annual return: 10%
- Compounding: monthly

$$
FV = P \times \frac{(1 + r)^n - 1}{r}
$$
Where:
- $ P = $500 $
- $ r = 0.10/12 = 0.00833 $
- $ n = 120 $ months

$$
FV = 500 \times \frac{(1.00833)^{120} - 1}{0.00833} = \$97,625
$$

With a **12% return** (achievable with SCHD or aggressive ETF allocation), final value = **$113,400**.

**Breakdown:**

- Gains: $53,400

Over half your final balance comes from compound interest—even in just 10 years.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### **Q: Can compound interest make me rich fast?**

**A:** “Fast” is relative. With a 10% return, $500/month becomes ~$100,000 in 10 years. Truly fast wealth ($1M+) takes 20–30 years—but **without compounding, it’s impossible**.

### **Q: Is compound interest better with stocks or savings accounts?**

**A:** For long-term growth, **stocks win**. A 4.5% savings account doubles money in 16 years. The S&P 500 (10% avg) doubles it in **7.2 years** (Rule of 72).

### **Q: How do dividends speed up compounding?**

**A:** Dividends buy more shares. More shares = more future dividends. Reinvesting creates a **self-reinforcing growth loop**. Over 30 years, reinvested dividends can contribute **60–80%** of total returns.

### **Q: Should I invest in individual stocks or ETFs for compounding?**

**A:** ETFs reduce risk and simplify reinvestment. **SCHD** gives you 100 dividend growers in one trade. Individual stocks like **JNJ** or **KO** work if you want control—but require more research.

### **Q: What’s the fastest way to start compounding?**

**A:** Open a brokerage account, set up automatic monthly investments into **VOO or SCHD**, and enable dividend reinvestment. Start with $100/month if needed—**consistency beats size**.

### **Q: Does tax affect compound interest speed?**

**A:** Yes. In taxable accounts, you pay taxes on dividends and capital gains, slowing compounding. Use **tax-advantaged accounts** (IRA, 401(k)) to let returns grow **tax-free or tax-deferred**.

Example:
- Taxable account: 10% return, 15% dividend tax → effective return: ~9.2%
- Roth IRA: 10% return, tax-free → full compounding

Over 30 years, that 0.8% difference costs you **$120,000+** on a $500/month investment.
