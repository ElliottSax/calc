## Introduction

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) offer real estate exposure without direct property ownership. With yields around 3-4%, REITs provide portfolio diversification and inflation hedging. This guide shows optimal REIT allocation (5-10%), fund selection, and integration into balanced portfolios.

## REIT Characteristics

**Benefits:**
- Real estate exposure without management hassle
- Inflation hedge (rents rise with inflation)
- Dividend yield 3-5%
- Diversification from stocks/bonds
- Liquidity (trade like stocks)

**Risks:**
- Interest rate sensitivity (rising rates hurt valuations)
- Recession vulnerability (occupancy rates fall)
- Must distribute 90% of income as dividends (no reinvestment)

## REIT Performance Cycles

**2020 COVID:** REITs crashed 30% (office/retail pain)
**2021-2022:** REITs recovered 40% (rent growth)
**2023-2024:** REITs flat to modest gains (rates high, valuations compressed)

**Key insight:** REITs volatile but provide income during volatility

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What percentage REIT allocation is optimal?**
A: 5-10% for most balanced portfolios. More adds concentration risk to real estate sector.

**Q: Are residential REITs safer than office?**
A: Yes. Residential demand stable, office uncertain (work from home). Use diversified broad REITs (VNQ, SCHH) not sector-specific.

**Q: Should I buy individual REITs or REIT ETFs?**
A: ETFs for most investors. Individual REITs only if deep knowledge of real estate sectors.

**Q: How do REITs perform in recessions?**
A: Mixed. Residential performs well (always need housing). Office/retail struggle. Diversified REIT ETFs weather recessions reasonably.

**Q: Are REITs good inflation hedges?**
A: Yes. Rents historically rise with inflation. Over 20+ years, REIT allocations provide inflation protection bonds lack.

## Conclusion

REIT allocation (5-10% of portfolio) provides real estate diversification, inflation protection, and 3-5% yields. A $250,000 balanced portfolio with 8% REIT allocation (SCHH or VNQ) generates 7.3% returns with meaningful real estate exposure. Over 20 years, REIT allocation contributes to inflation hedge—critical for 30-40 year retirements. Broad REIT ETFs (SCHH, VNQ) recommended over sector-specific REITs for diversification.
