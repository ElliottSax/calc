## Index Fund Fundamentals

### What Are Index Funds?

Index funds track market indices by holding all (or representative sample) of securities in that index.

**Major Index Fund Types:**

| Index | Fund Symbol | Characteristics | Holdings |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| S&P 500 | VOO, IVV, SPLG | 500 largest U.S. companies | ~500 stocks |
| Total U.S. Market | VTI, ITOT, SCHB | All publicly traded U.S. | ~3,500 stocks |
| Total Intl. Stock | VXUS, IEFA, SCHE | All developed/emerging markets | ~8,000 stocks |
| U.S. Bonds | BND, AGG, SCHZ | U.S. Treasury/corporate bonds | ~6,000 bonds |
| Total World Stock | VT, IEFA+VTI combo | U.S. + International combined | ~12,000 stocks |

## Rebalancing Strategy

Disciplined rebalancing captures "buy low, sell high" without active trading.

### Quarterly Rebalancing Rules

**Trigger:** Rebalance when allocation drifts >5% from target

**Example: Three-fund portfolio with 50/20/30 target**

| Fund | Target | Current | Drift | Action |
|------|--------|---------|-------|--------|
| VTI | 50% | 48.2% | -1.8% | Hold |
| VXUS | 20% | 23.5% | +3.5% | Hold |
| BND | 30% | 28.3% | -1.7% | Hold |

No rebalancing (drifts < 5%)

**Next quarter after market move:**

| Fund | Target | Current | Drift | Action |
|------|--------|---------|-------|--------|
| VTI | 50% | 44.1% | -5.9% | BUY $5,900 |
| VXUS | 20% | 26.8% | +6.8% | SELL $6,800 |
| BND | 30% | 29.1% | -0.9% | Hold |

Sell $6,800 VXUS, buy $5,900 VTI, hold difference as cash buffer

### Tax-Loss Harvesting During Rebalancing

When rebalancing requires selling losers, implement tax-loss harvesting.

**Example:**

VTI position down 8% → Current value $45,600 (cost basis $49,500)
Needs to be sold for rebalancing

**Tax-loss harvest:**
```
Sell VTI: Realize $3,900 loss
Buy VOO (similar, not identical): $45,600
30-day wait period before repurchasing VTI

Tax benefit at 32% effective rate: $3,900 × 0.32 = $1,248 tax savings
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**
A: Quarterly if you have >$100k and time to monitor. Annual if you have <$100k or limited time. The key is consistency, not frequency.

**Q: Should I reinvest dividends automatically?**
A: Yes, for long-term buy-and-hold. Set up dividend reinvestment (DRIP) for all funds. This compounds growth automatically.

**Q: What's the minimum allocation to international stocks?**
A: 15-20% minimum for proper diversification. International represents 35% of global market cap—underweighting costs return potential.

**Q: Are bond allocations still relevant with low rates?**
A: Absolutely. Bonds stabilize portfolios during stock crashes. Bonds fell only 5% in 2022 while stocks fell 20%. Stability has value.

**Q: Should I use target-date funds instead of creating my own?**
A: Target-date funds (VFORX, VFIAX) are simpler but more expensive (0.08% vs 0.04%). If you can't manage rebalancing, target-date funds are acceptable.

**Q: Can I use ETFs and mutual funds together?**
A: Yes. ETFs are slightly more tax-efficient but require brokerage account. Mutual funds work in retirement accounts. Mix based on location.

**Q: How much should I have in bonds at my age?**
A: Rule of thumb: 110 minus your age = stock percentage. Age 50: 60% stocks / 40% bonds. This is conservative but provides baseline.

**Q: What if I forget to rebalance?**
A: No major damage. Rebalancing is beneficial but not critical. Most investors benefit from rebalancing every 1-2 years minimum.
