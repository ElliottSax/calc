## Treasury Bond Harvesting Fundamentals

Treasury bonds are ideal for tax-loss harvesting because they're sensitive to interest rate changes, creating periodic price declines that generate harvestable losses. Unlike volatile stocks, Treasury losses are predictable and aligned with monetary policy cycles.

When the Federal Reserve raises interest rates, Treasury bond ETF prices decline, creating tax-loss opportunities without affecting your bond portfolio's core function (income and stability).

## Why Treasury Bonds Create Harvesting Opportunities

**Interest Rate Sensitivity**:
- IEF (7-10 year Treasury): Duration 7 years
- For every 1% rate increase: ~7% price decline
- For every 1% rate decrease: ~7% price appreciation

**Rate Cycle Example** (2021-2023):
- 2021: Rates at historic lows (0.25%), Treasuries trading premium
- 2022: Rates spike to 4.5%, Treasuries down 15-20%
- 2023: Rates stabilize, Treasuries begin recovery

This dramatic cycle created harvesting opportunities:
- June 2022: IEF down 15% from purchase price
- Harvestable loss on \$100,000 position: \$15,000

## Step-by-Step Harvesting Calculation

### Step 1: Identify Underwater Treasury Position

**Example**:
- Purchased IEF at \$106 (January 2022)
- Current price \$98 (December 2022)
- Position size: 1,000 shares
- Current value: \$98,000
- Realized loss: \$8,000

### Step 2: Calculate Tax Savings

**Formula**:
```
Tax Savings = Realized Loss × Your Marginal Tax Rate
```

**Calculation**:
- Loss: \$8,000
- Federal tax bracket: 24%
- Federal tax savings: \$8,000 × 24% = \$1,920

**Variations by Bracket**:
- 12% bracket: \$8,000 × 12% = \$960 tax savings
- 22% bracket: \$8,000 × 22% = \$1,760 tax savings
- 32% bracket: \$8,000 × 32% = \$2,560 tax savings
- 37% bracket: \$8,000 × 37% = \$2,960 tax savings

### Step 3: Execute Wash-Sale Compliant Swap

Sell IEF at loss, switch to non-substantially-identical alternative:

**Option A**: Vanguard VGIT (3-6 year Treasuries)
- Different duration, different ETF
- Avoids wash-sale violation
- Can return to IEF after 31 days if desired

**Option B**: Switch to SHV (short-term Treasury)
- Even shorter duration
- Minimal interest rate risk
- Provides stability while waiting 31 days

**Option C**: Hold direct Treasury obligations
- Purchase Treasury bills directly from TreasuryDirect.gov
- Avoid ETF entirely for 31 days
- Switch back after period

### Step 4: Account for Tax Basis Wash-Sale Adjustment

**Critical Rule**: IRS adjusts cost basis when wash-sale violation occurs

**Correct Example** (no violation):
- Sell IEF at \$8,000 loss
- Switch to VGIT (different security)
- IEF cost basis unaffected
- Harvested loss captured: Full \$8,000

**Incorrect Example** (if you repeat mistake):
- Sell IEF at \$8,000 loss
- Immediately buy IEF again (wash-sale violation)
- IRS disallows loss, adds \$8,000 to new purchase cost basis
- New cost: \$106 + \$8,000/1,000 = \$114 (artificial inflation)
- Harvested loss: \$0 (completely disallowed)

**Lesson**: Follow 31-day rule strictly. The loss is only real if you avoid wash-sale violation.

## Treasury Harvesting Strategy by Interest Rate Environment

### Rising Rate Environment (Yields attractive)

**Optimal Harvesting**:
- More positions underwater
- Harvest larger amounts
- Hold replacement securities longer (capture eventual recovery)
- Example: Rise from 3% to 5% creates 10-15% price declines = substantial harvests

**Tax Impact**:
- Multiple harvests possible
- Cumulative savings: \$5,000-\$10,000 annually
- Over 5-year rising cycle: \$25,000-\$50,000 total savings

### Falling Rate Environment (Prices rising)

**Minimal Harvesting**:
- Few positions underwater
- Don't force harvesting when prices appreciating
- Enjoy gains (long-term capital gains rates are favorable)

**Tax Impact**:
- Harvesting opportunities rare
- Focus on other positions (dividend stocks, international ETFs)
- Wait for next rate cycle

### Stable Rate Environment (Sideways markets)

**Moderate Harvesting**:
- Normal volatility creates periodic losses (2-3% dips)
- Quarterly harvesting reviews find opportunities
- Example: IEF down 2%, harvest \$5,000 loss, switch to VGIT

**Tax Impact**:
- Consistent but modest: \$1,000-\$2,000 annually
- Regular, predictable benefit

## Treasury Harvesting Tax Forms

**IRS Form 8949** (Sales of Capital Assets):
- Report each harvest transaction (sale of Treasury ETF at loss)
- Date acquired, date sold, sales price, cost basis
- Calculate realized loss

**Schedule D** (Capital Gains and Losses):
- Summary of all gains/losses from year
- Net capital loss reported here
- Up to \$3,000 deduction against ordinary income
- Excess carries forward indefinitely

**Example Form Entry**:

| Date | Security | Qty | Cost Basis | Sales Price | Loss |
|------|----------|-----|-----------|------------|------|
| 6/15 | IEF | 264 | \$28,584 | \$27,500 | (\$1,084) |
| 9/20 | VGIT | 150 | \$15,600 | \$14,850 | (\$750) |
| 12/10 | SHV | 50 | \$5,050 | \$4,950 | (\$100) |

Report each transaction individually on 8949, then summary on Schedule D.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I harvest only if losses are substantial (\$5,000+)?**
A: No. Even \$1,000 loss saves \$240-\$370 in taxes. Harvest all losses, regardless of size.

**Q: What if I harvest Treasury loss and rates fall (prices rise)?**
A: You've already captured tax benefit. If replacement security outperforms, excellent. If original was better, you can repurchase after 31 days.

**Q: Can I carry forward harvested losses indefinitely?**
A: Yes. Capital losses carry forward until you die. However, you can only deduct \$3,000 per year against ordinary income. Excess rolls forward.

**Q: Should I harvest Treasury losses in my IRA?**
A: No. Tax-deferred accounts (IRA, 401k) don't benefit from loss harvesting. Tax-loss harvesting works only in taxable accounts.

**Q: Does Treasury harvesting reduce my yield?**
A: No. If you switch to VGIT or SHV (both generating 4-5% yields), your income continues. Income is uninterrupted while harvesting losses.
