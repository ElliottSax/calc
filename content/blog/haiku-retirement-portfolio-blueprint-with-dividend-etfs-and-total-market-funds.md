Real-world portfolio management requires balancing multiple objectives: growth, income, tax efficiency, and risk control.

## Rebalancing Principles

Rebalancing maintains your target allocation by selling outperformers and buying underperformers. Research shows annual rebalancing optimizes risk-adjusted returns.

**Rebalancing Example:**

Target allocation: 70% stocks, 30% bonds

After market movements:
- Stocks grew 12%: now 75% of portfolio
- Bonds grew 2%: now 25% of portfolio

**Rebalance action:**
- Sell $50,000 of stocks (bringing to 70%)
- Buy $50,000 of bonds (bringing to 30%)

This forces you to sell high (stocks) and buy low (bonds) automatically.

## Tax Considerations

Rebalancing in taxable accounts triggers capital gains taxes. Use these techniques:

1. **Rebalance within retirement accounts first** (IRA, 401k tax-free)
2. **Direct new contributions** to underweight asset classes
3. **Harvest losses** when rebalancing forces sales
4. **Use limit orders** to execute at favorable prices

## Sector Rotation Strategies

Sector performance varies across economic cycles:

**Interest Rate Rising Cycle:**
Underweight: Utilities, REITs
Overweight: Financials, Technology

**Economic Recession Cycle:**
Underweight: Cyclicals (Consumer Discretionary, Industrials)
Overweight: Defensives (Consumer Staples, Healthcare, Utilities)

**High Inflation Cycle:**
Underweight: Bonds, Consumer Staples
Overweight: Commodities, Energy, Financials

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Impact

**Scenario: Implement tax-loss harvesting in taxable account**

Year 1:
- Gains from winners: $8,000
- Losses from losers: -$5,000
- Tax-loss harvesting creates: -$3,000 (excess loss)
- Taxable gains: $8,000 - $5,000 - $3,000 = $0

The $3,000 excess loss carries forward, reducing future taxable income. Over 20 years, consistent tax-loss harvesting saves $8,000-15,000 in taxes.

## FAQ

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**
A: Annually. Quarterly costs excess trading; monthly is obsessive. Annual rebalancing balances tax efficiency and risk control.

**Q: What triggers rebalancing?**
A: When allocations drift >2-3% from target. This threshold balances responsiveness with tax efficiency.

**Q: Can I rebalance with new contributions instead of selling?**
A: Yes. Direct new money to underweight classes. This avoids selling appreciated positions (tax-efficient).

**Q: Should I rebalance during market crashes?**
A: Absolutely. Buy depressed assets (stocks). This is the automatic discipline of rebalancing.
