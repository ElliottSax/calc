# Income Investing with REITs: Complete 2026 Guide

Real Estate Investment Trusts (REITs) deliver substantial income while providing real asset exposure. This guide explains REIT characteristics, evaluates top options, and shows how to integrate REITs into diversified income portfolios.

## What Are REITs?

Real Estate Investment Trusts combine multiple properties into single investment vehicles:

**Key Characteristics:**
- Real asset exposure: Buildings, land, infrastructure
- High dividend yields: 4.5-6.5% typically
- Professional management: Expert property operations
- Tax efficiency: Dividends taxed at ordinary rates (not capital gains)
- Liquidity: Trade like stocks, daily pricing

## Types of REITs

**Residential REITs:**
- Example: AVB (AvalonBay Communities)
- Properties: Apartments, single-family homes
- Yield: 3.5-4.5%

**Commercial REITs:**
- Example: SPG (Simon Property Group)
- Properties: Shopping centers, malls
- Yield: 4.0-5.0%

**Industrial REITs:**
- Example: DLR (Digital Realty)
- Properties: Warehouses, data centers
- Yield: 3.5-4.5%

**Healthcare REITs:**
- Example: OHI (Omega Healthcare)
- Properties: Medical offices, senior living
- Yield: 5.0-6.5%

## Calculating REIT Returns

**Example: $50,000 in healthcare REIT (5.5% yield)**

Year 1:
- Dividend income: $2,750
- Property appreciation: +2%
- Total return: (2,750 + 1,000) / 50,000 = 7.5%

## Tax Considerations

**REIT Dividend Taxation:**
- Dividends taxed as ordinary income (up to 37%)
- NO capital gains treatment
- Higher tax-drag than stock dividends (15%)

**Strategic Placement:**
- Place REITs in tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA)
- Keeps high tax-drag away from taxable accounts
- Significantly improves after-tax returns

## Interest Rate Sensitivity

REITs show inverse relationship with rates:

**Rising Rate Scenario:**
- REITs decline 10-15%
- New REIT yields become attractive
- Opportunity to reinvest at higher yields

**Falling Rate Scenario:**
- REITs appreciate 10-15%
- Dividend income stable
- Capital appreciation bonus

## Real Estate Cycle Considerations

REITs outperform during:
- **Early recovery**: Rising occupancy, rents
- **Mid-cycle**: Peak operations, strong dividends
- **Late cycle**: Property appreciation, capital gains

REITs underperform during:
- **Recessions**: Occupancy declines, defaulted rents
- **Rising rates**: Valuation compression

## FAQ

**Q: Are REITs appropriate for income investors?**
A: Yes. REITs provide substantial income (4.5-6.5% yields) with real asset backing.

**Q: Should I own REITs in taxable accounts?**
A: No. Place in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid high tax-drag from ordinary income treatment.

**Q: What REIT allocation makes sense?**
A: 15-25% of overall portfolio provides real estate diversification without excessive concentration.

**Q: Can REITs decline in value?**
A: Yes. Interest rate increases and occupancy declines create price volatility.

**Q: How do REIT dividends compare to stock dividends?**
A: Higher (4.5-6.5% vs 2-4%) but taxed unfavorably (ordinary rates vs qualified).
