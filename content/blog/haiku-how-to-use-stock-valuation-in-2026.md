## Stock Valuation Decision Framework for 2026

Using current conditions to make specific buy/sell decisions.

**Rule 1: S&P 500 Index Valuation**

Current P/E: 18.2x
Historical range: 16-20x
Assessment: Fair value (within normal range)

Decision framework:
- P/E < 16x: Undervalued (increase equity allocation)
- P/E 16-20x: Fair (maintain target allocation)
- P/E > 20x: Overvalued (reduce equity allocation)

**2026 Action:** Maintain target equity allocation. No urgency to buy or sell. Rebalance according to plan.

**Traditional rebalancing rule:** Rebalance when allocation drifts 5% from target.

**Another example:**

## 2026 Valuation Monitoring Checklist

Quarterly check-in framework.

**Quarterly Monitoring (March, June, September, December):**

- [ ] S&P 500 P/E ratio (is it still 16-20x?)
  - If > 20x: Reduce equity exposure
  - If < 16x: Increase equity exposure

- [ ] Treasury bond yield (still 4%+?)
  - If rising above 5%: Increase bond allocation
  - If falling below 4%: Reduce bond allocation

- [ ] Dividend yields (still 3%+?)
  - If > 4%: Excellent buying opportunity
  - If < 2.5%: Valuation stretched

- [ ] My portfolio allocation (drifted from target?)
  - If > 5% drift: Rebalance
  - Otherwise: Hold

- [ ] Any major sector moves?
  - Technology too expensive (>25x P/E): Trim
  - Financials cheap (<12x P/E): Add

**Time required:** 15-20 minutes quarterly

## Common 2026 Valuation Mistakes to Avoid

Learning from errors accelerates wealth building.

**Mistake 1: Chasing 2023-2024 returns**

What happened then:
- Magnificent 7 growth stocks surged
- 30%+ annual returns common
- FOMO (fear of missing out) intense

2026 reality:
- Growth stocks retreated (P/E fell from 30x to 22x)
- Returns normalized (6-8% expected)
- Trying to repeat past returns leads to overpaying

**Avoid:** Don't buy expensive growth hoping for 30% returns. Expect 7-8% long-term.

**Mistake 2: Avoiding bonds because of 2020-2021**

What happened then:
- Bonds yielded 0%
- Stocks surged 20%+
- Bonds seemed pointless

2026 reality:
- Bonds yield 4.8% (genuine income)
- Stocks yield 1.6% (minimal income)
- Bonds competitive with stocks for total return

**Avoid:** Include 30-50% bonds in 2026. Yields justify it.

**Mistake 3: Timing the market**

What they do:
- Wait for "perfect entry" at $150
- Miss 7-8% annual return waiting
- Eventually buy at $180 out of frustration

2026 reality:
- Markets rarely hit exact price targets
- Dollar-cost averaging outperforms waiting
- Start investing monthly now, not later

**Avoid:** Dollar-cost average $1,000/month consistently. Beat 95% of market timers.
