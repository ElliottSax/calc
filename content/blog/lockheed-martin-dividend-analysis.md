## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Lockheed Martin a Dividend Aristocrat?

Yes, Lockheed Martin achieved Dividend Aristocrat status in 2019 after 25 consecutive years of dividend increases. The company now has a 21-year streak and strong prospects for extending it.

### What is Lockheed Martin's dividend yield?

Lockheed Martin's current dividend yield is approximately 2.7-2.9%, higher than most aerospace/defense peers and above the S&P 500 average.

### How often does Lockheed increase its dividend?

Lockheed Martin increases its dividend annually, typically announced in September and effective for the December payment. Recent increases have averaged 7-9% annually.

### Is Lockheed Martin's dividend safe?

Yes, the dividend is highly secure. The 44% payout ratio, $160B contract backlog, and government customer base provide exceptional stability. Lockheed has never cut its dividend.

### What drives Lockheed Martin's dividend growth?

F-35 production ramp-up, international sales expansion, geopolitical tensions driving defense budgets, and sustainment revenue growth (recurring, high-margin) support 7-9% annual dividend increases.

### How does Lockheed compare to other defense stocks?

Lockheed offers the highest yield (2.8%) among major defense primes while maintaining strong dividend growth (7-9%). This combination of yield and growth makes it the premier defense dividend stock.
