## Calculating Risk-Adjusted Return for Sector ETFs

Risk-adjusted returns reveal whether sector ETF performance compensates you for sector-specific risk undertaken. This metric guides optimal sector allocation within your equity portfolio and justifies sector rotation strategies.

## Understanding Risk-Adjusted Returns

Different sectors deliver different returns with different volatility. Risk-adjusted metrics reveal which sectors provided best value for risks undertaken.

## The Sharpe Ratio Formula

**Sharpe Ratio = (Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation**

## Worked Example: Sector ETF Risk-Adjusted Return

Compare two sector ETFs:

**Utilities Sector ETF:**
- 5-year return: 8.2%
- Annual volatility: 11.5%
- Risk-free rate: 4.5%

**Calculation:**
Sharpe Ratio = (8.2% - 4.5%) / 11.5%
Sharpe Ratio = 3.7% / 11.5%
**Sharpe Ratio = 0.322**

**Technology Sector ETF:**
- 5-year return: 18.5%
- Annual volatility: 22.3%
- Risk-free rate: 4.5%

**Calculation:**
Sharpe Ratio = (18.5% - 4.5%) / 22.3%
Sharpe Ratio = 14% / 22.3%
**Sharpe Ratio = 0.628**

Tech delivered better risk-adjusted returns despite much higher volatility.

## Step-by-Step Calculation

1. Collect 5-year monthly returns for sector ETF
2. Calculate average return
3. Calculate standard deviation
4. Subtract risk-free rate from average
5. Divide excess return by volatility

## Sector Rotation and Risk-Adjusted Returns

Economic cycles affect sector Sharpe ratios:

**Expansion Phase:**
- Tech/discretionary Sharpe ratios improve
- Defensive sector Sharpe ratios decline
- Rotation into growth justified

**Contraction Phase:**
- Defensive sector Sharpe ratios improve
- Growth sector ratios decline
- Rotation into stability justified

## Comparing Sectors Using Sharpe Ratio

When allocating across sectors:
1. Calculate Sharpe ratios for identical periods
2. Compare to market average (usually 0.50-0.60)
3. Evaluate recent trends vs. long-term
4. Consider economic cycle positioning
5. Account for valuation (high valuations mean lower Sharpe ratios)

## Diversification Benefits

Holding multiple sectors improves overall portfolio Sharpe ratios:
- Low-correlation sectors diversify risk
- Portfolio volatility lower than weighted average of sectors
- Sector diversification essential for sustainable risk-adjusted returns

## FAQ

**Q: What's a good Sharpe ratio for sector ETFs?**
A: 0.4-0.6 is typical; above 0.6 is excellent.

**Q: Should I overweight high Sharpe ratio sectors?**
A: Not necessarily. Valuations change, and today's winners become tomorrow's losers.

**Q: How does sector rotation affect Sharpe ratios?**
A: Economic cycles cause sector Sharpe ratios to fluctuate significantly.

**Q: Can I use Sharpe ratios to time sector rotation?**
A: Use as one factor among many. Never rely on Sharpe ratio alone.

**Q: How often should I recalculate sector Sharpe ratios?**
A: Quarterly to assess changing risk-return dynamics.

## Conclusion

Calculating risk-adjusted returns for sector ETFs reveals which sectors provide best value for risks undertaken. By analyzing Sharpe ratios across sectors, you make informed allocation decisions balancing growth potential with risk management across economic cycles.
