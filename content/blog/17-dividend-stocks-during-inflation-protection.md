## Key Metrics and Calculations

### Essential Formulas

**Dividend Yield Formula:**
```
Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividend Per Share ÷ Stock Price) × 100
Example: $3 annual dividend ÷ $75 stock price = 4% yield
```

**Payout Ratio Formula:**
```
Payout Ratio = (Total Dividends Paid ÷ Net Income) × 100
Safe Range: 40-60% (varies by industry)
```

**Total Return Formula:**
```
Total Return = (Capital Gain + Dividends) ÷ Initial Investment × 100
Example: ($5 gain + $3 dividend) ÷ $100 = 8% total return
```

## FAQ: Dividend stocks during inflation protection

**Q: What is the most common mistake investors make with dividend stocks during inflation protection?**
A: The most common mistake is ignoring tax implications. Many dividend strategies that look profitable on paper become unprofitable after taxes. Always model after-tax returns before implementing any strategy.

**Q: How often should I review my dividend stocks during inflation protection strategy?**
A: Review quarterly at minimum, ideally aligned with quarterly earnings reports. However, avoid making emotional changes based on short-term market movements. Rebalance annually or when allocations drift beyond target ranges.

**Q: Can dividend stocks during inflation protection work during market crashes?**
A: Yes, and often better during crashes. Dividend stocks typically outperform during corrections, and income remains steady. Many investors use dividends from quality stocks to buy during downturns.

**Q: What's the relationship between dividend stocks during inflation protection and inflation?**
A: Dividend growth historically outpaces inflation for quality stocks. Companies with pricing power (utilities, consumer staples, healthcare) raise dividends faster than inflation, protecting your purchasing power.

**Q: Should I use dividend stocks during inflation protection in a retirement account?**
A: Absolutely. Tax-advantaged accounts (401k, IRA) are ideal for dividend stocks during inflation protection since you avoid dividend taxes. This allows compounding without tax drag.

**Q: How do I compare dividend stocks during inflation protection across different sectors?**
A: Each sector has different characteristics. Utilities are stable but low-growth. Tech dividend stocks grow faster but higher volatility. REITs provide inflation protection. Balance sectors based on your risk tolerance and goals.

**Q: What percentage of my portfolio should use dividend stocks during inflation protection?**
A: This depends on your goals. For income focus, 80-100% dividend stocks. For total return, 50% dividend stocks + 50% growth. Most balanced investors use 60-70% dividend focus.
