# Sector ETFs Portfolio Beta Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

Portfolio Beta measures how volatile your sector ETF mix is relative to the S&P 500 index. A beta of 1.0 means your portfolio moves exactly with the market. Beta above 1.0 indicates more volatility and potential returns. Understanding beta helps manage risk expectations.

## Understanding Portfolio Beta

Portfolio Beta measures how volatile your sector ETF mix is relative to the S&P 500 index:
- Beta = 1.0: Moves exactly with market
- Beta > 1.0: More volatile than market (higher risk, potential higher returns)
- Beta < 1.0: Less volatile than market (lower risk, moderate returns)

A portfolio with beta 1.3 declines 13% when market declines 10%.

## Why Calculate Sector ETF Beta

Different sectors have different betas:
- Tech (XLK): Beta ~1.4 (high growth, high volatility)
- Utilities (XLU): Beta ~0.7 (defensive, low volatility)
- Healthcare (XLV): Beta ~0.9 (defensive growth)

Knowing your portfolio beta helps manage risk and set realistic return expectations.

## Portfolio Beta Calculation Formula

**Portfolio Beta = (Weight_1 × Beta_1) + (Weight_2 × Beta_2) + ... + (Weight_n × Beta_n)**

Example with $100,000 sector allocation:
- Technology (XLK) 25% × Beta 1.4 = 0.35
- Healthcare (XLV) 25% × Beta 0.9 = 0.225
- Industrials (XLI) 20% × Beta 1.1 = 0.22
- Utilities (XLU) 20% × Beta 0.7 = 0.14
- Staples (XLP) 10% × Beta 0.8 = 0.08
- **Portfolio Beta: 1.01**

## Beta Volatility Impact

When S&P 500 declines 20%:
- Beta 0.87 portfolio: Down 17.4% (less pain)
- Beta 1.0 portfolio: Down 20% (market rate)
- Beta 1.21 portfolio: Down 24.2% (greater pain)

Conservative investors should target portfolio beta 0.85-1.0. Growth investors can accept 1.1-1.3.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I maintain high returns with low portfolio beta?**
A: Not always. Lower-beta portfolios (0.7-0.8) typically generate 7-8% returns vs. 9-10% for market-beta portfolios.

**Q: Does high beta guarantee higher returns?**
A: No. It increases volatility but not guaranteed returns. Market can decline while high-beta stocks fall more.

**Q: Should I rebalance if my portfolio beta drifts?**
A: Yes. Rebalance when beta drifts 0.1+ from target. Sector rotation naturally changes beta over time.

**Q: What's the ideal portfolio beta?**
A: For most investors, 0.95-1.05 is ideal. Tracks market with slight diversification benefit.

**Q: How do I lower my portfolio beta?**
A: Increase positions in healthcare, utilities, and staples. Reduce technology and energy holdings.
