## Introduction

All-weather portfolios designed specifically with low-cost index funds provide resilient returns across economic cycles at expense ratios below 0.10%. Build a portfolio that weathers economic uncertainty while minimizing fees and complexity.

## All-Weather Index Fund Allocation Framework

### Core Principle: Multi-Asset Diversification

All-weather index fund portfolios combine:
- **U.S. Stocks:** 40-50% (growth anchor)
- **International Stocks:** 10-15% (geographic diversification)
- **U.S. Bonds:** 25-35% (stability)
- **Real Assets:** 5-10% (inflation hedge)
- **Cash:** 0-5% (optionality)

## All-Weather Index Rebalancing Strategy

### Rules-Based Rebalancing

When allocation drifts >5%, rebalance:

```
Sell overweight assets
Buy underweight assets
Minimizes emotional decisions
Captures "buy low, sell high"
```

### Scenario-Based Adjustment

If expecting major economic change:

**If inflation likely:**
- Reduce long bonds (TLT) from 10% to 5%
- Increase TIPS/real assets from 15% to 20%
- This reduces duration loss when rates rise

**If recession likely:**
- Increase long bonds (TLT) from 10% to 15%
- Reduce stocks from 50% to 45%
- Increase cash from 5% to 10%
- This positions for stock crash and bond recovery

### Automatic Rebalancing Services

Some platforms offer automatic rebalancing:
- Vanguard Personal Advisor: $30k minimum
- Betterment: 0.25% fee, lower minimums
- DIY with spreadsheet: $0 cost

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is all-weather portfolio outperforming now?**
A: All-weather underperforms pure stock portfolios during bull markets (like 2023-2024), but provides 50%+ downside protection during crashes (like 2022).

**Q: How much return should I expect?**
A: 7-8% during normal periods, but 2-3% during stock bull markets, and +5% during recessions.

**Q: Should I use target-date index funds instead?**
A: Target-date funds (VFORX) are simpler but cost 0.08% (vs. 0.05% DIY) and use less real assets. DIY better for all-weather.

**Q: Can I build all-weather with only 3 funds?**
A: Yes, VOO/BND/VNQ is sufficient and extremely simple. More funds add complexity without major benefit.

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**
A: Quarterly or when 5% drift occurs. More frequent adds cost; less frequent loses benefits.

**Q: Should I include gold in all-weather?**
A: Optional. 5% IAU adds tail-risk protection but adds complexity. Many all-weather funds skip gold.
