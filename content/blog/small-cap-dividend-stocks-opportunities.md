## Introduction
As a savvy retail investor, you're likely no stranger to the allure of dividend investing. The promise of regular income and potential long-term growth can be a powerful draw, especially in today's low-interest-rate environment. While many investors focus on large-cap dividend stocks, small-cap dividend stocks offer a compelling alternative for those seeking higher yields and growth potential. In this article, we'll delve into the world of small-cap dividend stocks, exploring the opportunities and challenges they present, and highlighting several examples of promising small-cap dividend stocks to consider.

## Analysis
Small-cap dividend stocks are often overlooked in favor of their larger counterparts, but they can provide a unique combination of income and growth. These stocks typically have a market capitalization of less than $2 billion and often operate in niche industries or emerging markets. As a result, they can be more volatile than large-cap stocks, but they also offer the potential for higher returns. When evaluating small-cap dividend stocks, it's essential to consider factors such as current yield, payout ratio, and growth rate. A high current yield can provide a attractive income stream, while a reasonable payout ratio ensures the dividend is sustainable. A strong growth rate, meanwhile, can drive long-term appreciation in the stock's value.

One key benefit of small-cap dividend stocks is their ability to fly under the radar. While large-cap stocks are closely watched by analysts and investors, small-cap stocks can be less covered, creating opportunities for savvy investors to uncover hidden gems. Additionally, small-cap dividend stocks often have more room for growth, as they can expand into new markets, develop new products, or increase their market share. This growth potential, combined with a regular dividend payment, can make small-cap dividend stocks an attractive addition to a diversified portfolio.

Another example is **Triangle Capital (TCAP)**, a small-cap business development company (BDC) that yields 8.1% and has a payout ratio of 65%. In comparison, **Main Street Capital (MAIN)**, a large-cap BDC, yields 7.4% and has a payout ratio of 73%. While Main Street Capital is a more established player, Triangle Capital offers a higher yield and a more attractive valuation.

Other small-cap dividend stocks worth considering include **Gladstone Commercial (GOOD)**, which yields 6.5% and has a payout ratio of 78%, and **STAG Industrial (STAG)**, which yields 4.1% and has a payout ratio of 74%. **Hudson Pacific Properties (HPP)**, a small-cap REIT, yields 3.9% and has a payout ratio of 69%, with a 5-year average annual growth rate of 4.2%. These stocks demonstrate the diversity of opportunities available in the small-cap dividend space, from REITs to BDCs and beyond.

## Strategy
So, how can you incorporate small-cap dividend stocks into your investment strategy? First, it's essential to approach these stocks with a long-term perspective. Small-cap stocks can be volatile, and their prices may fluctuate rapidly. However, by focusing on the underlying fundamentals of the business and the dividend payment, you can ride out market fluctuations and reap the rewards of long-term growth.

When selecting small-cap dividend stocks, look for companies with a strong track record of dividend payments, a reasonable payout ratio, and a solid growth prospects. It's also crucial to diversify your portfolio, spreading your investments across a range of industries and sectors to minimize risk. Consider allocating a portion of your portfolio to small-cap dividend stocks, and regularly review your holdings to ensure they remain aligned with your investment goals.

## Conclusion
In conclusion, small-cap dividend stocks offer a compelling opportunity for retail investors seeking income and growth. By focusing on companies with strong fundamentals, a reasonable payout ratio, and a solid growth prospects, you can unlock the potential of these often-overlooked stocks. Whether you're a seasoned investor or just starting to build your portfolio, small-cap dividend stocks like **National Retail Properties (NNN)**, **Triangle Capital (TCAP)**, **Gladstone Commercial (GOOD)**, **STAG Industrial (STAG)**, and **Hudson Pacific Properties (HPP)** are definitely worth considering. With their high yields, growth potential, and relatively low valuations, small-cap dividend stocks can be a powerful addition to a diversified investment portfolio.
