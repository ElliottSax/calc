## Investment Fundamentals

Building wealth through investing requires understanding compounding, diversification, and discipline. Success comes from consistent contributions, appropriate asset allocation, and maintaining positions through market cycles.

## Core Concepts

**Compounding**: Each dollar earned generates future returns.

Formula: Final Value = Initial x (1 + rate)^years

Example $10,000 at 7%:
- 20 years: $38,697
- 30 years: $76,123

**Diversification**: Multiple uncorrelated assets reduce volatility. Single stock portfolios experience 40-60% volatility; diversified portfolios experience 12-18% volatility.

## Asset Allocation Framework

Time horizon determines appropriate allocation:

| Time Horizon | Stock % | Bond % | Alternatives |
|--------------|---------|--------|--------------|
| 0-3 years | 0% | 100% | 0% |
| 3-7 years | 30% | 70% | 0% |
| 7-15 years | 60% | 30% | 10% |
| 15+ years | 80% | 10% | 10% |
| 20+ years | 90% | 5% | 5% |

Younger investors with long time horizons can weather volatility and benefit from equity returns. Retirees with short horizons need bond stability.

## Dollar-Cost Averaging Strategy

Investing fixed amounts regularly reduces timing risk. Consistent investing captures market movements regardless.

**Example: $500 monthly investment**

| Month | Price | Shares | Total Invested | Total Shares |
|-------|-------|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Jan | $100 | 5.00 | $500 | 5.00 |
| Feb | $110 | 4.55 | $1,000 | 9.55 |
| Mar | $95 | 5.26 | $1,500 | 14.81 |
| Apr | $105 | 4.76 | $2,000 | 19.57 |
| May | $120 | 4.17 | $2,500 | 23.74 |

Average purchase price: $105.96

Dollar-cost averaging achieves more shares than lump sum.

## Risk Management Fundamentals

**Volatility vs. Risk:**
- Volatility: Short-term price fluctuations (normal)
- Risk: Permanent capital loss

**Risk Mitigation:**
1. Diversification: Own 20+ stocks or funds
2. Quality: Profitable companies with strong balance sheets
3. Valuation: Avoid overpaying (check P/E ratios)
4. Time Horizon: Longer horizons accept more volatility
5. Position Sizing: No single position exceeds 5% of portfolio

**Expected Drawdowns:**
- 100% stocks: 40-50% in bear markets (recovers in 3-5 years)
- 60/40 portfolio: 20-30% (recovers in 2-3 years)
- 40/60 portfolio: 10-15% (recovers in 1-2 years)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much should I have saved before investing?**
A: Maintain 3-6 months emergency fund in cash/savings. Invest amounts you won't need for 5+ years.

**Q: Is active investing better than index investing?**
A: Research shows 85% of active managers underperform benchmarks over 15+ years after fees. Index investing provides better risk-adjusted returns.

**Q: When should I rebalance my portfolio?**
A: Rebalance annually or when allocations drift 5-10% from target. Rebalancing forces buy low, sell high discipline.

**Q: Should I invest during market downturns?**
A: Yes. Lower prices mean each dollar buys more shares, improving long-term returns.

**Q: How do I know if I'm taking appropriate risk?**
A: Your risk tolerance should match your time horizon. If downturns prevent sleep, reduce equity exposure. Most investors can handle 30-40% drawdowns.
