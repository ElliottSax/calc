# 2026 Guide: Dollar-Cost Averaging for Generational Wealth

Generational wealth through dollar-cost averaging means investing the same amount monthly for 30-50 years, allowing compounding to create extraordinary wealth for descendants.

## Multi-Generation DCA Model

Unlike FI (15-20 year horizon), generational wealth DCA spans 50+ years across multiple lifetimes.

### Formula: 50-Year DCA Growth

```
Future Value = Monthly Investment × [((1 + Return/12)^(Months) - 1) / (Return/12)]
```

**Worked Example:**

Monthly DCA: $2,000 (= $24,000 annually)
Time period: 50 years (1,200 months)
Expected return: 7% annually

Future value calculation:
= $2,000 × [((1.00583)^1200 - 1) / 0.00583]
= $2,000 × 5,637
= **$11,274,000**

**$2,000 monthly for 50 years ($1.2M total invested) grows to $11.3M.**

For generational wealth, this $11.3M could be split:
- Generation 1 receives: $1M
- Generation 2 receives: $3M
- Generation 3 receives: $3M
- Dynasty trust: $4.3M (continuing for Gen 4+)

## DCA Allocation for Generational Wealth

### Long-Term Holdings (Multi-Generational)

For 50+ year horizons, allocation should emphasize:

1. **VTI (50%):** Broad market growth
2. **VXUS (25%):** International diversification, emerging market growth
3. **SCHD (15%):** Dividend growth, income for distributions
4. **Cash/Bonds (10%):** Stability

**Why this allocation?**
- No bonds needed for 50-year horizon (can tolerate volatility)
- Maximum equity exposure
- SCHD provides income for future generations
- Diversification protects against single-country risk

### Rebalancing Generational DCA

Rebalance minimally (every 5-10 years) to avoid taxes:

Target: 50/25/15/10

After 10 years (VTI outperformed = 55%, VXUS = 23%, etc):
- Sell $100,000 VTI
- Buy $50,000 VXUS, $50,000 bonds
- Return to 50/25/15/10

Rebalancing annually would trigger capital gains; once per decade is ideal.

## Tax Efficiency in Generational DCA

Long-term DCA portfolios benefit from stepped-up basis at death.

### Stepped-Up Basis Advantage

**Gen 1 dies owning $5M portfolio:**
- Cost basis: $192,000 (total invested over 50 years)
- Current value: $5,000,000
- Unrealized gain: $4,808,000 (untaxed)

**Gen 2 inherits:**
- New cost basis: $5,000,000 (stepped-up to current market value)
- No tax on Gen 1's $4.8M gain
- If Gen 2 sells immediately: Zero capital gains tax

**If Gen 2 holds 30+ more years:**
- Starting new basis: $5,000,000
- Growth: 7% annually
- 30-year value: $5,000,000 × (1.07)^30 = $38,100,000
- New gain from Gen 2's perspective: $33,100,000
- Only Gen 2's gain taxable when Gen 2 dies (not Gen 1's)

**Generational wealth gets "free pass" on taxes every generation through stepped-up basis.**

This is why generational DCA is so powerful: compounding + tax-free transfers every generation.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I DCA for 50 years in a 529 plan?**
A: No, 529 plans are for college funding (beneficiary ages 0-22). Use dynasty trust for 50+ year generational DCA.

**Q: Should Gen 2 contribute to generational DCA?**
A: Optional, but beneficial. Even $500/month from Gen 2 amplifies growth to $300M+ over their 50-year period.

**Q: What happens if I stop DCA early?**
A: Portfolio value is locked in. Compounding continues, but no additional new capital. A $2M portfolio at year 25 becomes $30M by year 50, vs. $11M if you stopped DCA at year 25.

**Q: Should I use dynasty trust or just let heirs inherit?**
A: Dynasty trust is strongly recommended. Avoids estate taxes, gives legal protection, ensures investment discipline continues.

**Q: What's the best DCA allocation for 50 years?**
A: 60% VTI, 25% VXUS, 15% SCHD, 0% bonds (bonds unnecessary for 50+ horizons). Maximum growth allocation.
