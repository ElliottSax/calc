## Concentration Strategy for Wealth Preservation

While diversification is usually preferred, concentrated positions in strongest moats can be appropriate:

- 30-35% in strongest moat (J&J)
- 20% in second-strongest (Coca-Cola)
- 15% in third (P&G)
- 15% in supporting holdings
- 15-20% in bonds/alternatives

This concentration on proven winners reduces complexity while maintaining safety through diversification within the basket of moats.

## FAQ

Q: Should retirees overweight dividend aristocrats?
A: Yes. For retirees, dividend reliability > growth. Aristocrats provide:
- Predictable 2-4% income
- Growing dividends (inflation protection)
- Minimal drawdown risk
- 40+ year proven track records

Allocation: 70-80% aristocrats, 20-30% growth/bonds

Q: What's the concentration risk of 3-5 positions?
A: Minimal for mega-cap moats:
- J&J = $400B market cap
- Single position unlikely to cut dividend
- Industry disruption extremely unlikely in 40 years
- 3-5 position concentration acceptable for $10M+ portfolios

Q: Should I diversify beyond value holdings?
A: Yes. Recommended:
- 70% value aristocrats
- 15% growth stocks (SCHD, VUG)
- 10% bonds
- 5% alternatives

This maintains 70% stability, 30% growth.

Q: Can individual aristocrats replace diversified index funds?
A: Yes, if:
- Portfolio >$5M (enough capital)
- 3-5+ positions held (diversification)
- 40+ year time horizon
- Tax-efficient structure

Individual aristocrats can outperform indexes through tax efficiency.
