## Introduction
As a retail investor, navigating the world of dividend investing can be daunting, especially with the vast array of stocks available in the market. Dividend stock screeners and tools have become essential for investors to filter through the noise and identify potential dividend-paying stocks that align with their investment goals. In this article, we will delve into the world of dividend stock screeners and tools, exploring their features, benefits, and how to use them to create a robust dividend investment portfolio. We will also examine specific dividend stocks, including their current yields, payout ratios, and growth rates, to provide a comprehensive understanding of the investment landscape.

## Analysis
Dividend stock screeners and tools are designed to help investors quickly and efficiently identify dividend-paying stocks that meet specific criteria. These criteria can include current yield, payout ratio, dividend growth rate, market capitalization, and sector. Some popular dividend stock screeners and tools include Finviz, Yahoo Finance, and Dividend.com. These platforms provide investors with a wealth of data and analytics to inform their investment decisions. For example, using Finviz, we can screen for dividend stocks with a current yield above 4%, a payout ratio below 70%, and a market capitalization above $10 billion. This screen yields stocks such as ExxonMobil (XOM), with a current yield of 4.32%, a payout ratio of 54.11%, and a 5-year dividend growth rate of 4.23%. Another example is 3M (MMM), with a current yield of 4.14%, a payout ratio of 57.14%, and a 5-year dividend growth rate of 7.14%.

This involves screening for dividend stocks that meet specific criteria, such as current yield, payout ratio, and dividend growth rate, and then conducting further research on the underlying company's financials, management team, and industry trends. For example, an investor looking to create a dividend-focused portfolio might start by screening for dividend stocks with a current yield above 3%, a payout ratio below 60%, and a market capitalization above $5 billion.
