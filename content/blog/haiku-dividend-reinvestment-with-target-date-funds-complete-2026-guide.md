## Understanding Target-Date Funds

Target-date funds automatically adjust allocation from aggressive (stocks) to conservative (bonds) as you approach retirement. They eliminate rebalancing complexity while providing appropriate risk for your life stage.

These funds pair exceptionally well with dividend reinvestment strategies.

## Target-Date Fund Basics

### How Target-Date Funds Work

**Target-date fund for age 65 (e.g., VFIAX target 2045):**

**Age 45 (20 years from retirement):**
- 80% stocks, 20% bonds (aggressive)
- Focus on growth

**Age 55 (10 years from retirement):**
- 60% stocks, 40% bonds (moderate)
- Balance growth and stability

**Age 65 (retirement):**
- 40% stocks, 60% bonds (conservative)
- Focus on income

Automatic glide path requires no active management.

## Target-Date Fund Advantages

### Automatic Glide Path

Target-date funds eliminate:
- Rebalancing decisions
- Determining appropriate allocation
- Tracking multiple positions
- Annual adjustment complexity

Simply invest and let it work.

### Diversification Built-In

Target-date funds provide:
- US stock diversification (1,000+ stocks)
- International diversification (25-35% allocation)
- Bond diversification (1,000+ bonds)
- Sector and geography balance

One fund = complete portfolio diversification.

### Professional Management

Vanguard's investment team:
- Monitors allocation continuously
- Adjusts glide path based on market conditions
- Rebalances automatically
- Improves returns through tactical management

Average outperformance: 0.3-0.5% annually.

## Target-Date Fund Limitations

### Lower Dividend Yield

Target-date funds yield 2-2.5%:
- Diversified across stocks and bonds
- Bonds drag yield down (4% interest, but bonds only 15-30%)
- International stocks lower yield
- Result: Lower reinvestment than pure dividend

### Fixed Glide Path

Target-date glide path cannot adjust for:
- Personal risk tolerance (some want less conservative at 60)
- Market conditions (tech boom may support higher stock allocation)
- Early/delayed retirement (inflexible structure)

### Overlap with Dividend Addition

Combining target-date with SCHD creates:
- Overlapping US stock exposure
- Redundant US market coverage
- Simpler to use pure target-date fund

## FAQ

**Should I use target-date funds or manually rebalance?**
Target-date easier for hands-off approach. Manual rebalancing slightly outperforms (0.2-0.3%) if disciplined.

**Can I use pure target-date without dividend overlay?**
Yes, works perfectly. Simplest approach. Target-date alone produces 6.5-7% returns (vs 7.5% with SCHD overlay).

**What if target-date fund underperforms?**
Compare to benchmark. VFIAX typically matches performance (not beats). This is normal and expected.

**Should I hold target-date in Roth IRA?**
Yes, excellent choice. Tax-free growth + automatic glide path = optimal combination.

**At what age should I stop dividend overlay?**
Age 55-60. Shift fully to target-date fund as retirement approaches. Pure target-date fund handles appropriate allocation glide path.
