## Introduction

An all-weather portfolio maintains performance across rising/falling rates, inflation, deflation, and recessions. Bond funds play a critical role, acting as shock absorbers during stock crashes while providing income stability. This comprehensive guide shows how to construct all-weather portfolios specifically designed around bond funds, including exact allocations for different economic scenarios, factor diversification, and expected returns across multiple conditions.

## Understanding All-Weather Portfolio Concept

Ray Dalio's all-weather framework allocates investments across economic regimes:

**Four economic conditions:**

1. **Rising inflation** - Stocks and commodities rise, bonds fall
2. **Falling inflation (growth)** - Stocks and bonds rise, commodities fall
3. **Rising deflation (slowdown)** - Bonds and commodities rise, stocks fall
4. **Falling deflation (recovery)** - Stocks rise, bonds fall moderately

Traditional 60/40 portfolio fails in scenario 3 (falling inflation, rising deflation):
- Stocks down 20-30%
- Bonds down 5% (modest benefit)
- Combined: 13-14% loss

All-weather portfolio designed for scenario 3:
- Stocks down 20-30%
- Bonds up 5-10%
- Commodities up 3-5%
- Combined: -2% to +3% (minimal loss, portfolio stability)

## Rebalancing Strategy for All-Weather

### Quarterly Rebalancing Triggers

When allocations drift >5%, rebalance:

```
Target: 25% TLT, 25% AGG, 20% LQD, 20% Stocks, 10% HYG
Current: 22% TLT, 27% AGG, 19% LQD, 23% Stocks, 9% HYG

Drift detected: AGG +2%, TLT -3%, Stocks +3%
Action: Sell $15,000 AGG, buy $15,000 TLT → reset to target
```

### Scenario-Aware Rebalancing

If you expect inflation, shift toward short-duration:

```
Rising inflation expected (rates likely up):
- Reduce TLT from 25% to 20%
- Increase VGSH from 10% to 15%
This reduces duration loss when rates rise
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is all-weather portfolio too complicated?**
A: Simple all-weather: 50% stocks, 40% bonds, 10% cash. This 3-fund approach works for most.

**Q: Should I use individual bonds or bond funds?**
A: Bond funds simpler for diversification. Ladders better for $250k+ if you want control.

**Q: What's the expected return for all-weather?**
A: 5-6% depending on starting yields. Lower than stocks alone (8-10%) but more stable.

**Q: Do I need TLT for all-weather?**
A: Not essential, but recommended 10-20%. TLT provides best protection in stock crashes.

**Q: How often should I rebalance?**
A: Quarterly or when 5% drift occurs. More frequent rebalancing captures mean reversion.

**Q: Can all-weather portfolio achieve 8% returns?**
A: Only with favorable rate environment. Conservative estimate: 5-6%. Growth estimate: 6-8%.
