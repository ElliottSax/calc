# How to Calculate Sortino Ratio for Treasury Bonds in 2026

Treasury bonds provide safety but still carry interest rate risk. The Sortino Ratio reveals which Treasury positions protect capital most effectively during market stress. This guide teaches you to calculate Sortino for Treasuries and use it to build resilient fixed-income portfolios.

## Understanding Treasury Bond Volatility

Treasury bonds exhibit lower volatility than most securities, but interest rate movements create measurable downside risk. Rising rates compress prices; falling rates inflate them. The Sortino Ratio specifically measures negative price movements without penalizing rate declines that boost future returns.

**The Formula:**

**Sortino Ratio = (Return - Target Return) / Downside Deviation**

For Treasury investors, the target return typically matches the current 10-year yield (approximately 4.5% in 2026).

## Treasury Maturity and Duration Effects

Different Treasury maturities show distinct volatility profiles:

**3-month T-bills:**
- Return volatility: minimal
- Downside deviation: negligible
- Sortino ratio: Not meaningful

- Sortino ratio: (4.7% - 4.5%) / 5.2% = 0.04

## Calculating Downside Deviation for Treasuries

Treasury bond monthly returns tracking:

Jan: 0.3%, Feb: -0.8%, Mar: 1.2%, Apr: 0.6%, May: -1.4%, Jun: 0.9%, Jul: 0.1%, Aug: -0.6%, Sep: 1.1%, Oct: 0.4%, Nov: 0.8%, Dec: 0.7%

Target return: 4.5% / 12 = 0.375% monthly

Shortfalls (below 0.375%):
- Jan: none
- Feb: -0.8% (shortfall 1.175%)
- Mar: none
- Apr: none
- May: -1.4% (shortfall 1.775%)
- Jun: none
- Jul: 0.1% (shortfall 0.275%)
- Aug: -0.6% (shortfall 0.975%)
- Sep: none
- Oct: none
- Nov: none
- Dec: none

Squared shortfalls: 1.381 + 3.150 + 0.076 + 0.951 = 5.558
Average: 5.558 / 12 = 0.463
Downside deviation: √0.463 = 0.680% monthly

Annualized: 0.680% × √12 = 2.36%

## Rising Rate Environment Sortino Impact

In rising-rate scenarios, Treasury Sortino deteriorates:

**Scenario: Rates rise 100 basis points**

10-year bond price decline: approximately 8%
Monthly impact: -0.67% average
New downside deviation: 4.2% (30% higher)
New annual return: 3.9% (after rate-induced losses)
New Sortino: (3.9% - 4.5%) / 4.2% = -0.14

Deteriorating Sortino signals rising interest rate risk.

## Falling Rate Environment Sortino Impact

Improving Sortino signals falling interest rate opportunities.

## FAQ

**Q: Why do Treasury Sortino ratios often turn negative?**
A: Current Treasury yields (4.5%) match historical norms. Downside deviation often equals or exceeds return above target, producing neutral or slightly negative Sortino.

**Q: Should I avoid Treasuries with negative Sortino?**
A: No. Negative Sortino simply indicates returns near or below target. Treasuries serve portfolio diversification and capital preservation roles beyond return optimization.

**Q: How does duration affect Treasury Sortino?**
A: Higher duration increases downside deviation substantially, depressing Sortino. Longer-term Treasuries require significant return premiums to justify duration risk.

**Q: Can I use Treasury Sortino to time interest rate movements?**
A: Partially. Improving Sortino suggests falling-rate scenarios (favorable for longer Treasuries). Deteriorating Sortino signals rising-rate risk.

**Q: What Treasury duration maximizes Sortino?**
A: Currently, 5-7 year Treasuries show slight positive Sortino while minimizing downside deviation. Intermediate-duration provides optimal risk-adjusted positioning.
