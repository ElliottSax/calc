## Strategic Allocation Framework

### Age-Based Approach
Simple rule: Age + Bonds % = Conservative allocation

- Age 25: 25% bonds, 75% stocks
- Age 35: 35% bonds, 65% stocks
- Age 45: 45% bonds, 55% stocks
- Age 55: 55% bonds, 45% stocks

### Diversification Benefits

Combining uncorrelated assets reduces overall portfolio volatility:
- Single stock volatility: 30%+
- 10-stock portfolio: 20%
- 100-stock portfolio (VTI): 15.8%
- 60/40 stocks/bonds: 9.2%

## Implementation Guide

### Step 1: Set Objectives
- Define retirement income target

- VTI: 70-80% of equity allocation (broad diversification)

### Step 4: Annual Rebalancing

- Check quarterly (no action)

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy

When position declines:
1. Sell at loss (realize loss)
2. Buy similar position (maintain exposure)
3. After 31 days, rebuy original (if desired)
4. Use loss against gains/income

**Example**:
- VTI declines \$100 → \$85 (-\$15)
- Sell, buy VOO (same market)
- Use \$15 loss against gains
- Tax savings (37% bracket): \$5.55
- Over 30 years: adds 0.3-0.5% annual return

## Risk Management

### Market Cycle Strategies

**Bull Market (Rising prices)**:
- Maintain allocation
- Don't chase winners
- Rebalance by buying bonds
- Tax-gain harvest winners

**Bear Market (Declining prices)**:
- Maintain allocation
- Use contributions to buy dips
- Tax-loss harvest bottoms
- View crashes as opportunities

**Sideways Market (Volatility)**:
- Rebalance when >5% drift
- Dollar-cost average steadily
- Tax-loss harvest volatility
- Stay focused long-term

## Comparison: Active vs. Passive

### Passive Investing
- Benefits: Low fees (0.03-0.10%), consistent returns, tax-efficient
- Returns: 8-10% historically
- Time required: 2-4 hours annually
- Best for: Most investors

### Active Investing
- Benefits: Potential outperformance (0.5-2%)
- Returns: 6-8% average (underperforms passive due to fees)
- Time required: 10-20 hours monthly
- Best for: <20% of investors (those with expertise/time)

**Data**: 85-90% of active managers underperform passive benchmarks after fees.

## FAQ

**Q: What's the best allocation?**
A: One matching your age, time horizon, and risk tolerance. Use age + bonds % rule.

**Q: Should I try market timing?**
A: No. Data shows 90% of investors underperform through timing.
- Missing best 10 days over 34 years cuts returns ~50%
- Buy and hold beats active trading

**Q: How much monthly?**
A: Save 20% of gross income:
- \$60K salary = \$12K annually (\$1K/month)
- Allocate across 401k/IRA/HSA/taxable

**Q: When to increase stock allocation?**
A: Only when:
- Career income rises
- Time horizon extends
- Risk tolerance increases
- NOT when market rising

**Q: Should I hold individual stocks?**
A: For most, no. Complex with minimal benefits.
- 85-90% broad market (VTI)
- 10-15% dividend stocks max (KO, JNJ, PG)
- Keep speculation <5% of portfolio

**Q: How often to review?**
A: Quarterly check-in (no trades), annual rebalance, 5-year strategic review.
