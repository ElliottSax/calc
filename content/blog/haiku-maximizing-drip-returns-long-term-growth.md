## Common Mistakes in Long-Term DRIPs

### Mistake 1: Stopping Contributions in Down Years
- Market drops 20%
- Investor panics, reduces contributions
- Misses buying opportunity
- Recovery is slower

**Solution:** Maintain consistent contributions regardless of market. Down markets mean buying at lower prices.

### Mistake 2: Selling During Downturns
- Portfolio down 25%
- Investor sells to "stop the bleeding"
- Locks in losses
- Misses recovery

**Solution:** DRIPs force buy discipline. Let automatic reinvestment continue. History shows 100% recovery within 3-5 years.

### Mistake 3: Chasing Performance
- One sector outperforms
- Investor chases hot sector
- Misses diversification benefits
- Eventually overexposed when sector corrects

**Solution:** Maintain target allocation. Rebalance to stay disciplined.

### Mistake 4: Failing to Automate
- Good intentions to contribute and reinvest
- Life gets busy, contributions lapse
- Missing compounding windows
- Motivation wanes during bear markets

**Solution:** Automate everything. Set up automatic contributions on paycheck day. Enable automatic DRIPs.

## FAQs

**Q: What's the minimum time horizon for DRIP wealth building?**
A: 10+ years shows meaningful results. 20+ years demonstrates extraordinary power. 30+ years creates multi-million-dollar wealth for most people.

**Q: Should I reinvest all returns or some?**
A: Reinvest all for maximum long-term wealth. Only stop reinvesting when you need income for living expenses.

**Q: Which is better: individual stocks or ETF DRIPs?**
A: ETF DRIPs offer better diversification for most investors. Individual stock DRIPs work if you hold 10+ positions.

**Q: Can I stop a DRIP and resume later?**
A: Yes, but avoid it. Stopping breaks momentum. Maintain DRIP through your entire wealth-building phase.

**Q: What if a company cuts its dividend?**
A: Impact depends on portfolio diversification. Single-position cut = 10% impact. Diversified portfolio = <1% impact.

**Q: Should I use leverage (margin) for DRIPs?**
A: No. Margin creates risk that contradicts long-term wealth building. Stick to 100% capital.

**Q: How do I calculate my personal DRIP potential?**
A: Use this formula: [(Monthly Contribution × 12) × ((1+r)^n - 1) / r] + [Initial × (1+r)^n]

**Q: When should I stop DRIPing and start withdrawing?**
A: When portfolio generates sufficient income for your needs. Transition from 100% reinvestment to 50% withdrawal over 3-5 years.
