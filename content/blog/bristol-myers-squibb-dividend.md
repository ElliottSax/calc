# Bristol Myers Squibb Dividend: Healthcare Income Analysis

Bristol Myers Squibb Company (BMY) offers one of the highest dividend yields in large-cap pharmaceuticals at 4.2%, backed by a diversified portfolio of oncology, immunology, and cardiovascular drugs. With 15 consecutive years of dividend increases and recent strategic acquisitions positioning the company for long-term growth, BMY presents a compelling case for income-focused healthcare investors.

## Key Takeaways

- BMY yields 4.0-4.4%, significantly above pharma peers and the S&P 500
- 15 consecutive years of dividend increases demonstrate commitment to shareholders
- Payout ratio of 52-58% balances income with R&D investment needs
- Recent acquisitions (Celgene, MyoKardia, Mirati) strengthen pipeline
- Oncology focus (60% of revenue) provides growth runway through 2030+

## Dividend History (2010-2026)

**2010**: $1.48 annual
**2014**: $1.64 annual
**2018**: $1.80 annual
**2022**: $2.00 annual
**2024**: $2.40 annual
**2026**: $2.60 annual

**Metrics**:
- 16-Year CAGR: 3.7%
- 5-Year CAGR: 5.4%
- Yield: 4.2%
- Payout: 55%

## Core Business Strengths

**Revenue**: $48 billion
**Operating Margin**: 25%
**Free Cash Flow**: $10-12 billion

**Key Products**:
1. Eliquis (anticoagulant): $12B revenue
2. Opdivo (oncology): $9B revenue
3. Revlimid (oncology): $6B (declining)
4. Orencia (immunology): $4B revenue

## Future Outlook (2027-2030)

**Base Case** (3-4% dividend growth):
- 2027: $2.70/share
- 2030: $3.00/share
- Yield on cost: 4.8%+ for 2026 buyers

## Comparison to Pharma Peers

**BMY**: 4.2% yield, 3-4% growth

**PFE**: 5.8% yield, 0-2% growth
**MRK**: 2.6% yield, 5-7% growth

BMY offers second-highest yield with moderate growth.

## Investment Strategy

Best suited for:
- Income-focused investors
- Healthcare sector allocation
- Defensive dividend portfolios
- Retirees needing current income

## Risks

- Revlimid patent expiry impact
- Pipeline execution risk
- Acquisition integration challenges
- Pricing pressure from government

## Conclusion

Bristol Myers Squibb delivers immediate income (4.2% yield) with moderate growth prospects (3-5% annually). The high current yield makes it attractive for retirees and income-focused investors willing to accept slower dividend growth. With a diversified product portfolio and strengthened pipeline from acquisitions, BMY should sustain dividends through patent cliffs and industry headwinds.
