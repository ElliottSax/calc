## Beyond Sharpe: The Downside Protection Advantage of Sortino Ratio

While Sharpe Ratio treats all volatility equally, Sortino Ratio distinguishes between beneficial upside volatility and harmful downside losses. For Tax-Efficient portfolios, this distinction proves critical.

## Understanding Sortino Ratio

Sortino Ratio measures excess return per unit of downside volatility:

$$	ext{Sortino Ratio} = rac{	ext{Portfolio Return} - 	ext{Risk-Free Rate}}{	ext{Downside Deviation}}$$

Downside deviation only counts periods falling below the target return.

## Sharpe vs. Sortino: A Critical Distinction

| Volatility Type | Sharpe | Sortino |
|-----------------|--------|---------|
| Upside (exceeds target) | Penalty | Ignored |
| Downside (below target) | Penalty | Penalty |

A portfolio with consistent gains plus rare crashes shows moderate Sharpe but low Sortino. A steady-boring-gains portfolio shows lower Sharpe but higher Sortino.

## Calculating Sortino Ratio

### Step 1: Identify Target Return
For Tax-Efficient portfolios, use 0% (no losses) or risk-free rate (4.2%).

### Step 2: Calculate Downside Deviation
Using monthly returns, calculate variance only for periods below target.

### Example
Monthly returns: Jan +0.8%, Feb +0.6%, Mar -1.2%, Apr +1.1%, May -0.3%, Jun +0.9%

With 0% target:
- Downside variance: [(-1.2)² + (-0.3)²] / 12 = 0.1275
- Downside deviation: 3.57% (annualized)
- Annual return: 1.9%
- Sortino: (1.9% - 4.2%) / 3.57% = -0.64

## Sortino Limitations

### 1. Requires Long Data
24+ months needed for meaningful calculations.

### 2. Assumes Consistent Distribution
Real downside events cluster. Crisis periods feature correlated drawdowns.

### 3. Target Dependency
Different targets alter Sortino significantly.

### 4. Ignores Opportunity Cost
Low-volatility portfolio with 3% returns may fail retirement goals.

## FAQ: Sortino Ratio

**Q1: Should Sortino be my primary metric?**
A: Use both Sharpe and Sortino for complete perspective.

**Q2: What Sortino is excellent for Tax-Efficient?**
A: Target 1.20-1.40. Above 1.40 indicates exceptional downside protection.

**Q3: How does dividend income affect Sortino?**
A: Positive. Dividends reduce downside volatility while maintaining returns.

**Q4: Can I achieve high Sortino without sacrificing returns?**
A: Yes, through diversification and defensive positioning. Well-constructed Tax-Efficient achieves 7-8% returns with Sortino >1.20.

**Q5: Should young investors focus on Sortino or growth?**
A: Even young investors benefit through behavioral benefits. Avoiding 30%+ drawdowns enables staying invested.
