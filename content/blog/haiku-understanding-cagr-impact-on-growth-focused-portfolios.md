## CAGR in Growth Portfolios

Growth portfolios target 8-12% CAGR through 70-90% equities and minimal bonds. Small improvements in CAGR create enormous wealth differences over career accumulation phases.

The difference between 8% and 10% CAGR is trivial annually but represents 60%+ difference over 30 years for young investors.

## Why CAGR Matters for Growth Investors

**Real Example**: \$250,000 portfolio over 30-year career accumulation (age 25-55)

**Growth Portfolio A** (8% CAGR):
- Year 10: \$540,747
- Year 20: \$1,172,889
- Year 30: \$2,543,648
- **Total gain: \$2,293,648**

**Growth Portfolio B** (10% CAGR):
- Year 10: \$648,394
- Year 20: \$1,678,644
- Year 30: \$4,437,053
- **Total gain: \$4,187,053**

**Difference**: \$1,893,405 additional wealth (75% more return)

The 30-year timeframe amplifies CAGR impact exponentially. This is why young investors should pursue maximum growth—time multiplies even small CAGR differences.

## The Volatility Cost of Higher CAGR

Higher growth CAGR comes with volatility. Understanding this is crucial for sustaining discipline.

| Portfolio | CAGR | Typical Max Drawdown | Recovery Time |
|-----------|-----|---|---|
| Conservative | 5.5% | -15% | 12 months |
| Balanced | 7% | -20% | 18 months |
| Growth | 9% | -30% | 24 months |
| Aggressive | 11% | -40% | 24-36 months |

Growth-focused investors must psychologically prepare for 20-30% annual declines approximately every 3-5 years. If these declines cause you to abandon your strategy, your actual CAGR collapses below target.

## Sustaining Growth CAGR Through Volatility

The key to achieving 9%+ CAGR is maintaining discipline during downturns:

**During 2022 Market Decline** (-18% for VOO):
- **Weak investor**: Sells everything after 15% decline, locks in loss
- Actual return over next 10 years: 0-2% (due to being out of market)

- **Disciplined investor**: Harvests losses, rebalances, continues contributions
- Actual return over next 10 years: 8-10% (captures recovery)
- **Long-term return difference: 2-3% CAGR** (10-year impact: \$200,000+ on \$500,000 portfolio)

This discipline multiplier is invisible in calm markets but becomes critical during crises.

## CAGR Calculation for Growth Portfolios

**Formula**:
```
CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1 / Years) - 1
```

**Example**: \$250,000 grows to \$650,000 over 10 years

```
CAGR = (650,000 / 250,000)^(1/10) - 1
CAGR = (2.6)^(0.1) - 1
CAGR = 1.0996 - 1
CAGR = 9.96% (approximately 10%)
```

## FAQ

**Q: Is 10% CAGR realistic for growth portfolios?**
A: Yes, with 70-80% stocks. Markets have returned 10%+ historically over extended periods.

**Q: Should I target 12%+ CAGR?**
A: Not unless you can tolerate -40%+ drawdowns without panic selling. Higher CAGR requires higher leverage/concentration—risky.

**Q: What if I achieve only 8% CAGR?**
A: Still excellent. Better safe 8% than risky 12% that you can't psychologically maintain.

**Q: Does higher CAGR require more effort?**
A: Not necessarily. Discipline (holding through volatility) matters more than skill (stock-picking).

**Q: How can I improve growth CAGR from 8% to 9%?**
A: Tax harvesting (+0.5%), quality positioning (+0.3%), rebalancing discipline (+0.2%). Total: +1% CAGR. Effort: Minimal (quarterly reviews).
