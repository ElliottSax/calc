## The Math of Fast Wealth Building: It’s All About Compounding and Cash Flow

Wealth grows through two primary engines: **compound returns** and **consistent cash flow**. The faster you deploy capital and reinvest returns, the quicker your net worth accelerates.

### The Compound Growth Formula

The future value (FV) of an investment is calculated as:

```
FV = P × (1 + r)^t + [C × ((1 + r)^t - 1) / r]
```

Where:
- `P` = initial principal
- `r` = annual rate of return
- `t` = time in years
- `C` = annual contribution

**Example**: You invest $20,000 today (P), add $10,000 per year (C), and earn 9% annually (r) over 15 years (t):

```
FV = 20,000 × (1.09)^15 + [10,000 × ((1.09)^15 - 1) / 0.09]
FV = 20,000 × 3.642 + [10,000 × (3.642 - 1) / 0.09]
FV = 72,840 + [10,000 × 29.36]
FV = 72,840 + 293,600 = **$366,440**
```

That’s $366,440 from $170,000 in total contributions — a 115% return on capital, driven by compounding.

To build wealth *fast*, you must maximize:
1. **Contribution rate (C)**
2. **Rate of return (r)**
3. **Time (t)** — starting early is non-negotiable

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q: Can I build wealth fast without a high income?
**A**: Yes — if your savings rate is high. A teacher earning $55,000 who saves $15,000/year (27%) will build wealth faster than a lawyer earning $200,000 who saves $20,000 (10%).

### Q: Is real estate faster than stocks?
**A**: Not necessarily. Leveraged real estate *can* accelerate returns, but it’s illiquid, management-heavy, and concentrated. VTI gives instant diversification with 0 effort. For most people, low-cost equities are faster and safer.

### Q: How much do I need to start?
**A**: $100. Open a brokerage account, buy one share of VTI (~$250) or fractional shares. Start now. A 25-year-old who invests $200/month at 9% has **$880,000 by 65**.

### Q: Should I pay off debt first?
**A**: Depends on interest rate.
- Student loan at 3%? Invest while paying minimum.
- Credit card at 22%? Pay it off first — that’s a guaranteed 22% return.

### Q: How do taxes affect fast wealth building?
**A**: Use tax-advantaged accounts:
- 401(k): $23,000/year (2024)
- IRA: $7,000/year
- HSA (if eligible): $4,150 (self) / $8,300 (family)

Investing $30,000/year in tax-sheltered accounts turbocharges growth.

### Q: What if the market crashes?
**A**: Buy more. During the 2008–09 crash, VTI dropped 51%. Investors who kept contributing averaged **15%+ returns** from 2009–2019. Volatility is your friend if you’re accumulating.
