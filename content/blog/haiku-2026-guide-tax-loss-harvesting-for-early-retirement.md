## Introduction

Tax-loss harvesting—systematically selling positions at losses, immediately repurchasing similar assets, and using the realized losses to offset capital gains and income—can reduce tax burdens by $5,000-$15,000+ annually for early retirees building toward financial independence.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Mathematics

### Annual Tax Savings Potential

Formula: Annual tax loss potential = Portfolio volatility × Average position size × Capital gains tax rate

Example: $500,000 portfolio with 12% volatility experiences ~$60,000 peak-to-trough movements annually

If harvesting $20,000-$30,000 in losses annually:
- Tax savings: $20,000 × 24% = $4,800/year
- Over 20 years: $96,000+ total tax savings

## Systematic Quarterly Harvesting Schedule

**Q1 (January-March):**
- Monitor year-end positions for losses
- Harvest losses immediately in January
- Immediately repurchase similar assets
- Tax benefit: Offset Q1 dividend income

**Q2 (April-June):**
- Monitor spring earnings season
- Harvest losses in beaten-down positions
- Tax benefit: Offset Q2 dividend income

**Q3 (July-September):**
- Largest harvest opportunity (summer volatility)
- Harvest meaningful losses (>$5,000)
- Tax benefit: Offset Q3 dividend income

**Q4 (October-December):**
- Final harvest window
- Complete annual tax planning
- Use losses to offset full-year capital gains

## Wash Sale Avoidance for Early Retirees

**Rule:** Cannot repurchase "substantially identical" security within 30 days

**Compliant harvesting:**
- Sell VOO (S&P 500 large-cap)
- Immediately buy VTI (Total market, includes small-cap)
- NOT substantially identical ✓
- Can re-transition to VOO after 31+ days

## FAQ

Q: How much tax-loss harvesting is realistic annually?
A: For $500k portfolios: $15,000-$25,000 annually
For $1M portfolios: $25,000-$50,000 annually
For $5M portfolios: $50,000-$100,000+ annually

Larger volatility in positions enables larger harvests.

Q: Should I harvest losses constantly or wait for major downturns?
A: Both:
- Constant harvesting: Capture small losses monthly (avoid big losses)
- Downturn harvesting: Major harvests in 15-30% corrections

Best approach: Continuous + opportunistic.

Q: Can I harvest losses in my 401k?
A: No. Tax-loss harvesting only available in taxable accounts. Keep low-yield, high-volatility positions in taxable accounts where harvesting is possible.
