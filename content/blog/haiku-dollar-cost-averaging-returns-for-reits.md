## Calculating Dollar-Cost Averaging Returns for REITs

Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) with real estate investment trusts reduces timing risk in property-focused investments. Consistent monthly or quarterly investments smooth volatility while building positions in this income-generating asset class.

## The DCA Concept

Invest fixed amounts regularly regardless of REIT prices. Market timing becomes irrelevant; discipline matters more.

## Worked Example: DCA for REITs

Invest $500 monthly for 6 months:

| Month | Price | Investment | Shares |
|-------|-------|------------|--------|
| Jan | $50 | $500 | 10.00 |
| Feb | $48 | $500 | 10.42 |
| Mar | $52 | $500 | 9.62 |
| Apr | $51 | $500 | 9.80 |
| May | $49 | $500 | 10.20 |
| Jun | $51 | $500 | 9.80 |
| **Total** | **Avg: $50.17** | **$3,000** | **59.84** |

**Average Cost = $50.11**

## Return Calculation

If current price is $55:

- Position Value: 59.84 x $55 = $3,291.20
- Cost: $3,000
- Gain: $291.20
- Return: **9.7%**

Plus accumulated REIT distributions (typically monthly).

## REIT-Specific DCA Advantages

REITs mandate 90% income distribution, making regular DCA particularly powerful for income building:

- Steady dividend distributions during accumulation
- Reinvested distributions compound returns
- Build positions in property portfolios systematically
- Reduce impact of REIT price swings

## FAQ

**Q: Are REITs good for DCA?**
A: Yes, their high distributions and volatility make DCA particularly effective.

**Q: How do REIT distributions affect DCA?**
A: Reinvest distributions to enhance compounding within your DCA program.

**Q: Should I track DCA returns including dividends?**
A: Yes, total return = price appreciation + reinvested distributions.

**Q: How long should DCA continue?**
A: 3-5+ years to smooth REIT price cycles.

**Q: Can I use DCA to build a REIT ladder?**
A: Yes, DCA can fund progressive positions across different property types.

## Conclusion

Dollar-cost averaging works exceptionally well with REITs given their monthly distributions and periodic volatility. Systematic investment builds wealth through compounding of both price appreciation and reinvested distributions.
