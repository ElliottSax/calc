## Tax-Loss Harvesting Fundamentals

Tax-loss harvesting sells securities at losses to offset capital gains and ordinary income. Municipal bond portfolios experience frequent harvesting opportunities given interest rate volatility.

When interest rates rise, bond prices fall, creating tax-loss harvesting opportunities. Harvesting these losses saves taxes while maintaining portfolio positioning.

## Step-by-Step Harvesting Calculator

**Scenario: $100,000 VTEB position with loss**

**Step 1: Identify unrealized loss**

- Purchase price: $50,000 (1,000 shares at $50)
- Current price: $48 per share
- Current value: $48,000
- Unrealized loss: $2,000

**Step 2: Calculate tax savings**

Tax savings formula: Loss × Marginal Tax Rate

For investor with 37% federal + 3.8% NIIT = 40.8% combined rate:
- Tax savings: $2,000 × 40.8% = $816

**Step 3: Identify replacement security**

Must not violate wash-sale rule. Can't repurchase VTEB within 30 days.

Suitable alternatives:
- BND (taxable municipal equivalent)
- Municipal bond CEF with similar duration
- Different municipal ETF (MUB)

**Step 4: Execute harvest and replacement**

- Sell VTEB at loss: Realize -$2,000 loss
- Buy replacement: Same $48,000
- Tax savings: $816
- Net cost: $2,000 - $816 = $1,184

## Wash-Sale Rule Compliance

Tax-loss harvesting requires careful attention to wash-sale rules.

**Wash-sale rule timeline:**

Cannot rebuy same security or "substantially identical" within:
- 30 days BEFORE sale
- Day of sale
- 30 days AFTER sale

Total 61-day period of restriction.

**Example compliance:**

- November 15: Sell VTEB at loss
- December 14: Cannot repurchase VTEB (within 30 days)
- December 15: Can repurchase VTEB (exactly 31 days later)

Suitable replacement bonds during waiting period:
- BND (taxable municipal-equivalent)
- VMLX (long-term municipals)
- MUB (different municipal ETF)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Can I harvest losses with municipal bonds indefinitely?**
A: Yes, if bonds decline. Extended low-rate environments limit opportunities; rising rate environments create abundant harvesting.

**Q2: What's the best replacement security for harvested VTEB?**
A: BND (taxable municipal equivalent) or different municipal ETF for simplicity and similarity.

**Q3: If I harvest a loss, can I buy back same bond immediately?**
A: Not same bond (wash-sale rule). Can buy similar municipal bond, then repurchase original after 31 days.

**Q4: How much tax savings should I expect annually?**
A: Depends on rate environment. Normal: $1,000-3,000. Bear markets: $5,000-10,000+.

**Q5: Should I harvest if I'm already in a loss position?**
A: Only if new security maintains portfolio goals. Don't harvest just for tax savings; ensure strategic fit.
