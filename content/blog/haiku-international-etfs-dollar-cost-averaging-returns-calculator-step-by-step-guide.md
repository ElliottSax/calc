## Dollar-Cost Averaging Overview

Dollar-cost averaging involves investing fixed amounts at regular intervals regardless of price. This strategy reduces timing risk and removes emotion from investing. International ETFs like VXUS benefit from DCA given currency and geopolitical volatility.

## DCA Mathematics

Formula: Total Shares = Sum(Monthly Investment / Share Price)

**Example: $1,000 monthly into VXUS for 12 months**

| Month | Share Price | Investment | Shares Purchased | Total Shares |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | $95 | $1,000 | 10.53 | 10.53 |
| 2 | $92 | $1,000 | 10.87 | 21.40 |
| 3 | $98 | $1,000 | 10.20 | 31.60 |
| 4 | $100 | $1,000 | 10.00 | 41.60 |
| 6 | $88 | $1,000 | 11.36 | 63.48 |
| 12 | $105 | $1,000 | 9.52 | 122.65 |

Total invested: $12,000

Average cost: $97.84 per share
Current value at $105: $12,878
Gain: $878 (7.3% over one year)

**Lump Sum ($12,000 at start):**
- Shares purchased: 126.32 (at $95)

- Gain: $1,264 (10.5%)

Lump sum outperforms DCA in rising markets, but DCA provides psychological discipline during volatility.

## Impact of Sequence on DCA

**Scenario A: Markets constantly rising**
- Average cost lower
- Final value: Higher
- Advantage: Lump sum wins

**Scenario B: Markets declining then recovering**
- Average cost: Lower (forced buying at lows)
- Final value: Similar or better
- Advantage: DCA wins

**Scenario C: Markets volatile**
- Average cost: Moderate
- Final value: Middle ground
- Advantage: DCA provides stability

## DCA Benefits

Key advantages:

1. Removes timing risk by averaging cost
2. Forces discipline during downturns
3. Prevents panic selling
4. Smooths volatility impact
5. Psychologically easier than lump sum

## DCA Disadvantages

Key drawbacks:

1. Theoretically inferior in rising markets
2. Delayed capital deployment
3. Repeated purchases generate fees
4. Misses best days
5. More purchases in bear markets

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Should I DCA or lump sum invest?**
A: Historical data favors lump sum 2/3 of the time. Use DCA if it maintains discipline.

**Q2: Does DCA work with ETFs?**
A: Yes. ETFs ideal for DCA due to low costs and liquidity.

**Q3: What's optimal DCA frequency?**
A: Monthly DCA balances fees and frequency. Weekly excessive; quarterly insufficient.

**Q4: Should I DCA across multiple ETFs?**
A: Yes. Diversifying DCA across country/sector ETFs provides geographic balance.

**Q5: Does DCA help with currency risk?**
A: Partially. Regular purchases average currency rates over time but don't eliminate risk.
