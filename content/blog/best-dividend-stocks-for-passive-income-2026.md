## Analysis
When evaluating dividend stocks, it's essential to consider a combination of factors, including dividend yield, payout ratio, and historical growth rate. The dividend yield represents the annual dividend payment per share as a percentage of the stock's current price. The payout ratio, calculated by dividing the annual dividend payment by the company's earnings per share, helps determine the sustainability of the dividend. A lower payout ratio generally indicates a more sustainable dividend. The historical growth rate, measured by the compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of the dividend payment over a specified period, provides insight into the company's ability to increase its dividend over time.

* **Procter & Gamble (PG)**, offering a yield of 2.64% and a payout ratio of 59.15%. PG has a long history of dividend growth, with a 5-year CAGR of 4.21%.

## Comparison
When comparing these dividend stocks, it's essential to consider your individual investment goals and risk tolerance. For example, if you prioritize a high yield, ExxonMobil (XOM) and Realty Income (O) may be more attractive options. In addition to individual stock selection, it's essential to consider the overall diversification of your portfolio. A well-diversified dividend portfolio should include a mix of stocks from various sectors, such as consumer staples, energy, healthcare, and real estate. This approach can help mitigate risk and increase potential long-term returns.

## Strategy
To build a successful dividend portfolio, consider the following strategies:

1. **Dollar-cost averaging**: Invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals, regardless of the market's performance, to reduce timing risks and avoid emotional decision-making.
2. **Dividend reinvestment**: Automatically reinvest dividend payments to take advantage of compounding and potentially increase your portfolio's growth rate over time.
3. **Regular portfolio rebalancing**: Periodically review your portfolio and rebalance it to maintain your target asset allocation, ensuring that your investments remain aligned with your goals and risk tolerance.
4. **Tax-efficient investing**: Consider the tax implications of your investments and aim to minimize tax liabilities by holding tax-efficient investments, such as index funds or tax-loss harvesting.

By implementing these strategies and selecting a diversified portfolio of high-quality dividend stocks, you can create a passive income stream that helps you achieve your long-term financial goals.
