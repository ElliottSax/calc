# How to Calculate Withdrawal Rate for Value Stocks in 2026

Determining sustainable withdrawal rates for value stock portfolios proves essential for income-focused retirees. Value stocks deliver both dividends and steady appreciation, creating unique withdrawal dynamics. This guide teaches you to calculate appropriate withdrawal rates for value-stock-centric portfolios.

## Understanding Withdrawal Rate Basics

The 4% rule provides a foundation, but value portfolios warrant customization.

**Formula:**
Annual Withdrawal = Portfolio Value × Withdrawal Rate

**Example with value stocks:**
- Portfolio: $1,000,000 in value stocks
- 3.8% withdrawal rate: $38,000 annually
- Year 2: $38,000 × 1.02 inflation = $38,760

## Why Value Stock Withdrawal Rates Differ

Value portfolios show specific characteristics:

1. **Higher dividend yields**: 3.5-4.5% average
2. **Lower price volatility**: Steadier portfolio values
3. **Mature companies**: Predictable fundamentals
4. **Lower growth expectations**: Modest price appreciation

These factors suggest slightly lower withdrawal rates than all-stock portfolios.

## Calculating Your Value Stock Withdrawal Rate

**Step 1: Timeline Assessment**

- Dividend yields above 3.8%: Add 0.2%

## FAQ

**Q: Is 3.8% withdrawal rate safe for value stock portfolios?**

**Q: Should I harvest only dividends or also realize capital gains?**
A: Pure dividend harvesting works for 1-10 years if portfolio is well-sized. Beyond that, selective capital gains realization may become necessary.

**Q: How do dividend cuts affect my withdrawal plan?**
A: Dividend cuts reduce withdrawal capacity. Monitor holdings quarterly and replace underperformers immediately to maintain withdrawal sustainability.

**Q: Can dividend growth outpace inflation indefinitely?**
A: Not indefinitely, but typically for 20-30 years. Dividend aristocrats show 5-7% growth historically; sustainability requires monitoring.

**Q: How often should I adjust withdrawals?**
A: Annually based on inflation and portfolio performance. Quarterly monitoring ensures early detection of sustainability threats.
