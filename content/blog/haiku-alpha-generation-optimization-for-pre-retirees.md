## Introduction

Pre-retirees face a unique investment challenge: they have enough time to pursue active alpha generation strategies, but not enough to recover from major losses. The optimal approach combines selective active management with a defensive core portfolio. ## Understanding Alpha in the Pre-Retirement Context

Alpha represents returns above a benchmark index, typically the S&P 500 or a target-date fund equivalent. For pre-retirees (typically 5-10 years from retirement), alpha generation becomes strategically important.

**Alpha Formula:**
```
Alpha = (Portfolio Return - Risk-Free Rate) - Beta × (Benchmark Return - Risk-Free Rate)
```

For a pre-retiree with a $500,000 portfolio generating 9% annualized returns while the S&P 500 averages 10% but with higher volatility:

- Portfolio Return: 9%
- Risk-Free Rate (10-year Treasury): 4.5%
- S&P 500 Return: 10%
- Portfolio Beta: 0.85 (lower volatility)

**Calculation:**
```
Alpha = (9% - 4.5%) - 0.85 × (10% - 4.5%)
Alpha = 4.5% - 0.85 × 5.5%
Alpha = 4.5% - 4.675%
Alpha = -0.175%
```

This shows that even slightly underperforming the index with lower risk can be acceptable for pre-retirees since lower volatility reduces sequence-of-returns risk near retirement.

## Options Income Strategy

Covered calls and cash-secured puts generate 2-4% annual income alpha with limited downside risk for pre-retirees.

**Conservative Covered Call Example:**

Position: 100 shares of VOO at $500/share = $50,000

Selling monthly covered calls at 2% out-of-the-money strike:
```
Call Premium: $2.50 per share × 100 shares = $250/month
Annual Premium: $250 × 12 = $3,000 (6% annualized)

VOO Dividend Yield: 1.3% annually = $650/year

Total Income: $3,000 + $650 = $3,650
Total Return on $50,000: 7.3% annually (excluding capital appreciation)
```

**Cash-Secured Put Strategy:**

Selling monthly puts on high-quality dividend stocks at 5% below market price:

```
Target Stock: SCHD (Schwab Dividend Appreciation ETF) at $95/share
Strike Price: $90.25 (5% below market)
Put Premium: $1.75 × 100 shares = $175/month
Annual Premium: $175 × 12 = $2,100

Annualized Yield: 2,100 / 9,025 = 23.3% (if assigned throughout year)
More realistic: 5-8% annualized due to assignment probability
```

**Volatility Control Calculation:**

Assuming portfolio volatility at 10% with 70/30 stocks/bonds:
```
Portfolio Volatility = √[(0.70)²(0.18)² + (0.30)²(0.05)² + 2(0.70)(0.30)(0.18)(0.05)(0.30)]
```

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is 2-3% annual alpha realistic for individual investors?**
A: Yes, but requires discipline. Studies show active investors using systematic approaches (dividend growth, value screening, sector rotation) can achieve 1.5-3% after fees. The key is consistency and avoiding behavioral mistakes.

**Q: Should I pursue alpha aggressively 5 years before retirement?**
A: No. Use a 70/30 core-satellite approach. The 70% core protects against significant losses, while the 30% satellite pursues alpha. Never risk your retirement on aggressive strategies.

**Q: How often should I rebalance my portfolio?**
A: Pre-retirees should rebalance quarterly or when allocations drift 5% or more. As you approach retirement, increase to monthly rebalancing for risk control.

**Q: What's the best fund for dividend growth?**
A: SCHD (Schwab Dividend Appreciation), VIG (Vanguard Dividend Appreciation), or individual dividend aristocrats. SCHD has lower expense ratio (0.06%) and focuses on consistent dividend growers.

**Q: Can I use options strategies safely before retirement?**
A: Yes, but conservatively. Covered calls (sell calls on stock you own) and cash-secured puts (sell puts with cash backing) are appropriate. Avoid naked calls, spreads, and leverage.

**Q: How do taxes affect alpha returns?**
A: Significantly. A 2% gross alpha becomes 1.3% net alpha after 35% tax on gains. Use tax-loss harvesting, hold winners long-term, and locate assets strategically (high-turnover in IRAs, tax-efficient in taxable).

**Q: What if I'm too busy to actively manage satellite positions?**
A: Use factor ETFs instead: VTV (value), VBR (small-cap value), MTUM (momentum), QUAL (quality). These capture alpha factors with minimal management.
