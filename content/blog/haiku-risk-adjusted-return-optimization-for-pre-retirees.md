# Risk-Adjusted Return Optimization for Pre-Retirees

Pre-retirees are typically ages 55-67 with 5-15 years to retirement. They need to transition from accumulation to income while managing sequence of returns risk. Understanding risk-adjusted returns during this critical period shapes retirement success.

## Pre-Retiree Definition and Goals

Pre-retirees are typically ages 55-67 with:
- 5-15 years to retirement
- Need to transition from accumulation to income
- Moderate risk tolerance (can't recover from major losses)
- Goal: Generate rising income while preserving capital growth
- Portfolio size: Usually $500,000-$2,000,000+

## Pre-Retiree Optimal Asset Allocation

Target allocation for $800,000 portfolio:

| Asset Class | Weight | Amount | Yield | Purpose |
|------------|--------|--------|-------|---------|
| Dividend stocks (SCHD) | 30% | $240,000 | 3.6% | Growth + income |
| Growth stocks (VTI) | 20% | $160,000 | 1.5% | Capital appreciation |
| Municipal bonds (MUB) | 30% | $240,000 | 4.0% | Tax-free income |
| Treasury bonds (BND) | 15% | $120,000 | 4.5% | Safety |
| REITs (VNQ) | 5% | $40,000 | 3.0% | Diversification |
| **Total** | **100%** | **$800,000** | **3.4%** | **$27,200/yr** |

## Calculating Pre-Retiree Risk-Adjusted Returns

Scenario: $800,000 portfolio above

Expected portfolio return: 6.5% (higher than age alone suggests)
Expected volatility: 9.2%
Risk-free rate: 4.5%
Sharpe Ratio = (6.5% - 4.5%) / 9.2% = 0.22

Interpretation: 0.22 Sharpe Ratio is adequate for pre-retirees. Target 6-7% returns with 9-10% volatility for best risk adjustment.

## Income Growth Strategy

**Years 1-5 (Ages 55-60):**
- Reinvest all dividends
- Take salary + work income
- No portfolio withdrawals yet
- Position: Dividend reinvestment growing portfolio

**Years 6-10 (Ages 61-67):**
- Start taking 50% of dividends ($13,600/year)
- Reinvest remaining 50%
- Create bridge income before Social Security
- Maintain capital growth through reinvestment

This transition eases from work income to portfolio income.

## Tax Optimization for Pre-Retirees

Place assets strategically:
- Taxable account: Dividend stocks (SCHD), municipal bonds, growth stocks
- Traditional 401k/IRA: Treasury bonds, REIT ETFs
- Roth IRA: Growth stocks (QQQ, VUG)
- HSA: High-growth small-cap stocks (tax-free growth)

Proper placement saves $2,000-$4,000 in annual taxes.

## Sequence of Returns Risk Management

Pre-retirees are vulnerable to market declines near retirement:
- $800,000 portfolio down 30% = $240,000 loss (recoverable in 5 years but impacts retirement timing)
- Bond allocation (45%) limits overall decline to 18% (more manageable)

Maintain conservative bond allocation even as tempted by rising stocks.

## Social Security Optimization

Delaying Social Security to age 70 increases benefits 32% above age 67:
- Age 67: $2,500/month × 12 = $30,000/year
- Age 70: $3,300/month × 12 = $39,600/year
- Extra: $9,600/year for life

Portfolio income ($27,200 from 3.4% yield) bridges the gap until Social Security.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I shift to bonds aggressively approaching retirement?**
A: No. Gradual shifts prevent locking in losses. Move 3-5% to bonds annually starting at 55.

**Q: At what portfolio size can I retire at 62?**
A: Generally need 25x annual expenses. $50,000 annual need = $1.25 million portfolio minimum.

**Q: Is 6% portfolio withdrawal rate safe?**
A: Too high. Pre-retirees should withdraw 3-4% initially, increasing with inflation. Preserve capital for 30+ year retirement.

**Q: How much should I have in bonds at 60?**
A: Generally 40-50% at age 60. Increase 1-2% yearly toward 60% by retirement age 67.

**Q: Should I still own growth stocks as pre-retiree?**
A: Yes. 30-40% growth stocks provides inflation protection over 30-year retirement. Bonds alone lose purchasing power.
