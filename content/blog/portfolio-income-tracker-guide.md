# Portfolio Income Tracker: Best Tools and Spreadsheet Templates

Tracking dividend income is essential for every income investor, yet most portfolio tracking tools focus on capital gains and total return while treating dividends as an afterthought. Dedicated dividend tracking provides visibility into your income stream, highlights growth trends, identifies payment gaps, and confirms that your portfolio is progressing toward your financial goals.

This guide covers the best free and paid dividend tracking tools, shows you how to build a custom tracking spreadsheet, and explains the key metrics every income investor should monitor.

## Why Tracking Dividend Income Matters

Portfolio value fluctuates daily with the market, but your dividend income stream is far more stable and predictable. Tracking dividends separately from portfolio value provides several critical benefits:

**Income predictability.** Knowing exactly how much income your portfolio generates each month and each year enables precise financial planning.

**Growth measurement.** Tracking dividend income over time reveals your annual income growth rate, which is the best measure of whether your investment strategy is working.

**Problem detection.** When a holding reduces its dividend, your tracker shows the income impact immediately, prompting you to evaluate and respond.

**Goal tracking.** If your goal is $5,000 per month in dividend income, a tracker shows exactly where you stand and how quickly you are progressing.

**Tax planning.** Accurate income tracking ensures you set aside enough for estimated taxes and simplifies year-end tax preparation.

## Best Free Dividend Tracking Tools

### Stock Events

Stock Events has become one of the most popular dividend tracking apps, particularly among mobile users. It provides:

- Automatic dividend detection and payment tracking
- Forward income projections based on current holdings
- Calendar view showing upcoming ex-dividend and payment dates
- Dividend growth rate calculations per holding
- Portfolio income summary with monthly and annual views

**Strengths:** Clean mobile interface, accurate dividend data, free tier is generous.

**Limitations:** Advanced features require premium subscription. Limited portfolio analysis beyond dividends.

**Best for:** Mobile-first investors who want quick access to dividend data on the go.

### Dividend Tracker by Simply Wall St

Simply Wall St's dividend tracking features include snowflake visualizations that combine income, growth, value, and safety metrics. The dividend-specific features provide:

- Future dividend income estimates
- Payout sustainability analysis
- Dividend history charts per holding
- Industry comparisons for yield and safety

**Strengths:** Visual presentation makes complex data accessible. Good safety analysis integration.

**Limitations:** Full features require paid subscription. Focused on individual stock analysis rather than portfolio-level income tracking.

**Best for:** Investors who want dividend tracking combined with broader fundamental analysis.

### Google Sheets with GOOGLEFINANCE

Google Sheets provides a free, flexible platform for custom dividend tracking. The GOOGLEFINANCE function automatically retrieves stock prices and some dividend data:

```
=GOOGLEFINANCE("KO", "price")
=GOOGLEFINANCE("JNJ", "eps")
```

**Strengths:** Completely free, fully customizable, shareable, accessible from any device.

**Limitations:** GOOGLEFINANCE has limited dividend-specific data. Manual entry required for dividend amounts and payment dates. Requires spreadsheet skills to build a comprehensive tracker.

**Best for:** Data-savvy investors who want complete control over their tracking system.

### Yahoo Finance Portfolio

Yahoo Finance offers a free portfolio tracker that displays dividend yield, payment dates, and projected income. Its strength lies in integration with Yahoo Finance's broader research tools.

**Strengths:** Free, integrates with Yahoo Finance ecosystem, shows upcoming ex-dividend dates.

**Limitations:** Limited customization. No historical income tracking or growth rate calculations.

**Best for:** Casual dividend investors who want basic tracking within a familiar platform.

## Best Paid Dividend Tracking Tools

### Sharesight

Sharesight is widely regarded as the best portfolio tracking tool for dividend investors. It provides:

- Automatic dividend recording and reinvestment tracking
- Future income projections based on confirmed and estimated dividends
- Detailed performance reports separating capital gains from income returns
- Tax reporting with capital gains tax calculations
- Support for international stocks, ETFs, managed funds, and multiple currencies

**Pricing:** Free for one portfolio and up to 10 holdings. Paid plans start at approximately $15/month for unlimited holdings.

**Strengths:** Most comprehensive income tracking available. Excellent tax reporting. Supports international investors with multi-currency.

**Limitations:** Paid plans required for serious use. Interface has a learning curve.

**Best for:** Serious dividend investors with larger portfolios who want professional-grade income tracking and tax reporting.

### Simply Safe Dividends

Simply Safe Dividends combines portfolio tracking with proprietary dividend safety analysis:

- Portfolio income dashboard showing annual and monthly income
- Dividend Safety Scores (0-100) for each holding
- Real-time alerts when dividend safety changes
- Projected income growth based on analyst estimates
- Dividend cut risk assessment

**Pricing:** Approximately $499/year or $49/month.

**Strengths:** Unmatched dividend safety analysis. Portfolio income projections incorporating safety risk. Excellent for retirees concerned about dividend cuts.

**Limitations:** Expensive. Primarily U.S. stock coverage. Less useful for tracking mechanics (actual payments, tax lots) versus dividend analysis.

**Best for:** Income-dependent investors who prioritize dividend safety analysis alongside tracking.

### Seeking Alpha Premium

Seeking Alpha provides dividend tracking within its broader investment research platform:

- Dividend grades (A+ through F) for growth, yield, safety, consistency
- Portfolio monitoring with dividend alerts
- Quant ratings that incorporate dividend metrics
- Community discussion and analysis for each holding

**Pricing:** Approximately $239/year.

**Strengths:** Combines tracking with deep research community. Quant ratings provide quick assessments.

**Limitations:** Dividend tracking is one component of a broader platform. Less focused than dedicated dividend tools.

**Best for:** Investors who want dividend tracking integrated with comprehensive stock research.

## Building a Custom Dividend Tracking Spreadsheet

For investors who prefer complete control, a custom spreadsheet provides maximum flexibility. Here is a framework for building an effective dividend tracker.

### Sheet 1: Holdings Summary

Create a table with these columns:

| Ticker | Shares | Avg Cost | Current Price | Annual Div/Share | Forward Yield | Yield on Cost | Annual Income | % of Total Income |
|--------|--------|----------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|---------------|---------------|-------------------|

This summary provides a snapshot of your portfolio's income characteristics.

### Sheet 2: Monthly Income Calendar

Track actual and expected dividend payments by month:

| Month | Stock 1 | Stock 2 | Stock 3 | ... | Total |
|-------|---------|---------|---------|-----|-------|
| Jan | $125 | | $200 | | $325 |
| Feb | | $180 | | | $180 |
| Mar | | | $200 | | $200 |

This reveals income distribution across months and highlights gaps.

### Sheet 3: Income History

Record actual dividends received over time:

| Date | Ticker | Div/Share | Shares | Total Payment | Reinvested? |
|------|--------|-----------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| 2026-01-15 | KO | $0.485 | 500 | $242.50 | Yes |

This history enables accurate income growth calculations and tax reporting.

### Sheet 4: Growth Dashboard

Track key metrics over time:

| Year | Total Dividend Income | YoY Growth % | Portfolio Value | Yield on Portfolio |
|------|----------------------|--------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| 2023 | $4,200 | - | $140,000 | 3.0% |
| 2024 | $5,100 | 21.4% | $162,000 | 3.1% |
| 2025 | $6,200 | 21.6% | $185,000 | 3.4% |

The growth dashboard reveals whether your portfolio is on track to meet income goals.

### Automating Your Spreadsheet

Use APIs and data feeds to reduce manual entry:

- **Google Sheets GOOGLEFINANCE function** for current prices
- **IEX Cloud or Alpha Vantage APIs** for dividend data (free tiers available)
- **Import from brokerage** by exporting CSV files of dividend payments monthly

## Key Metrics to Track

### Annual Dividend Income

The total dollar amount of dividends received in a calendar year. This is your headline number and the primary measure of income investing success.

### Annual Income Growth Rate

Year-over-year growth in total dividend income. This growth comes from three sources: dividend increases by existing holdings, DRIP purchasing additional shares, and new capital deployed into dividend stocks.

A well-managed dividend growth portfolio should target 8-15% annual income growth through the combination of these three sources.

### Weighted Average Yield

Your portfolio's total annual income divided by total portfolio value. This tells you how efficiently your capital generates income.

### Weighted Average Dividend Growth Rate

The income-weighted average growth rate across all holdings. Weight each holding's growth rate by its contribution to total portfolio income.

### Income Diversification

Track what percentage of total income comes from each holding and each sector. No single stock should contribute more than 10% of total income, and no sector should contribute more than 25%.

### Yield on Cost

Your portfolio's total annual income divided by total capital invested. This metric shows how your income has grown relative to the actual dollars you invested.

### Forward Income Projection

Estimate next year's income using current holdings, expected dividend increases, and planned new investments. Compare this projection to your income goal.

## Tracking Best Practices

### Monthly Reviews

Each month, record actual dividends received, update share counts for DRIP purchases, and note any dividend changes. This takes 15-30 minutes and keeps your tracker current.

### Quarterly Deep Dives

Each quarter, review dividend safety for all holdings, check payout ratios and free cash flow coverage, evaluate income diversification, and compare actual income to projections. This analysis takes 1-2 hours.

### Annual Comprehensive Review

Once per year, calculate total annual income and growth rate, rebalance portfolio income allocations, update forward income projections, review sector and holding concentration, and set income goals for the coming year.

## Key Takeaways

- Dedicated dividend income tracking provides visibility, predictability, and goal measurement that standard portfolio trackers miss
- Free tools like Stock Events and Google Sheets work well for basic tracking, while paid tools like Sharesight and Simply Safe Dividends offer comprehensive analysis
- A custom spreadsheet with holdings summary, monthly calendar, income history, and growth dashboard provides maximum flexibility and control
- Track annual income, income growth rate, weighted average yield, yield on cost, and income diversification as your core metrics
- Monthly recording, quarterly analysis, and annual comprehensive review provide the right cadence for maintaining an accurate, actionable income tracker

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best free dividend tracker?

Stock Events offers the best free dividend tracking experience for most investors, with automatic dividend detection, forward projections, and a clean calendar view. For investors comfortable with spreadsheets, Google Sheets provides unlimited customization at zero cost.

### How often should I update my dividend tracker?

Record dividends monthly (or whenever payments are received) and conduct a thorough review quarterly. The quarterly review should include dividend safety checks, income concentration analysis, and comparison to your income goals.

### Can I track dividends from multiple brokerage accounts in one tracker?

Yes. Third-party trackers like Sharesight can link to multiple brokerages for consolidated tracking. For spreadsheet-based tracking, manually combine holdings from all accounts into a single tracker. The total portfolio view is essential for accurate income analysis regardless of how many brokerages you use.

### What is a good annual income growth rate to target?

A target of 8-15% annual income growth is reasonable for an actively managed dividend growth portfolio. This growth comes from dividend increases (typically 5-8%), DRIP reinvestment (adding 2-3%), and new capital deployment (variable). Portfolios without new capital contributions should target 7-11% from organic dividend growth and reinvestment alone.

### Should I track gross or net (after-tax) dividend income?

Track both. Gross income tells you how your portfolio is performing as an income generator. Net income tells you how much cash you actually have available for expenses or reinvestment. The difference between gross and net highlights your tax drag and can inform decisions about account placement and tax optimization.
