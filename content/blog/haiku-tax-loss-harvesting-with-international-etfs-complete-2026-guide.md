## International ETFs and Tax-Loss Harvesting

International ETFs add complexity to tax-loss harvesting but also create unique opportunities. Foreign currency fluctuations and regional market cycles generate harvesting opportunities distinct from domestic assets.

Understanding how to harvest losses in international positions while managing wash-sale rules across regions is critical for globally diversified portfolios.

## Major International ETF Categories

**Developed Markets**:
- VXUS (Vanguard Total International Stock): $1.2 trillion AUM, lowest cost (0.08%)
- IEFA (iShares Core MSCI EAFE): $45 billion, similar exposure
- EFA (iShares MSCI EAFE): $35 billion, older vintage

**Emerging Markets**:
- VWO (Vanguard FTSE Emerging Markets): 0.08% expense ratio

- FXI (China): Chinese equities

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy for International ETFs

### Step 1: Identify Underwater Positions

**Example Portfolio**:
- 30% US (VOO): $150,000
- 20% Developed International (VXUS): $100,000
- 10% Emerging Markets (VWO): $50,000
- 40% Bonds: $200,000

**Market Scenario**: Dollar strengthens, international stocks decline

- VOO: $150,000 → $147,000 (loss: $3,000)
- VXUS: $100,000 → $92,000 (loss: $8,000) ← Primary harvesting target
- VWO: $50,000 → $48,000 (loss: $2,000)
- Bonds: $200,000 → $198,000 (loss: $2,000)

**Harvestable Loss Opportunity**: $15,000 total

### Step 2: Execute Wash-Sale Compliant Swaps

**VXUS Harvest Strategy** (lose $8,000):

Correct wash-sale compliant alternatives:
- Switch to IEFA (iShares Core MSCI EAFE) ✓ Different fund family
- Switch to IEMG (add emerging market exposure) ✓ Different region
- Switch to EFA (older EAFE fund) ✓ Slightly different underlying

Incorrect (wash-sale violation):
- Sell VXUS, buy more VXUS ✗ Substantially identical
- Sell VXUS, buy VOI (Vanguard EAFE) ✗ Same fund family, same index

**Key Rule**: Different ETF provider or different underlying index avoids wash-sale violation.

### Step 3: Calculate Tax Savings

Tax savings from harvesting $8,000 VXUS loss:
- At 24% federal rate: $1,920 tax savings
- At 32% rate (higher bracket): $2,560 tax savings
- Total harvestable loss: $15,000
- Total tax savings: $3,600-$4,800

## Currency Impact on Harvesting

Currency fluctuations create unique harvesting opportunities and challenges:

### Scenario 1: Dollar Strengthening (2022-2023)

When USD strengthens, international assets decline:
- VXUS declines 15% from currency headwind
- Harvesting opportunity: $15,000 loss on $100,000 position
- Tax savings: $3,000-$3,600
- Currency recovery opportunity: Hold replacement position for currency rebound

**Optimal Strategy**: Harvest when dollar strong, hold replacement for eventual currency recovery

### Scenario 2: Currency Declining

When USD weakens, international assets appreciate:
- Minimal harvesting opportunity
- Currency is working for you (gains)
- Focus harvesting on other positions
- International harvesting will resume when dollar strengthens again

### Currency Hedging Consideration

Some international ETFs offer currency-hedged versions:
- USO (Vanguard Emerging Markets Currency Hedged): Removes currency risk
- IEFV (MSCI Emerging Markets Currency Hedged): Similar hedge

**Harvesting Impact**: Hedged versions eliminate currency volatility, reducing harvesting opportunities but also reducing risk. Choose based on your foreign currency view.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I avoid international ETFs due to harvesting complexity?**
A: No. International diversification provides returns uncorrelated with US markets. Harvesting complexity is minimal with clear wash-sale rules.

**Q: Can I harvest VXUS and buy VWO without wash-sale violation?**
A: Yes. They're different indices (EAFE vs. emerging markets). However, tax impact of buying different risk level should be intentional.

**Q: What about currency-hedged international ETFs for harvesting?**
A: Hedged versions reduce harvesting opportunities (less volatility) but provide more predictable returns. Choose based on your preference, not harvesting convenience.

**Q: Does international harvesting get more complicated with foreign tax credits?**
A: Possibly. Keep detailed records of where losses occurred. Consult a tax professional if international allocation exceeds 30% of portfolio.

**Q: Should I harvest losses in emerging markets more aggressively?**
A: Yes. Emerging markets are more volatile, creating 2-3x more harvesting opportunities than developed markets annually.
