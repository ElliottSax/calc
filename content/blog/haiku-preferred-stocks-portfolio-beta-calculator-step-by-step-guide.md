## Understanding Preferred Stock Beta

Preferred stock beta measures interest rate sensitivity and equity risk. Preferred stocks typically have beta of 0.6-0.9, between bonds (0.0-0.2) and stocks (0.9-1.2).

Formula: Beta = Covariance(Return, Market) / Variance(Market)

## Typical Preferred Stock Betas

Different preferred stock categories:

| Category | Beta | Duration | Risk Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Short-term preferred | 0.35 | 2-3 years | Very low interest rate risk |
| Intermediate preferred (PFF) | 0.65 | 5-6 years | Moderate risk |
| Long-term preferred | 0.85 | 8-12 years | Higher interest rate risk |
| High-yield preferred | 0.75 | 5-7 years | Credit risk component |
| Individual preferred stocks | 0.70 | Varies | Company-specific risk |

PFF's 0.65 beta means it moves 65% as much as broad market indices.

## Step-by-Step Portfolio Beta Calculation

**Example: $200,000 income-focused portfolio**

**Step 1: List holdings with beta values**

| Holding | Amount | Weight | Beta |
|---|---|---|---|
| BND (Bonds) | $80,000 | 40% | 0.10 |
| PFF (Preferred) | $60,000 | 30% | 0.65 |
| SCHD (Dividend) | $40,000 | 20% | 0.95 |
| Cash | $20,000 | 10% | 0.00 |

**Step 2: Calculate weighted betas**

| Holding | Weight × Beta |
|---|---|
| BND | 40% × 0.10 = 0.040 |
| PFF | 30% × 0.65 = 0.195 |
| SCHD | 20% × 0.95 = 0.190 |
| Cash | 10% × 0.00 = 0.000 |
| **Portfolio Beta** | **0.425** |

Portfolio beta of 0.425 means 42.5% of market volatility.

## Impact of Preferred Stock Allocation Changes

## Currency and Credit Beta in Preferred Stocks

Preferred stock beta contains multiple components:

**Duration beta (interest rate sensitivity):**
- Accounts for 70% of preferred volatility
- PFF duration 5-6 years = 0.50 beta impact

**Credit beta (default/spread risk):**
- Accounts for 30% of preferred volatility
- Investment-grade preferred = 0.15 beta impact

Total PFF beta = 0.50 + 0.15 = 0.65

Higher-yield preferred funds have higher credit beta (0.20-0.25).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Should preferred beta match my stock beta?**
A: No. Use lower beta for income focus (0.4-0.6 preferred) versus growth (0.8-1.2 stock).

**Q2: Is 0.65 beta appropriate for income portfolio?**
A: Yes, middle ground. Provides income stability with some growth participation.

**Q3: What preferred beta suits retirees?**
A: 0.3-0.5 (conservative). Shift from bonds (0.1) to preferred (0.65) only gradually.

**Q4: Can preferred stock beta be 1.0?**
A: Rarely. 1.0 beta would require 100% exposure to high-risk growth preferred shares.

**Q5: Should I rebalance when beta drifts above/below target?**
A: Yes, quarterly check. Rebalance if beta drifts >0.10 from target to maintain risk profile.
