# 2026 Guide: Growth Investing for College Funding

College funding with an 18-year time horizon allows aggressive growth investing. Stocks appreciate on average 7-10% annually, while bonds provide only 4-5%, making equities ideal for longer horizons.

## Growth College Fund Allocation

### Aggressive Growth (18 Years to College)

**VTI (U.S. Total Market): 60%**
- Diversified, low-cost, 1.4% yield
- Expected return: 8% annually

**VXUS (International): 25%**
- Emerging market exposure, currency diversification
- Expected return: 9% annually (emerging markets)

**Growth Stocks/Small-Cap Growth: 10%**
- AVUV (small-cap growth) or individual growth picks
- Expected return: 10%+ annually

**Cash: 5%**
- Emergency liquidity
- Yield: 5%

**Blended expected return: 8.2% annually**

Why this allocation? Time horizon is long enough to weather any crash. Any money invested today has 18 years to recover and grow.

## Growth Investing Risk: Market Corrections

Growth portfolios experience larger downturns than balanced portfolios.

### Historical Drawdown: 2008 Financial Crisis

Conservative portfolio (60/40 stocks/bonds):
- Peak value: $100,000 (2007)
- Trough value: $80,000 (2009)
- Drawdown: 20%
- Recovery time: 3 years

Aggressive growth portfolio (90/10 stocks/bonds):
- Peak value: $100,000 (2007)
- Trough value: $60,000 (2009)
- Drawdown: 40%
- Recovery time: 5 years

**For 18-year college fund, 5-year recovery is acceptable:**
- Invest 2007-2009: $10,000 annual + $5,000 contributions = $30,000 invested during crash
- Crash hits: portfolio drops 40%
- But continue adding $5,000/year (dollar-cost averaging)
- 2014 (5 years later): Fully recovered + growth
- 2026 (18-year endpoint): Fully funded college

## Growth Investing Strategy for College

### Three-Phase Allocation Shift

**Phase 1: Years 1-12 (Aggressive Growth)**
- 90% stocks (VTI 60%, VXUS 25%, growth 5%)
- 10% bonds/cash
- Rationale: Time to recover from crashes

**Phase 2: Years 13-16 (Moderate Growth)**
- 70% stocks (VTI 40%, VXUS 20%, growth 10%)
- 20% bonds
- 10% cash
- Rationale: Approaching college, reduce volatility slightly

**Phase 3: Years 17-18 (Conservative Transition)**
- 50% stocks (VTI 30%, VXUS 15%, bonds 20%, cash 10%)
- Rationale: College arrival imminent, protect gains

**Why this shift?** Reduces sequence-of-returns risk (market crash in Year 17 is devastating; crash in Year 2 is manageable).

## College Fund Rebalancing During Growth Phase

### Annual Growth Portfolio Review

Target: 60/25/10/5 (VTI/VXUS/growth/cash)

After market movement:
- VTI: 65% (overweight, up strongly)
- VXUS: 20% (underweight)
- Growth: 12% (overweight)
- Cash: 3% (underweight)

**Rebalancing action:**
- Sell 5% VTI
- Sell 2% growth stocks
- Buy 4% VXUS
- Buy 3% cash

**Result:** Return to target allocation

**Frequency:** Annual rebalancing preferred (tax-efficient, forces discipline)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is aggressive growth too risky for college?**
A: Not with 18-year horizon. Crashes recover in 3-5 years. College fund has time.

**Q: Should I move to bonds in final years?**
A: Yes. Years 16-18, shift 30-40% to bonds/cash to protect gains from late-stage crashes.

**Q: What if market crashes in year 17?**
A: Devastating. With proper glide path (to bonds in years 16-18), this risk is minimized. 40% bond allocation cushions market declines.

**Q: Can growth investing beat 8%+ annually?**
A: Possibly in bull markets (2010-2021 averaged 12%+). But conservative estimate of 8-10% is safer for planning.

**Q: Is VXUS emerging markets too risky?**
A: VXUS includes developed + emerging. More stable than pure emerging market ETFs. 25% allocation is reasonable.
