# How to Calculate Tax-Loss Harvesting Savings for Small-Cap Funds in 2026

Small-cap funds exhibit greater volatility, creating frequent harvesting opportunities. Tax-loss harvesting becomes especially valuable for small-cap investors, potentially offsetting 30-50% of portfolio losses during downturns. This guide teaches you to calculate and implement tax-loss harvesting for small-cap fund investments.

## Small-Cap Harvesting Advantages

Small-cap positions present optimal harvesting conditions:

1. **Higher volatility**: More frequent losses creating harvesting opportunities
2. **Multiple substitutes**: VB vs SCHA, IWM vs JWSM offer similar exposure
3. **Momentum preservation**: Harvesting into similar small-caps maintains exposure
4. **Tax-loss accumulation**: Volatile positions generate recurring harvesting dollars
5. **Market correction alignment**: Biggest harvesting opportunities occur during worst downturns

Small-cap investors in 2024-2025 faced substantial downside, creating multi-year harvesting opportunities.

## The Core Tax Savings Formula

**Tax Savings = Realized Loss × Your Marginal Tax Rate**

For small-cap investors, this becomes:

**Example:**
- VB (Vanguard Small-Cap) position: $50,000 purchase
- Current value after market decline: $43,000
- Loss: $7,000
- Marginal tax rate: 32%
- Tax savings: $7,000 × 0.32 = $2,240

The $2,240 represents direct tax liability reduction.

## Wash Sale Navigation for Small-Caps

Small-cap fund substitution options:

**Substantially identical (wash sale applies):**
- VB (total small-cap) vs SCHA (small-cap) - same index, different providers
- IJR vs IWM vs IWN - all track Russell 2000

**NOT substantially identical (harvest safely):**
- VB (small-cap blend) vs VBR (small-cap value)
- VB vs VBK (small-cap growth)
- Small-cap US funds vs small-cap international funds
- Small-cap value vs small-cap growth
- Small-cap large-cap blend (as temporary alternative)

**Harvesting strategy:**
Sell VB losses, immediately buy SCHA (different provider, same index). While both track small-cap market, IRS accepts different-provider tracking as non-identical. Alternatively, switch between VBR (value) and VBK (growth) to access completely different market segments.

## Multi-Position Harvesting Strategies

Optimize across small-cap holdings:

**Strategy 1: Same-Index Switching**
VB losses → Switch to SCHA, IJR, or IWM (all track small-cap, different providers)

**Strategy 2: Value/Growth Rotation**
VB (blend) losses → Switch to VBR (value) or VBK (growth)
When VBR (value) shows losses → Switch to VBK (growth)

**Strategy 3: Market-Cap Rotating**
Small-cap losses → Temporarily switch to mid-cap (IWN) or large-cap (VOO)
Rebalance back to small-cap when small-caps rebound

**Strategy 4: International Diversification**
US small-cap losses → Switch to international small-cap (IEMG, EWG, or EUSA)
Maintains growth exposure while accessing different market dynamics

## FAQ

**Q: Should I harvest small-cap losses aggressively even if I'm bullish on small-caps?**
A: Yes. Harvesting doesn't reduce exposure; immediate reinvestment into similar funds maintains positioning while capturing tax benefits.

**Q: Can I harvest, then wait 60 days to rebuy the same fund?**
A: No. The 30-day rule applies both directions. You must wait 31+ days after selling to repurchase the identical fund or violate wash sale rules.

**Q: Are losses from small-cap ETFs different from small-cap mutual fund losses?**
A: No. Tax treatment identical; wash sale rules apply equally to ETFs and mutual funds.

**Q: What percentage portfolio loss triggers harvesting?**
A: Harvest any position down $1,000+; any portfolio down 10%+. Smaller thresholds create minimal tax benefit relative to transaction costs.

**Q: Do small-cap losses carry forward if I don't need them?**
A: Yes. Unused losses carry forward indefinitely. Harvest aggressively in down years; apply accumulated losses against future gains.
