# REITs CAGR Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) measures the smooth annual growth rate of your REIT investment over multiple years, accounting for the volatility and ups/downs along the way. A 12% CAGR means your investment grew 12% annually on average, compounding over the period.

## Understanding REIT CAGR

Year-to-year REIT returns fluctuate wildly: +15% one year, -5% the next. CAGR shows the true growth trajectory by smoothing these variations. This metric helps investors compare REITs fairly regardless of market conditions during different time periods.

## Why REIT CAGR Matters More Than Annual Returns

When evaluating REITs, annual returns are misleading because they vary so much by year. CAGR eliminates this noise by showing what steady annual return you would need to match your actual ending value. This helps you set realistic expectations for future REIT investing.

## CAGR Formula Explained

**CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/Number of Years) - 1**

Example with real numbers:
- Starting investment in VNQ: $10,000 in January 2021
- Ending value: $14,500 in January 2026 (5 years)
- CAGR = ($14,500 / $10,000)^(1/5) - 1 = 7.76%

## REIT CAGR Calculation Step-by-Step

1. Determine starting investment amount (original purchase price)
2. Find ending portfolio value (including dividends if reinvested)
3. Count exact years held (use decimal for partial years)
4. Divide ending value by starting value
5. Raise result to power of (1/years)
6. Subtract 1 and multiply by 100 for percentage

This gives you the smoothed annual growth rate regardless of year-to-year fluctuations.

## Impact of Dividend Reinvestment on CAGR

Reinvesting dividends substantially boosts REIT CAGR through compounding:

- Without reinvestment: 6.5% CAGR
- With reinvestment: 8.1% CAGR
- 5-year difference: $10,000 grows to $13,700 vs. $13,200

Always include dividends when calculating true REIT returns. This is your actual wealth-building performance.

## Comparing REIT CAGR to Other Investments

REIT CAGR (7-8%) typically falls between:

- Bonds: 4-5% CAGR (low volatility, steady)
- Large-cap stocks (VTI): 8-10% CAGR (moderate volatility)
- Small-cap stocks: 9-12% CAGR (higher volatility)

REITs offer stability with reasonable growth, making them attractive core holdings for diversified portfolios.

## FAQ

**Q: Why do some REIT websites show different CAGR?**
A: Time period differences are the main cause. A 5-year CAGR differs significantly from 10-year CAGR due to different starting prices. Always check the dates specified.

**Q: Does CAGR include management fees?**
A: Yes. CAGR reflects your actual net returns after all fees deducted. This is the true performance you achieve.

**Q: Is 7% REIT CAGR good?**
A: Yes. That's slightly below long-term stock averages but with less volatility and better income. Good risk-adjusted returns.

**Q: How does REIT CAGR change during bear markets?**
A: It decreases as current values fall. Historical CAGR stays fixed; forward CAGR projections decline until recovery occurs.

**Q: Should I use CAGR or annualized return?**
A: Use CAGR for periods longer than 1 year. They're identical for single-year periods but CAGR is more accurate for multiple years.
