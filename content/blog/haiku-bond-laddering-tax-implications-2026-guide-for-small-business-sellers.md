## Bond Laddering for Small Business Sellers

Bond laddering distributes investments across bonds maturing in different years. For small business sellers, this strategy coordinates with tax planning to maximize after-tax returns.

## Tax Implications

Municipal bonds deliver federal tax-free income. The advantage depends on your marginal tax bracket.

For 32% bracket: Municipal bonds at 3.2% equal 4.71% corporate taxable equivalent yield.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting

When rates rise 2%, bond prices fall $1,000 per $50,000:
- Sell at loss, realize -$1,000
- Tax benefit: $240 (24% rate)
- Rebuy higher-yielding bonds immediately
- Net result: Better yield + tax savings

- 5-year duration

### Early Retirement (Distribution)
- 100% municipal bonds (maximize tax-free)

### Stable Retirement (Late Distribution)
- 75% municipal, 25% dividend stocks
- 3-5 year ladder
- Coordinate with Social Security, Medicare

## Key Tax Rules

1. **Verify municipal bond tax-exemption** - Avoid private activity bonds that trigger AMT
2. **Track cost basis** - Essential for tax-loss harvesting
3. **Coordinate with Social Security** - Bond interest counts toward MAGI
4. **Monitor AMT risk** - Combined income >$128K triggers AMT on tax-exempt interest

## FAQ

**Q: Should I use municipal or Treasury bonds?**
A: If federal rate >28%, munis win. Below 24%, Treasuries' higher coupon compensates.

**Q: Can I use bond ETFs instead?**
A: Yes. VGIT simplifies ladder management—no maturity tracking needed.

**Q: What about TIPS for inflation protection?**
A: Allocate 20-30% to TIPS if you expect inflation >3%.

**Q: Should I hold ladder in Roth or traditional IRA?**
A: Traditional IRA—bonds benefit most from tax deferral.

**Q: How does ladder interact with Medicare IRMAA?**
A: Bond income counts toward MAGI. High MAGI triggers higher Medicare premiums.

**Q: If rates drop, am I locked in?**
A: You keep locked-in rate (benefit!). New maturities reinvest at lower rates.
