## The Power of Dividend Growth Rate

Dividend growth rate dramatically impacts long-term wealth preservation.

### Formula: Future Annual Dividends

```
Future Dividend = Current Dividend × (1 + Growth Rate)^Years
```

**5% Dividend Growth (Conservative):**
$30,400 × (1.05)^30 = $134,140 annual income in Year 30

**6% Dividend Growth (Typical for Aristocrats):**
$30,400 × (1.06)^30 = $179,800 annual income in Year 30

**7% Dividend Growth (Aggressive SCHD):**
$30,400 × (1.07)^30 = $241,400 annual income in Year 30

**Same $1M portfolio, different dividend growth rates, creates $65,000-$211,000 swing in Year 30 income.**

This demonstrates: focus on dividend growth rate, not just yield. Higher growth compounds to dramatically higher income.

## Preservation Portfolio Rebalancing

Dividend growth portfolios need rebalancing to maintain allocation.

### Annual Rebalancing Review

**Target allocation:** 40% SCHD, 40% aristocrats, 10% VTI, 10% BND

**After market movement (Year 10):**
- SCHD: $520,000 (45% vs. target 40%, overweight due to strong dividend growth)
- Aristocrats: $380,000 (33%, underweight due to slower growth)
- VTI: $140,000 (12%, overweight)
- BND: $90,000 (8%, underweight due to rising rates)

**Rebalancing action:**
- Sell $40,000 SCHD
- Buy $40,000 of aristocrats and BND
- Return to 40/40/10/10

**Why rebalance?** Dividend-growth stocks often outperform, drifting allocation. Annual rebalancing forces buying underperformers (mean reversion).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is dividend growth better than bond income for preservation?**
A: Yes, for 30+ year horizons. Dividends grow past inflation; bonds decline in purchasing power. For 10-year horizons, bonds competitive.

**Q: What if dividends get cut?**
A: Rare for aristocrats (25+ years = proven safety). If cut, company removed from portfolio. Diversification (100+ companies) protects.

**Q: Should I reinvest dividends or withdraw?**
A: Reinvest for first 5-10 years (compound growth). Switch to withdrawal at retirement (Years 15+, when dividend income substantial).

**Q: Is SCHD risky for preservation?**
A: No. SCHD is dividend index of high-quality payers. Less risky than individual dividend stocks. Appropriate for preservation.

**Q: What allocation for most conservative preservation?**
A: 50% SCHD, 30% aristocrats, 10% VTI, 10% bonds. Increases bond allocation, lowers volatility, reduces dividend growth potential slightly.
