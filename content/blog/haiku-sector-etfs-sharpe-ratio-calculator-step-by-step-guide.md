# Sector ETFs Sharpe Ratio Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

Sharpe Ratio measures excess return per unit of risk for sector ETF portfolios. This reveals whether a sector portfolio justifies its volatility through superior returns compared to risk-free Treasury bonds.

## Sharpe Ratio for Sector ETFs

Sharpe Ratio measures excess return per unit of risk for sector ETF portfolios:

**Sharpe = (Portfolio Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Portfolio Volatility**

This reveals whether a sector portfolio justifies its volatility through superior returns.

## Why Sector ETF Sharpe Ratio Matters

Comparing raw sector returns is deeply misleading:
- Tech up 20% annually but with 25% volatility
- Healthcare up 12% annually with 10% volatility

Sharpe Ratio reveals healthcare's superior risk-adjusted performance (higher return per volatility unit).

## Calculating Sector Portfolio Sharpe Ratio

Example: Sector portfolio with allocation:

**Step 1: Calculate weighted average return**
- Tech (30%) × 12% = 3.6%
- Healthcare (30%) × 8% = 2.4%
- Utilities (20%) × 5% = 1.0%
- Industrials (20%) × 10% = 2.0%
- **Weighted return: 9.0%**

**Step 2: Calculate weighted average volatility**
- Tech (30%) × 22% = 6.6%
- Healthcare (30%) × 12% = 3.6%
- Utilities (20%) × 8% = 1.6%
- Industrials (20%) × 14% = 2.8%
- **Weighted volatility: 14.6%** (approximate)

**Step 3: Apply Sharpe formula**
- Risk-free rate: 4.5%
- Sharpe = (9.0% - 4.5%) / 14.6% = 0.31

## Sharpe Ratio Interpretation Guide

- Below 0.2: Sector returning less than justified by volatility
- 0.2 to 0.4: Fair return for volatility (acceptable)
- 0.4 to 0.6: Good risk adjustment
- 0.6+: Exceptional risk adjustment (rare)

Most sectors cluster in 0.2-0.4 range.

## Market Conditions Affecting Sector Sharpe Ratios

**Rising interest rates (2023-2024):**
- Tech Sharpe fell (growth stocks discounted)
- Financials Sharpe improved (banks benefit)
- Utilities Sharpe compressed (bond alternative)

**Falling interest rates (if occur):**
- Tech Sharpe improves (growth rewarded)
- Financials Sharpe falls (margins compress)
- Utilities Sharpe improves (defensive demand)

Monitor sector Sharpe ratios during rate regime changes.

## FAQ

**Q: Should I only buy sectors with Sharpe Ratio > 0.35?**
A: No. Diversification requires holding lower-Sharpe sectors for balance. Maintain 20-30% in defensive sectors.

**Q: Can Sharpe Ratio forecast future sector performance?**
A: No. Sharpe is historical. Future returns and volatility will differ. Use as context, not prediction.

**Q: How often should I recalculate sector Sharpe ratios?**
A: Quarterly. Market volatility shifts quickly, changing relative risk-adjusted attractiveness.

**Q: What Sharpe ratio should I target?**
A: 0.25-0.35 for diversified portfolios, 0.35+ for aggressive sector allocations. Higher is better.

**Q: Can I improve Sharpe ratio by adding bonds?**
A: Yes. Bonds reduce volatility more than they reduce returns, improving overall Sharpe ratio.
