## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long has Walmart paid dividends?

Walmart has paid dividends since 1974 and increased them for 51 consecutive years, making it one of the longest-running Dividend Aristocrats.

### What is Walmart's dividend yield?

Walmart's current dividend yield is approximately 1.3-1.5%, lower than some retail peers but backed by exceptional reliability and 51-year growth streak.

### How often does Walmart increase its dividend?

Walmart increases its dividend annually, typically announced in February and effective for the April payment. Recent increases have been 1-5% annually.

### Is Walmart a Dividend Aristocrat?

Yes, Walmart is a Dividend Aristocrat, having increased dividends for 51 consecutive years—far exceeding the 25-year requirement.

### What is Walmart's dividend payout ratio?

Walmart's payout ratio is 38-42% of earnings and 42-46% of free cash flow, providing a healthy balance between growth investment and shareholder returns.

### Will Walmart keep increasing its dividend?

Highly likely. The 51-year streak demonstrates commitment, and the conservative payout ratio provides room for continued increases barring severe economic crisis.

## Conclusion

Walmart represents the epitome of dividend safety and reliability. The 51-year streak of consecutive increases, recession-tested business model, and essential goods focus make it one of the most dependable income investments available. While the 1.4% yield won't excite income seekers and the 2-4% growth rate lags many dividend growth stocks, Walmart's defensive characteristics and aristocrat status warrant a place in conservative income portfolios.

For investors prioritizing dividend safety over growth, Walmart delivers. The company has survived and thrived through stagflation (1970s), recessions (1980-82, 1990-91, 2001, 2008-09, 2020), and competitive threats from Amazon. This resilience, combined with a fortress balance sheet and $23+ billion in annual free cash flow, suggests the dividend streak will continue for decades.

Walmart won't make you rich quickly, but it will reliably pay you quarterly for decades—exactly what defensive dividend investors seek.
