## Formula Used by Calculators

Modern calculators employ the future value formula:

**FV = PV(1 + r)^n + PMT × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r]**

Deconstructed:
- **PV(1 + r)^n** = Initial investment compounding
- **PMT × [((1 + r)^n - 1) / r]** = Monthly contributions compounding

Where:
- FV = Future Value
- PV = Present Value (initial investment)
- r = Monthly return rate (annual ÷ 12)
- n = Number of months
- PMT = Monthly payment

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Tools

Sophisticated tools identify tax-loss harvesting opportunities automatically.

**Example Scenario:**
- Purchased 100 shares of VXUS at $80 = $8,000 cost basis
- Current value: $73 = $7,300
- Unrealized loss: $700

Tool identifies this loss and automatically:
1. Sells 100 VXUS shares at $73 loss
2. Simultaneously purchases similar international fund (IEMG)
3. Realizes $700 tax loss
4. Maintains international exposure
5. Generates tax savings: $700 × 24% bracket = $168 tax reduction

Over a year with multiple losses, a $3,000-5,000 annual tax reduction is common.

Modern brokers offer automatic rebalancing that maintains target allocations.

**Automatic Rebalancing Example:**

- Stocks: 60%
- Bonds: 40%

Current Allocation (After market movement):
- Stocks: 68% (gained value)
- Bonds: 32% (lost value)

Automatic Rebalancing Action:
- Sell $40,000 of stocks at 68% valuation

Benefits:
- Automatic buy-low, sell-high discipline
- Eliminates emotional decisions
- Maintains risk target
- Triggers quarterly (not market-timer behavior)

## FAQ

**Q: Which tools do I really need?**
A: Start with your broker's free tools: automatic DRIP, portfolio tracking, and dividend monitoring. Add a retirement calculator. That covers 95% of needs for most investors.

**Q: Are robo-advisors worth the fees?**
A: If they help you avoid emotional decisions and maintain discipline, yes. If you'd otherwise do nothing, robo-advisors are worth 0.25-0.50% annually. If you have discipline, self-direct through low-cost brokers.

**Q: Should I use multiple brokers' tools?**
A: Consolidate if possible. One account tracking is simpler than multiple logins. Ensure your primary broker offers good tools before consolidating.

**Q: How often should I check my portfolio?**
A: Quarterly for rebalancing purposes, annually for goal progress assessment, weekly maximum for psychological comfort. Checking daily leads to emotional decisions.

**Q: What tool helps most with wealth building?**
A: Automatic reinvestment (DRIP). It requires zero ongoing effort, happens automatically, and compounds for decades without intervention.
