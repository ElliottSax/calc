## The Sharpe Ratio: Your Portfolio's Efficiency Scorecard

The Sharpe Ratio transforms portfolio evaluation from raw return chasing into sophisticated risk-management. This single metric reveals whether your Balanced portfolio efficiently converts risk into returns.

## Understanding the Sharpe Ratio

The Sharpe Ratio measures excess return per unit of volatility:

$$	ext{Sharpe Ratio} = rac{	ext{Portfolio Return} - 	ext{Risk-Free Rate}}{	ext{Portfolio Standard Deviation}}$$

A Sharpe Ratio of 0.50 means you earn 0.50% excess return for every 1% of volatility endured.

## Interpreting Sharpe Ratios

| Sharpe | Interpretation | Type | Example |
|--------|----------------|------|---------|
| <0.0 | Underperforms risk-free | Avoid | High-risk failures |
| 0.0-0.20 | Poor performance | Unacceptable | Speculative |
| 0.20-0.35 | Decent performance | Acceptable | Conservative |
| 0.35-0.50 | Good performance | Desirable | Optimized |
| 0.50-1.00 | Excellent performance | Target | Elite managers |
| >1.00 | Exceptional | Rare | Legendary |

## Calculating Sharpe Ratio Step-by-Step

### Your Balanced Portfolio Example
- $30,000 in VOO (S&P 500)
- $20,000 in BND (Bonds)
- $10,000 in SCHD (Dividends)
- Total: $60,000

**Weights:** VOO 50%, BND 33.33%, SCHD 16.67%

**Expected Returns:**
- VOO: 10.0%
- BND: 4.0%
- SCHD: 8.5%
- Portfolio: 8.05%

**Volatility Calculation:** 9.82%

**Sharpe Ratio:** (8.05% - 4.2%) / 9.82% = **0.39**

This excellent Sharpe indicates good risk-adjusted performance.

## Sharpe Ratio Limitations

### 1. Assumes Normal Distribution
Real returns exhibit fatter tails (more extreme events) than assumptions.

### 2. Sensitive to Time Period
Different periods produce different Sharpe Ratios. 10+ years most meaningful.

### 3. Doesn't Distinguish Volatility Direction
High upside and low downside look identical to low upside and high downside.

### 4. Assumes Consistent Correlation
Crisis periods create correlation changes Sharpe doesn't capture.

## FAQ: Sharpe Ratio

**Q1: What should my Balanced portfolio target?**
A: Target 0.30-0.45. Conservative aims 0.25-0.35; growth targets 0.35-0.45.

**Q2: How often should I check?**
A: Review quarterly. Monthly calculations are unreliable.

**Q3: Can I improve Sharpe without reducing returns?**
A: Yes, through diversification, cost reduction, and tax optimization.

**Q4: Should I switch to higher historical Sharpe?**
A: Only if structural advantages justify change. Past performance isn't guaranteed.

**Q5: Does higher Sharpe guarantee better future returns?**
A: No. It indicates better historical risk-adjustment. Future depends on conditions and valuations.

## Conclusion

The Sharpe Ratio transforms portfolio evaluation from raw return chasing to risk-adjusted analysis. For Balanced portfolios, target Sharpe Ratios of 0.35-0.40. By constructing portfolios optimized for Sharpe efficiency—through diversification, cost management, and tax awareness—you create investment vehicles delivering consistent, sustainable wealth building.
