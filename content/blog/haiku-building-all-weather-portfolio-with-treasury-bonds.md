## Introduction

Treasury bonds provide the ultimate all-weather hedge—they typically rise when stocks crash due to flight-to-safety behavior. This guide shows how to structure Treasury-focused all-weather portfolios using GOVT, TLT, and Treasury ladders, optimizing for economic resilience while maintaining 6-7% returns.

## Treasury Bond Performance in Crises

**2008 financial crisis (stocks -37%):**
- Long Treasury (TLT): +14%
- Short Treasury (VGSH): +5%
- Treasury ladder: +7-9%

**2020 COVID crash (stocks -34%):**
- Long Treasury: +17%
- Short Treasury: +4%
- Treasury ladder: +6-8%

**2022 rate hikes (stocks -18%):**
- Long Treasury: -16% (duration loss)
- Short Treasury: -5%
- Treasury ladder: -3-5%

**Key insight:** Long Treasuries (TLT) provide crash protection. Short Treasuries protect against rate increases. Balance both.

## Treasury Valuation Considerations

**When to favor Treasuries:**

| Condition | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Rates elevated (4.5%+) | Favor bonds, ladder out |
| Recession signals rising | Increase Treasury allocation |
| Equity valuations expensive | Shift to Treasuries |
| Yield curve inverted | Bullish for bonds |

**When to reduce Treasuries:**

- Rates extremely low (<2%)
- Strong economic growth
- Stock market crash recovery
- Valuation cycle early

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much Treasury allocation for all-weather?**
A: 25-40% for conservative/moderate, 15-25% for aggressive. Treasuries are portfolio insurance.

**Q: Should I use Treasury ETFs or individual bonds?**
A: Ladder individuals for $200k+. ETFs simpler for <$100k.

**Q: Which Treasury maturity is best for all-weather?**
A: Mix—short (GOVT 2-5yr) for rate stability, long (TLT 20-30yr) for crash protection.

**Q: Do Treasuries really protect in crashes?**
A: Usually yes. 2008, 2020 showed strong protection. 2022 showed rate-hike risk. Duration matters.

**Q: Should I include TIPS?**
A: Yes, 5-10% of bond allocation. Inflation protection valuable over 20+ year horizons.
