# The Ultimate Guide to Capital Gains in 2026

## Understanding Capital Gains Fundamentals

A capital gain occurs when you sell an investment for more than your purchase price:

$$\text{Capital Gain} = \text{Sale Price} - \text{Purchase Price}$$

The critical distinction separates short-term and long-term capital gains based on holding period:

**Short-term Capital Gains:** Held less than one year
- Taxed as ordinary income
- Tax rates: 10% to 37% (2026 brackets)

**Long-term Capital Gains:** Held more than one year
- Preferential tax rates: 0%, 15%, or 20%
- Significantly lower than ordinary income rates

## Calculating Wash Sale Implications

The wash sale rule prevents immediately repurchasing substantially identical securities within 30 days. This impacts timing calculations:

**Scenario: Selling VOO at a loss**
- Sale date: March 1, 2026
- Loss: $2,000
- Wash sale window: February 1 - April 1, 2026

If you repurchase VOO before April 1:
- Immediate loss deduction: $0 (disallowed)
- Adjusted basis of new purchase: Original cost + $2,000 loss
- Cost basis increases, delaying loss recognition

Optimal strategy: Sell VOO on March 1, purchase VXUS (different fund) immediately, repurchase VOO on April 2. This preserves the $2,000 loss deduction while maintaining market exposure.

## Stepped-Up Basis Planning

Strategic holding and positioning can optimize estate planning:

**Example: Inherited stock with stepped-up basis**

- Original owner purchase: $50 per share
- Death date (current price): $150 per share
- Heir receives $150 × 100 = $15,000
- New cost basis: $150 per share (stepped up)

Heir sells immediately:
- Capital gain: $0 (no tax)
- Proceeds: $15,000

Without step-up, if heir waited to sell at $180:
- Capital gain: $30 × 100 = $3,000
- Tax at 15%: $450

The step-up basis saved the heir $450 in taxes.

## FAQ

**Q: How long must I hold a stock for long-term capital gains rates?**

You must own it for more than one year. The holding period starts the day after purchase. A stock purchased March 15, 2025 and sold March 16, 2026 qualifies as long-term.

**Q: Can I carry forward capital losses to future years?**

Yes. You can use $3,000 in capital losses annually to offset ordinary income. Excess losses carry forward indefinitely to future years. If you have $10,000 in losses, use $3,000 this year and carry forward $7,000.

**Q: Are crypto capital gains taxed differently?**

No. Cryptocurrencies receive identical tax treatment as stocks. A one-year holding period qualifies for long-term rates, and wash sale rules apply.

**Q: Should I sell winners or losers when rebalancing?**

Sell losers first to harvest losses, then winners only if needed. This maximizes tax efficiency while achieving allocation targets.

**Q: What happens if I inherit appreciated stocks?**

You receive a stepped-up basis at the inheritance date value. You can sell immediately with zero capital gains tax. This tax advantage makes holding appreciated assets until death extremely valuable for estate planning.
