# How to Calculate CAGR for Growth Stocks in 2026

CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) is the single most important metric for evaluating long-term growth stock performance. It accounts for compounding and provides annualized rates enabling meaningful comparison.

## Understanding CAGR Formula

CAGR = (Ending Value / Beginning Value)^(1/Number of Years) - 1

## Real Example

VGT tech ETF, 2015-2025:
- Beginning: 0,000
- Ending: 8,400
- CAGR = (8,400/0,000)^(1/10) - 1 = 14.7%

VGT delivered 14.7% annualized growth.

## Growth Stock CAGR Performance

- VGT (Tech): 14.7% CAGR, 22% volatility
- QQQ (Nasdaq): 16.2% CAGR, 24% volatility
- VOO (S&P 500): 12.5% CAGR, 13.5% volatility

Growth stocks deliver higher CAGR with increased volatility.

## Calculating Your Growth Stock CAGR

1. Determine purchase and current value
2. Calculate years held
3. Apply formula
4. Include reinvested dividends

## Growth vs Value CAGR

Growth (tech-heavy):
- 10-year CAGR: 14-16%
- Volatility: 20-24%

Value (dividend-heavy):
- 10-year CAGR: 8-10%
- Volatility: 12-15%

## FAQ

**Q: Is 15% CAGR realistic?**
A: Tech CAGR historically averages 12-18% over 10+ years.

**Q: Should I hold 100% growth?**
A: No. Recommended 60-70% growth + 30-40% value/bonds.

## Conclusion

Calculate CAGR quarterly to track growth stock performance against benchmarks.
