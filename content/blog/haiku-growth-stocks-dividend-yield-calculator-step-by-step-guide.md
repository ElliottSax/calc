# Growth Stocks Dividend Yield Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

Dividend yield measures the annual dividend return on investment, expressed as percentage. Understanding and calculating dividend yield enables informed allocation decisions between growth and income positions. This guide provides formulas, examples, and calculation methods.

## Understanding Dividend Yield

Dividend yield represents annual dividends per dollar invested. It differs from capital appreciation, providing immediate income alongside potential growth.

## Dividend Yield Formula

**Dividend Yield = Annual Dividend Per Share / Stock Price × 100%**

### Simple Example

Stock price: $100 per share
Annual dividend: $3.50 per share

**Dividend Yield = ($3.50 / $100) × 100% = 3.5%**

### Example with ETF

VYM (Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF)
Price: $145 per share
Annual dividend: $5.89 per share

**Dividend Yield = ($5.89 / $145) × 100% = 4.06%**

## Yield on Cost: Long-Term Metric

As stock prices appreciate, yield on cost shows historical dividend return:

### Historical Yield on Cost Example

Buy date: 2010
Share price: $50
Annual dividend: $1.50 (3% yield)

Current date: 2024
Share price: $120 (appreciation)
Annual dividend: $3.50 (dividend growth)

**Current yield: $3.50 / $120 = 2.92%**
**Yield on cost: $3.50 / $50 = 7.0%**

Yield on cost (7.0%) shows superior income generation compared to current yield (2.92%).

## Calculating Portfolio Dividend Yield

### Portfolio Weighted Yield

Positions:
- $25,000 in SCHD (3.87% yield)
- $15,000 in VOO (1.43% yield)
- $10,000 in JNJ (2.87% yield)
- Total: $50,000

**Portfolio Yield = (25k × 3.87% + 15k × 1.43% + 10k × 2.87%) / 50k**
**= (967.50 + 214.50 + 287) / 50,000**
**= 1,469 / 50,000 = 2.94%**

## FAQ

**Q: What dividend yield should I target?**
A: Dividend stocks: 3-5%. Growth stocks: 1-2%. Balanced: 2-3%.

**Q: How often does dividend yield change?**
A: As stock price changes daily. As annual dividends change annually. Review quarterly for significant changes.

**Q: Should I buy high-yield stocks?**
A: High yield (>6%) often signals risk. Balance yield with dividend sustainability and capital appreciation.

**Q: Does dividend yield reinvestment increase returns?**
A: Yes. DRIP (Dividend Reinvestment Plans) compound dividend income. Automatic reinvestment maximizes wealth building.

**Q: How do I compare dividend yields between stocks?**
A: Use same measurement date for accurate comparison. Higher yield with lower growth may underperform lower-yield high-growth alternatives.
