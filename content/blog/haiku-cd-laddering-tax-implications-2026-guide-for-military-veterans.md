## FAQ

**Q: Lump sum or dollar-cost average?**
A: Lump sums beat DCA 67% of the time. But DCA reduces stress.

**Q: What allocation should I use?**
A: Age rule: Stocks = 110 - age. At 50: 60% stocks.

**Q: How often rebalance?**
A: Once yearly. More frequent = unnecessary costs.

**Q: Individual stocks or ETFs?**
A: ETFs are simpler. Individual stocks need 20-year commitment.

**Q: Markets crash 30%—what do I do?**
A: Don't panic. Markets recover in 3-5 years. Rebalance to buy low.

**Q: Can I use CDs or bonds instead?**
A: Yes, but growth is slower. Current CDs (4.6%) underperform historical 7% equity returns.

**Q: How do emergency withdrawals work?**
A: Use bonds/cash first. Avoid selling stocks at losses. Keep 6-month emergency fund separate.

**Q: Should I invest in 401k or pay debt?**
A: Priority: Employer match → high-interest debt (>5%) → max 401k.
