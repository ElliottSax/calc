# Sector ETFs Tax-Loss Harvesting Savings Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

Tax-loss harvesting sells losing sector ETF positions to realize tax losses, then immediately buys similar replacements. The tax loss offsets gains, reducing taxable income and creating substantial after-tax return improvements.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Overview

Tax-loss harvesting sells losing sector ETF positions to realize tax losses, then immediately buys similar (but not identical) replacements. The tax loss offsets gains, reducing taxable income.

## Why Tax-Loss Harvesting Works with Sector ETFs

Sector ETFs have close substitutes:
- Own XLK (tech)? Switch to VOOG + QQQ (similar tech exposure)
- Own XLU (utilities)? Switch to XLRE + XLV (defensive diversification)
- Own XLE (energy)? Switch to XLP + XLI (cyclical alternative)

Wash sale rules (30-day restriction) don't apply when swapping to different ETFs.

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy

1. In January, identify losing sector ETF positions
2. Sell XLK at loss, immediately buy VUG + QQQ
3. Wait 31+ days, then can buy XLK back
4. Harvest losses throughout year as positions decline
5. Carry forward unused losses to future years

Average investor can harvest $3,000-$8,000 in losses annually.

## Tracking and Compliance

Maintain records:
- Sell date and price of original ETF
- Buy date and price of replacement ETF
- Repurchase date if applicable
- Realized loss amount and offset gains

IRS requires proof if audited. Most brokerages track automatically.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I harvest tax losses every year?**
A: Yes, if your portfolio experiences losses. Market cycles create regular harvesting opportunities.

**Q: Does tax-loss harvesting reduce long-term returns?**
A: No. You maintain the same sector exposure through replacements. It's a free tax efficiency benefit.

**Q: What if I can't use all my losses?**
A: Carry forward unused losses indefinitely. $3,000 annually offsets ordinary income; remainder carried to future years.

**Q: Can I harvest losses in retirement accounts?**
A: No. Tax-loss harvesting only works in taxable accounts. Retirement accounts have no capital gains taxes.

**Q: Is a 30-day wait period required?**
A: Only if buying the same ETF back. Different ETFs have no restriction. Can rotate immediately with different sectors.
