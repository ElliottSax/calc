## Aggressive Investor Profile

Aggressive investors seek maximum long-term growth with high volatility tolerance. Target portfolio beta ranges from 1.1 to 1.5, significantly above market (1.0).

These investors have 20+ year time horizons, allowing recovery from major market declines. They prioritize capital appreciation over current income.

## Rebalancing Frequency for Aggressive Portfolios

Aggressive portfolios should rebalance quarterly or semi-annually:

**Target beta: 1.3**

After strong tech rally:
- Portfolio beta drifts to 1.45 (too aggressive)
- Action: Reduce tech, increase diversified holdings
- New beta: 1.3 (rebalanced)

After market correction:
- Portfolio beta drifts to 1.15 (too conservative)
- Action: Increase growth, reduce defensive holdings
- New beta: 1.3 (rebalanced)

## Volatility and Drawdown Reality

Higher beta means accepting greater drawdowns:

**Crash scenario: 30% market decline**

Portfolio beta 1.3 declines: 1.3 × -30% = -39%
$500,000 portfolio falls to $305,000 (loss: $195,000)

Recovery: Requires 64% gain ($305,000 → $500,000)

Aggressive investors must accept this reality and maintain discipline during crashes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Is portfolio beta 1.3-1.5 appropriate for anyone?**
A: Only investors with 20+ year horizons and psychological tolerance for 35-40% declines.

**Q2: Should aggressive portfolios hold bonds?**
A: Minimal (0-5%). Bonds reduce portfolio beta below target.

**Q3: Can I get higher returns without higher beta?**
A: Rarely. Higher returns require either higher risk (beta) or exceptional skill (alpha).

**Q4: How do I manage aggressive portfolio emotionally?**
A: Automatic investments, ignore daily prices, trust long-term strategy.

**Q5: When should I reduce aggressive positioning?**
A: When age approaches 50 or financial situation changes (inheritance, big expenses).
