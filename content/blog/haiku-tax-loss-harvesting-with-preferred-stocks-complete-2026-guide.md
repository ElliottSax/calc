## Preferred Stocks and Tax-Loss Harvesting

Preferred stocks are hybrid securities combining bond-like fixed income with equity characteristics. They generate high yields (4-6%) with moderate volatility, making them excellent candidates for systematic tax-loss harvesting.

Understanding preferred stock dynamics and wash-sale rules specific to this asset class is critical for optimizing harvesting strategies.

## Understanding Preferred Stocks

Preferred stocks are a security type between common stocks and bonds:

**Characteristics**:
- Fixed dividend rate: 4-8% (higher than common stock dividend)
- Senior to common stock: Paid before common stock dividends
- Convertible or non-convertible: Some convert to common stock
- Callable: Issuer can buy back at set price
- Yield-driven: Primary benefit is income

**Major Preferred Stock ETFs**:
- PFF (iShares Preferred & Income Securities): $13 billion, 5.6% yield
- PFFA (Invesco Preferred ETF): Lower expense ratio
- PSK (Invesco Preferred Stocks): Similar focus

**Individual Preferred Stocks**:
- JP Morgan JPM-F: 4.8% yield
- Bank of America BAC-D: 5.2% yield
- Citigroup C-D: 5.1% yield

## Tax-Loss Harvesting Opportunity in Preferred Stocks

### Why Preferred Stocks Create Harvesting Opportunities

Preferred stocks are sensitive to interest rates (like bonds) but more volatile:

**Interest Rate Rise Scenario** (2022-2023):
- PFF price declines 15-25% (similar to bond funds)
- Creates large harvestable losses
- Meanwhile, yield is locked in (5%+)

**Example**: $100,000 preferred stock position

- Purchase: PFF at $22/share (4,545 shares)
- Rates rise, price falls to $18.50
- Loss: $15,454 (15.5%)
- Dividend income during decline: $5,600
- Net loss after dividend: $9,854 (harvestable)

## Preferred Stock Harvesting Mechanics

### Step 1: Monitor Preferred Stock Valuations

Preferred stocks trade based on three factors:

| Factor | Impact on Price |
|--------|---|
| Interest Rates | Inverse (rates up, prices down) |
| Credit Spread | Inverse (spreads wide, prices down) |
| Call Risk | Inverse (callable preferreds less valuable) |

**Harvesting Trigger**: When combination creates 8-15% declines

### Step 2: Identify Harvestable Positions

**Multi-Position Preferred Stock Portfolio**:

| Security | Entry Price | Current Price | Yield | Loss |
|----------|---|---|---|---|
| PFF | $24.00 | $20.50 | 5.6% | -14.6% |
| PFFA | $26.50 | $23.00 | 5.4% | -13.2% |
| PSK | $25.00 | $23.50 | 5.5% | -6% |
| JPM-F | $26.00 | $25.00 | 4.8% | -3.8% |

**Harvestable Positions**: PFF, PFFA have significant losses

**Total Harvestable**: $5,000-$8,000 on $150,000 position

### Step 3: Execute Wash-Sale Compliant Swaps

**PFF Harvest** (\$8,000 loss):

Correct alternatives:
- Switch to PFFA ✓ Different provider/structure
- Switch to PSK ✓ Different issuer composition
- Switch to individual preferred stocks ✓ Different security

Incorrect:
- Buy more PFF ✗ Substantially identical
- Switch to PFF-M (closed-end fund of PFF) ✗ Derived from same underlying

### Step 4: Continue Collecting Dividend Income

**Key Advantage of Preferred Stocks**:

You harvest losses while collecting 5-6% dividend income on the replacement security. This is superior to:
- Harvesting stocks (lower dividend)
- Harvesting bonds (no income during replacement period)

**Example**:
1. Own PFF at \$24, now worth \$20 (loss: \$4,000)
2. Sell PFF, harvest \$4,000 loss
3. Switch to PFFA (also 5.6% yield)
4. Collect \$1,400 annual dividend while waiting 31 days (no income loss)
5. After 31 days, can rebalance back to PFF if desired

The continuous dividend income makes preferred stock harvesting elegant.

## Preferred Stock Call Risk and Harvesting

**Key Risk**: Most preferred stocks are callable, meaning issuers can buy them back at set prices.

**Harvesting Impact**:

If you buy preferred stock trading at $23 with $25 call price, and it rallies to $24.50, issuer will likely call it away at $25. This limits upside.

**Strategy**: When harvesting losses and switching positions:
1. Avoid non-callable or long-dated call protected preferreds
2. Accept callable preferreds when on sale (discount to call price)
3. Harvest non-callable preferreds when underwater
4. Plan around call dates (don't expect above-call-price appreciation)

**Example**:
- PFF with $25 call price trading at $20 is harvestable
- Plan to hold replacement for 2-3 years (until callable)
- Accept that you won't profit above $25 (by design)

## Preferred Stock Ladder Strategy

**Create Multiple Preferred Holdings** by maturity:

| Position | Holding | Yield | Purpose |
|----------|---------|-------|---------|
| PFF | Broad preferred | 5.6% | Core income |
| PFFA | Active preferred | 5.4% | Harvesting alternative |
| FIXD | Fixed income ETF | 5.2% | Lower volatility |
| Individual preferred (JPM-F) | Single issuer | 4.8% | Specific credit exposure |

**Harvesting Advantage**: When PFF declines, switch to PFFA or FIXD. When those decline, switch back. This creates ongoing harvesting opportunities while maintaining income.

## FAQ

**Q: Are preferred stocks safe?**
A: Generally yes, but dividend cuts can occur during crises. 2008-2009 saw many preferreds cut dividends. Stick with investment-grade issuers (banks, utilities).

**Q: Should preferred stocks make up my entire income portfolio?**
A: No. Limit preferred to 25-35% of income portfolio. Diversify with dividend stocks, bonds, and alternatives.

**Q: Can I harvest preferred stock losses in my IRA?**
A: No. Tax-deferred accounts don't benefit from loss harvesting. Save preferred stocks for taxable accounts only.

**Q: What if a preferred stock is called away?**
A: You'll get the call price (usually $25), and you'll need to reinvest. Plan for this by building a preferred stock ladder where you're expecting calls periodically.

**Q: Do preferred stocks ever have wash-sale issues?**
A: Rarely, as long as you switch to genuinely different preferreds or funds. Switching between PFF, PFFA, and PSK is safe.
