## Calculating Sharpe Ratio for Sector ETFs

The Sharpe ratio quantifies excess returns relative to risk taken. For sector ETFs, this metric reveals whether sector returns appropriately compensate you for volatility undertaken, guiding optimal sector allocation and rotation timing.

## The Sharpe Ratio Formula

**Sharpe Ratio = (Average Return - Risk-Free Rate) / Standard Deviation of Returns**

## Understanding the Components

**Average Return:** Historical annualized sector ETF return
**Risk-Free Rate:** Yield of 10-year US Treasury (currently ~4.5%)
**Standard Deviation:** Measure of sector price volatility

## Worked Example: Sharpe Ratio Calculation

Calculate Sharpe ratio for a sector ETF:

**Data Collection (5-year period):**
- Annual returns: 12%, 18%, 8%, 16%, 14%
- Average: (12 + 18 + 8 + 16 + 14) / 5 = 13.6%
- Deviations: 1.6%, 4.4%, -5.6%, 2.4%, 0.4%
- Variance: (2.56 + 19.36 + 31.36 + 5.76 + 0.16) / 5 = 11.84
- Standard Deviation: sqrt(11.84) = 3.44%

**Sharpe Calculation:**
Sharpe Ratio = (13.6% - 4.5%) / 3.44%
Sharpe Ratio = 9.1% / 3.44%
**Sharpe Ratio = 2.646**

## Volatility Calculation Process

1. Collect 5-10 years of monthly sector ETF returns
2. Calculate average return across period
3. Calculate deviation of each return from average
4. Square each deviation
5. Sum squared deviations
6. Divide by number of periods (variance)
7. Take square root (standard deviation)

## Sector Rotation and Sharpe Ratios

Sharpe ratios change dramatically across economic cycles:

**Expansion Phase:**
- Tech Sharpe: 0.70+
- Healthcare Sharpe: 0.60+
- Utilities Sharpe: 0.30

**Contraction Phase:**
- Utilities Sharpe: 0.50+
- Healthcare Sharpe: 0.55+
- Tech Sharpe: 0.25

## Comparing Sectors Using Sharpe Ratio

When allocating across sectors:
1. Calculate 5-year and 10-year Sharpe ratios for each sector
2. Compare recent performance (1-2 years) to long-term
3. Evaluate economic cycle positioning
4. Consider valuation levels (high valuations reduce Sharpe)
5. Account for dividend yields

## Correlations and Portfolio Sharpe Ratio

Individual sector Sharpe ratios differ from portfolio impacts:
- Defensive sectors (utilities, staples) have low correlation
- Growth sectors (tech, discretionary) more correlated
- Mixing uncorrelated sectors improves portfolio Sharpe ratio
- Diversification across sectors critical for optimal returns

## FAQ

**Q: What's a good Sharpe ratio for sector ETFs?**
A: 0.4-0.6 is typical; above 0.6 indicates relative strength.

**Q: Should I overweight sectors with highest Sharpe ratios?**
A: Not necessarily. High Sharpe ratios don't predict future returns.

**Q: How do valuations affect sector Sharpe ratios?**
A: High valuations reduce future Sharpe ratios as growth expectations decrease.

**Q: Can I use sector Sharpe ratios to time rotations?**
A: Use as one factor, but combine with valuation, momentum, and macroeconomic analysis.

**Q: How often should I recalculate sector Sharpe ratios?**
A: Quarterly to assess evolving risk-return dynamics.

## Conclusion

The Sharpe ratio provides valuable framework for evaluating sector ETF allocations on risk-adjusted basis. By calculating and comparing Sharpe ratios across sectors, understanding cycle implications, and combining with other factors, you construct optimized sector allocation strategies.
