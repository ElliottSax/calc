## The Mechanics of Dividend Growth Investing

Dividend growth is not passive. It requires screening, selection, and rebalancing. The core metric is **Dividend Growth Rate (DGR)**:

```
DGR = [(Latest Dividend / Dividend n Years Ago)^(1/n) - 1] × 100
```

For example, Johnson & Johnson (JNJ) paid:
- $3.60/share in 2020
- $4.76/share in 2025

```
DGR = [(4.76 / 3.60)^(1/5) - 1] × 100 = 5.6% CAGR
```

JNJ has raised its dividend for **61 consecutive years**—a Dividend King—and is projected to grow payouts by **5.5% annually through 2026**.

## Risks to Dividend Growth in 2026

No strategy is risk-free. Key threats in 2026 include:

1. **Earnings Slowdown**: If S&P 500 EPS growth falls below 4%, dividend hikes may stall.
2. **Overvaluation**: VIG trades at 20.3x earnings—above its 10-year average (18.5x).
3. **Sector Concentration**: SCHD is 22% financials—vulnerable to credit stress if unemployment rises.
4. **Dividend Cuts**: Even "safe" stocks can falter. Exxon (XOM) cut its dividend in 2020 during oil crash.

Mitigation strategy:
- Limit any single stock to **≤5%** of portfolio.
- Monitor payout ratios—avoid companies above **65% for cyclicals**, **80% for utilities**.
- Diversify across sectors (consumer staples, tech, healthcare, energy).

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q: Is dividend growth still relevant in a low-interest-rate world?
**A**: Yes. Even with 10-year yields at 3.8%, dividend growth stocks offer **total returns of 8–10%** with income that rises over time. Bonds pay fixed coupons that lose to inflation.

### Q: What’s a safe dividend growth rate to assume in 2026?
**A**: 6–7% is realistic for a diversified portfolio. The S&P 500 Dividend Aristocrats Index grew payouts at **7.2% CAGR** from 2016–2025.

### Q: Should I prioritize yield or growth?
**A**: Prioritize growth. A 2.5% yield with 8% growth outearns a 4% yield with 2% growth by Year 10. Use ETFs like SCHD to balance both.

### Q: Can dividend growth protect against inflation?
**A**: Yes. Companies with pricing power (KO, JNJ) raise prices and dividends together. Over 20 years, dividend growers have outpaced inflation by **3–4% annually**.

### Q: What if a company cuts its dividend?
**A**: Sell immediately. Dividend cuts signal financial stress. Since 2000, S&P 500 stocks that cut dividends underperformed the index by **12% in the following 12 months** (J.P. Morgan study).

### Q: How much should I allocate to dividend growth?
**A**: For retirees: 40–60% of equities. For accumulation phase: 20–40%. Always maintain diversification.

### Q: Are tech stocks reliable dividend growers?
**A**: Increasingly yes. Microsoft (MSFT) has raised dividends for **13 straight years** (10.3% 5-yr DGR). Apple (AAPL) yields 0.5% but has 12% DGR. Use DGRO for exposure.
