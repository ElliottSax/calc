# REITs Dividend Yield Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

REITs are required by law to distribute 90% of taxable income as dividends, making them exceptional income vehicles. Dividend yield shows what percentage of your investment returns as cash annually. A $10,000 REIT investment with 5% yield generates $500 in annual dividends, providing meaningful passive income.

## REIT Dividend Yield Fundamentals

Unlike stocks where dividends are optional, REITs mandate distributions. This requirement creates predictable and reliable cash flow. Investors relying on income should track REIT yields carefully, as they vary significantly by property type and economic conditions.

## Why REIT Dividend Yield Is Critical

The mandatory 90% distribution requirement distinguishes REITs from regular stocks. This means REIT yields reflect real business economics rather than management discretion. Understanding yield variations helps investors select appropriate REITs for income goals.

## Dividend Yield Formula

**Dividend Yield = (Annual Dividends per Share / Current Share Price) × 100**

Example with Realty Income (O):
- Annual dividend: $2.76 per share
- Current price: $46.00
- Yield = ($2.76 / $46.00) × 100 = 6.0%

## REIT Dividend Yield Calculation Steps

1. Find annual dividend amount (usually quarterly payments × 4)
2. Get current share price from your broker
3. Divide dividend by price
4. Multiply by 100 for percentage
5. Compare to peers for context

For ETFs like SCHD, calculate weighted average yield of all holdings.

## Forward vs. Trailing Dividend Yield

**Trailing Yield** = Last 12 months of actual dividends (historical, proven)
**Forward Yield** = Expected next 12 months (projected, uncertain)

Forward yield matters more for investment decisions, but verify forecasts independently using company guidance.

## Tax Implications of REIT Dividends

REIT dividends are taxed as ordinary income (not capital gains), creating substantial tax bills:

- 22% marginal tax bracket: 5% yield becomes 3.9% after-tax
- 37% marginal tax bracket: 5% yield becomes 3.15% after-tax
- Consider REITs in tax-advantaged retirement accounts
- High-yield REIT funds (5-6% yield) generate significant tax bills in taxable accounts

## FAQ

**Q: Is a 6% REIT yield sustainable?**
A: Yes, if the REIT covers it through operations. Check payout ratios (dividends/FFO) below 80% for safety.

**Q: Can REIT yield fall?**
A: Yes. Economic downturns reduce property values and rents, forcing dividend cuts. Monitor occupancy rates.

**Q: Should I chase the highest REIT yield?**
A: No. High yield often signals distress or unsustainable distributions. Balanced 4-5% yields are safer than 7%+ offerings.

**Q: How do I calculate portfolio REIT yield?**
A: Sum all dividend income from REIT holdings, divide by total REIT investment, multiply by 100.

**Q: Are REIT yields better than bond yields?**
A: Currently comparable. Bonds yield 4-5%, REITs yield 3-5% in 2026. Consider tax treatment and diversification benefits.
