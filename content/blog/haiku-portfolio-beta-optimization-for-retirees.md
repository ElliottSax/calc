Retirees (65+) have 20-30 year time horizons despite retirement status, but prioritize capital preservation and income over growth. They cannot recover from major losses through earning additional income.

Target portfolio beta: 0.3-0.6 (significantly below market, income-focused).

## Sequence of Returns Risk for Retirees

Retirees face critical sequence-of-returns risk:

**Scenario A: Retire 1/1/2022 (market top, strong dollar)**

Year 1: Market down 20%, portfolio down 9.5% (beta 0.475)
- Starting: $1,000,000
- Decline: -$95,000
- Withdrawal: -$35,000
- Ending: $870,000

Recovery years take much longer because withdrawals coincide with market declines.

**Scenario B: Retire 1/1/2023 (market bottom, weak dollar)**

Year 1: Market up 20%, portfolio up 9.5% (beta 0.475)
- Starting: $1,000,000
- Gain: +$95,000
- Withdrawal: -$35,000
- Ending: $1,060,000

Early gains provide buffer for later declines.

This 10-year retirement gap illustrates sequence risk importance.

## Rebalancing Strategy for Retirees

Retirees should rebalance quarterly to maintain beta targets:

**Target beta: 0.45, allows 0.35-0.55 drift**

Quarterly review:

After strong equity market:
- Portfolio beta drifts to 0.58 (too aggressive)
- Sell appreciated stock positions
- Buy bonds/dividend stocks
- New beta: 0.45

After market correction:
- Portfolio beta drifts to 0.35 (too conservative)
- Sell bond positions
- Buy growth stocks
- New beta: 0.45

Automatic rebalancing ensures portfolio remains structured for withdrawals.

## Longevity Planning

Retirees with family longevity history should consider growth:

**Family history: Lives to 95 (30-year retirement from 65)**

If portfolio declines to zero in year 20:
- Years 1-20: Portfolio supports withdrawals
- Years 20-30: Only Social Security/other income available

Conservative beta (0.3-0.4) risk: Portfolio grows too slowly, insufficient for 30+ year need.

Moderate beta (0.45-0.55) better: Portfolio maintains purchasing power through growth.

## Annuity Considerations vs. Beta Optimization

Some retirees use annuities to reduce portfolio risk:

**Annuity + lower-beta portfolio strategy**

Allocate $400,000 to lifetime annuity:
- Provides $20,000/year guaranteed income (5%)
- Reduces portfolio to $600,000
- Allows beta 0.3 (very conservative) for remaining portfolio
- Lower sequence risk

Comparison:
- Pure portfolio: 0.45 beta, full market risk
- Annuity hybrid: 0.3 beta, more conservative but less growth

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Should retirees avoid stocks entirely?**
A: No. 20-30% stock allocation (beta 0.2-0.3) provides inflation protection. 100% bonds risk purchasing power erosion.

**Q2: Can retirees afford beta 0.6+?**
A: Only if very young (65 with family longevity) or with additional income sources (pensions, rental income).

**Q3: What beta suits a 75-year-old?**
A: 0.25-0.35 (very conservative). Shorter time horizon = less stock exposure.

**Q4: Should I rebalance during market crashes?**
A: Yes. Buy stocks at discounts (rebalance from bonds to stocks at lower prices).

**Q5: How much portfolio growth should I expect (3% withdrawal + 0.4 beta)?**
A: With 7% market returns and 0.4 beta = 2.8% portfolio return. With 3% withdrawal, portfolio declines 0.2% annually—manageable for 30 years.
