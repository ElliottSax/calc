## STRATEGIC FRAMEWORK

The dividend ladder strategy involves creating a 12-stock portfolio with quarterly payers, staggering their ex-dividend dates to ensure a consistent monthly income stream. The core principles of this strategy are:

* **Quarterly payers**: Select stocks with a history of quarterly dividend payments
* **Staggered ex-dividend dates**: Spread the ex-dividend dates across the month to create a seamless cash flow
* **12-stock portfolio**: Construct a portfolio with 12 stocks, each paying dividends on a different date
* **Monthly dividend income**: Calculate the total monthly dividend income from the portfolio

To implement the dividend ladder strategy, you'll need to select stocks that meet the following criteria:

| Criteria | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Dividend yield | 3% or higher |
| Dividend growth rate | 5% or higher over the past 5 years |
| Ex-dividend date | Quarterly, with a staggered schedule |
| Payout ratio | 50% or lower |
| Market capitalization | $5 billion or higher |

## IMPLEMENTATION STEPS

To implement the dividend ladder strategy, follow these steps:

1. **Select the right stocks**: Identify 12 quarterly payers that meet the selection criteria
2. **Determine the ex-dividend dates**: Spread the ex-dividend dates across the month, ensuring a consistent cash flow
3. **Construct the portfolio**: Allocate the selected stocks to the portfolio, ensuring a balanced mix of industries and sectors
4. **Calculate the monthly dividend income**: Add up the total monthly dividend income from the portfolio
5. **Monitor and adjust**: Regularly review the portfolio and adjust the stocks as needed to maintain the desired dividend income

## FIVE FAQs

1. **Q: How do I select the right stocks for my dividend ladder?**
A: Look for stocks with a history of quarterly dividend payments, a dividend yield of 3% or higher, and a dividend growth rate of 5% or higher over the past 5 years.
2. **Q: How do I stagger the ex-dividend dates?**
A: Spread the ex-dividend dates across the month, ensuring a consistent cash flow. You can use a dividend calendar or a spreadsheet to plan the ex-dividend dates.
3. **Q: How do I calculate the monthly dividend income?**
A: Add up the total monthly dividend income from the portfolio by multiplying the dividend yield by the portfolio value.
4. **Q: Can I use the dividend ladder strategy in a taxable account?**
A: Yes, but consider the tax implications of dividend income and adjust the portfolio accordingly.
5. **Q: How often should I review and adjust my dividend ladder portfolio?**
A: Regularly review and adjust the portfolio every 6-12 months to maintain the desired dividend income and asset allocation.
