# Eli Lilly Dividend Stock: Pharma Growth and Income

Eli Lilly and Company (LLY) represents a unique blend of growth and income, combining blockbuster diabetes/obesity drugs with a modest but growing dividend. While the current 0.7% yield won't attract pure income investors, LLY's position in the $150+ billion obesity market and consistent dividend growth make it compelling for total return-focused portfolios.

## Key Takeaways

- LLY yields 0.65-0.75%, low but backed by explosive revenue growth
- 10+ consecutive years of dividend increases with 10-12% annual growth
- Payout ratio under 25% provides massive room for future increases
- Mounjaro and Zepbound (GLP-1 drugs) generate $15+ billion annually
- Pipeline strength and obesity market leadership drive long-term dividend sustainability

## Dividend Metrics

**Current**: $0.75 quarterly ($3.00 annual)
**Yield**: 0.72%
**Growth Rate**: 11.4% (5-year)
**Payout Ratio**: 22%

## Blockbuster Drug Portfolio

**Mounjaro/Zepbound** (tirzepatide):
- Diabetes Revenue: $8.2B (2025)
- Obesity Revenue: $6.8B (2025)
- Peak Potential: $30B+ (2030)

**Trulicity** (GLP-1):
- Revenue: $6.5B (declining as Mounjaro grows)

**Verzenio** (oncology):
- Revenue: $4.2B
- Growth: 20%+ annually

## Future Projections

**2027**: $3.50/share (+17%)
**2030**: $5.25/share (+15% CAGR)

Low payout ratio (22%) allows aggressive increases as revenue grows.

## Why Lilly Keeps Payout Low

Management priorities:
1. R&D investment ($9B+ annually)
2. Manufacturing capacity expansion
3. Strategic acquisitions
4. Buybacks ($5B+ annually)
5. Dividends (conservative approach)

## Investment Thesis

**Buy LLY for**:
- Obesity market exposure
- Dividend growth potential (10-15%)
- Strong pipeline
- Total return opportunity

**Don't buy for**:
- Current income (0.7% yield insufficient)

## Conclusion

Eli Lilly prioritizes growth over immediate income, making it unsuitable for retirees needing current cash flow. However, for dividend growth investors with long time horizons, LLY's combination of 11%+ dividend growth, 22% payout ratio, and dominant position in the $150B obesity market creates compelling long-term value. Consider LLY as a growth stock with dividend characteristics rather than a traditional income investment.
