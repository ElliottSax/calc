# REITs with Highest Dividend Yields: 2026 Complete Guide

Real Estate Investment Trusts are among the highest-yielding investments available in public markets. By law, REITs must distribute at least 90% of their taxable income to shareholders, creating structurally high dividend yields. In 2026, with interest rates having stabilized, many REITs offer compelling yields that significantly exceed the S&P 500 average. This guide covers the highest-yielding REITs across every subsector and helps you identify which are worth owning.

## Why REITs Pay High Dividends

REITs were created by Congress in 1960 to give individual investors access to income-producing real estate. The defining feature is the distribution requirement: to maintain REIT tax status, a company must distribute at least 90% of its taxable income as dividends. This mandate creates yields that typically range from 3% to 12%, far above most other equity investments.

REITs generate income through:

- **Rent collection** from commercial, residential, or industrial tenants
- **Interest income** from mortgage-backed securities (mortgage REITs)
- **Property appreciation** and development gains
- **Lease escalators** that increase rents annually

## Highest-Yielding Equity REITs

Equity REITs own and operate physical properties. They tend to be more stable than mortgage REITs because their income comes from real rent payments.

- **Lease Escalators:** CPI-linked annual increases
- **Investment Grade:** Yes (BBB+)

### W.P. Carey (WPC) — Yield: ~5.8%

W.P. Carey is a diversified net-lease REIT with 1,400+ properties across the U.S. and Europe. Industrial, warehouse, and essential retail properties dominate the portfolio.

- **Property Type:** Diversified (industrial, warehouse, retail)

## Highest-Yielding Mortgage REITs

Mortgage REITs (mREITs) invest in mortgage-backed securities rather than physical properties. They use significant leverage to amplify yields, making them higher yielding but also more volatile.

### AGNC Investment (AGNC) — Yield: ~14.5%

AGNC invests primarily in agency mortgage-backed securities guaranteed by Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac. The government guarantee eliminates credit risk, but interest rate risk remains significant.

- **Portfolio:** Agency MBS (government-guaranteed)
- **Leverage:** Typically 7-9x equity
- **Payout Frequency:** Monthly
- **Risk:** High sensitivity to interest rate changes and yield curve shape

### Annaly Capital Management (NLY) — Yield: ~13.0%

Annaly is the largest mortgage REIT by market cap. It primarily invests in agency MBS but also has credit and residential lending segments.

- **Portfolio:** Agency MBS (primarily) with credit diversification
- **Leverage:** Similar to AGNC
- **Market Cap:** ~$10 billion
- **Risk:** Interest rate and prepayment risk

### Starwood Property Trust (STWD) — Yield: ~9.5%

Starwood differs from pure agency mREITs by focusing on commercial real estate debt, CMBS, and property ownership. This diversification reduces pure interest rate sensitivity.

- **Portfolio:** Commercial mortgage loans, CMBS, and owned properties
- **Diversification:** More diversified than pure agency mREITs
- **Managed By:** Starwood Capital Group (Barry Sternlicht)
- **Risk:** Commercial real estate credit risk

## Tax Implications of REIT Dividends

REIT dividends are generally taxed as ordinary income rather than at the lower qualified dividend rate. This is because REITs pass through rental income, which does not qualify for preferential tax treatment.

However, the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act provides a 20% deduction on qualified REIT dividends through Section 199A, effectively reducing the tax rate. For a taxpayer in the 24% bracket, the effective rate on REIT dividends is approximately 19.2%.

**Tax optimization strategies:**

- Hold REITs in tax-advantaged accounts (IRAs, 401(k)s) when possible
- Offset REIT income with capital losses in taxable accounts
- Consider the Section 199A deduction when calculating after-tax yields

## How to Evaluate REIT Safety

Not all high-yield REITs are safe. Use these metrics to assess sustainability:

- **AFFO Payout Ratio:** Below 85% for equity REITs provides a safety margin
- **Occupancy Rate:** Above 95% indicates strong demand for properties
- **Weighted Average Lease Term (WALT):** Longer is better — 7+ years is solid
- **Debt-to-EBITDA:** Below 6x for equity REITs
- **Credit Rating:** Investment grade (BBB- or better) indicates financial stability
- **Fixed vs. Variable Rate Debt:** More fixed-rate debt reduces interest rate risk

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Are REITs good investments in 2026?

REITs are attractive in 2026 as interest rates have stabilized and many REITs trade at discounts to net asset value. The combination of high yields, growing rents, and potential for property value appreciation makes REITs compelling for income investors.

### Why are mortgage REIT yields so much higher?

Mortgage REITs use significant leverage (7-9x equity) to amplify returns on their MBS portfolios. This leverage magnifies both gains and losses, resulting in higher yields but greater volatility. The elevated yield compensates investors for this additional risk.

### Can REIT dividends be cut?

Yes. REITs can and do cut dividends when property values decline, occupancy drops, or tenants default. During the 2020 pandemic, several retail and hotel REITs reduced or suspended dividends. Always evaluate the AFFO payout ratio and balance sheet strength before investing.

### What is the best REIT ETF?

The Vanguard Real Estate ETF (VNQ) provides broad REIT exposure with a ~3.8% yield and 0.12% expense ratio. For higher yield, the iShares Mortgage Real Estate ETF (REM) yields approximately 9% but carries more risk.

### Should I invest in REITs in my IRA or taxable account?

Generally, REITs are better held in IRAs or other tax-advantaged accounts because their dividends are taxed as ordinary income. However, the Section 199A deduction partially offsets this disadvantage in taxable accounts. If you hold both stocks and REITs, prioritize REITs for tax-sheltered placement.
