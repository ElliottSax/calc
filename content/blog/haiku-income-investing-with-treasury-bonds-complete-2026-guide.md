# Income Investing with Treasury Bonds: Complete 2026 Guide

Treasury bonds provide safe, stable income for conservative investors. This guide explains Treasury characteristics, evaluates options, and shows how to build Treasury-based income portfolios.

## What Are Treasury Bonds?

Treasury bonds represent US government debt backed by the full faith and credit of the United States:

**Key Characteristics:**
- Safety: Zero default risk (US government backing)
- Liquidity: Highly liquid, daily trading
- Tax treatment: Federal tax, state tax-exempt
- Yield: 4.2-4.6% currently (2026)
- Maturity: 2-year notes through 30-year bonds

## Treasury Bond Types

**Treasury Bills (T-Bills):**
- Maturity: 1 year or less
- Yield: 5.0-5.2%
- Risk: Minimal
- Income purpose: Minimal (mainly price return)

**Treasury Notes (T-Notes):**
- Maturity: 2-10 years
- Yield: 4.2-4.5%
- Risk: Low (rate sensitivity)
- Income purpose: Moderate

**Treasury Bonds (T-Bonds):**
- Maturity: 20-30 years
- Yield: 4.5-4.7%
- Risk: Moderate (high rate sensitivity)
- Income purpose: Good (highest yields)

## Calculating Treasury Bond Income

**Example: $50,000 in 10-year Treasury at 4.5% yield**

Annual income: $50,000 × 4.5% = $2,250

If rates fall 50 basis points:
- Price appreciation: ~4% = $2,000
- Total return: (2,250 + 2,000) / 50,000 = 8.5%

If rates rise 50 basis points:
- Price depreciation: ~4% = -$2,000
- Total return: (2,250 - 2,000) / 50,000 = 0.5%

Rate outlook critically impacts Treasury returns.

## Building a Treasury Bond Portfolio

**Conservative Approach:**
- 70% Treasury bonds (mix of maturities)
- 30% Treasury inflation-protected securities (TIPS)
- Blended yield: 4.3%
- Safety focus: Highest (pure government)

**Income Approach:**
- 50% Intermediate Treasury (5-10 year)
- 30% Long-term Treasury (20-30 year)
- 20% Treasury bills
- Blended yield: 4.4%
- Laddered approach: Reduces reinvestment risk

## Treasury Bond Ladder Strategy

Build laddered Treasury portfolio:

| Maturity | Amount | Yield | Annual Income |

## Tax Considerations

**Treasury Tax Treatment:**
- Federal income tax: Yes (full taxation)
- State/local income tax: No (exempt)
- Tax-advantaged accounts: Minimal benefit

**Placement Strategy:**
- Taxable accounts: Use Treasury ladders (tax-efficient)
- 401k/IRA: Bonds acceptable but not primary focus
- Overall: Treasuries work in any account

## Comparing Treasury Options

**Direct Treasury Purchase:**
- Safety: Highest
- Yields: Current (4.5%)
- Liquidity: Good
- Ladder potential: Excellent
- Complexity: Moderate

**Treasury ETFs (TLT, VGIT, SHV):**
- Safety: High
- Yields: Close to direct (within 0.1%)
- Liquidity: Excellent
- Ladder potential: Less effective
- Complexity: Low

**Treasury Funds:**
- Safety: High
- Yields: Slightly lower (fees)
- Liquidity: Daily pricing
- Management: Active or passive
- Complexity: Low

Most income investors prefer laddered direct Treasury purchases for maximum control.

## Interest Rate Risk

Treasury prices inversely correlate with rates:

**Rising Rate Scenario:**
- 10-year Treasury declines 4% per 1% rate move
- 30-year Treasury declines 18% per 1% rate move
- Income: Unaffected (coupons continue)
- Total return: Negative

**Falling Rate Scenario:**
- Prices appreciate same magnitudes
- Income: Unaffected
- Total return: Positive

Duration management (shorter/longer bonds) adjusts rate risk.

## FAQ

**Q: Are Treasury bonds appropriate for income investors?**
A: Yes. Treasuries provide safe, government-backed income with no default risk.

**Q: Why own Treasuries when stock dividends yield 3-4%?**
A: Safety, liquidity, and predictability. Treasuries suit risk-averse investors prioritizing capital preservation.

**Q: Should I ladder Treasuries or buy a Treasury fund?**
A: Ladders offer greater control and tax efficiency; funds offer simplicity. Choose based on preferences.

**Q: What Treasury maturity maximizes income?**
A: Currently, 20-30 year Treasuries yield 4.5-4.7% (highest yields). Intermediate Treasuries offer better rate-risk balance.

**Q: How much of my portfolio should be Treasury bonds?**
A: 20-50% depending on risk tolerance. Conservative investors: 50%+. Moderate: 25-40%. Aggressive: <20%.
