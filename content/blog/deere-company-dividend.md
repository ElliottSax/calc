# Deere & Company Dividend: Agriculture Equipment Income Stock

Deere & Company (DE), known globally as John Deere, dominates agricultural and construction equipment with an 18+ year dividend increase streak and a business model increasingly focused on high-margin precision agriculture technology. With a 1.2% yield, strong dividend growth potential, and exposure to global food production trends, Deere offers cyclical industrial income with technology upside.

## Key Takeaways

- Deere has increased dividends for 18 consecutive years, approaching Dividend Aristocrat status
- Current yield of 1.1-1.3% modest but backed by precision agriculture transformation
- Payout ratio of 20-24% among the lowest of quality dividend stocks
- Free cash flow of $6-8 billion supports dividends and technology R&D
- Smart farming and autonomy drive margin expansion and premium pricing

## Dividend Metrics (2026)

**Quarterly**: $1.47
**Annual**: $5.88
**Yield**: 1.18%
**Payout Ratio**: 22%
**Growth (5-year)**: 11.2%

## Business Transformation

**Traditional Equipment** (60% of profit):
- Tractors, combines, planters
- Construction equipment
- Forestry machinery

**Precision Agriculture** (40% of profit, growing):
- See & Spray technology (targeted herbicide application)
- AutoTrac guidance systems
- Operations Center data platform
- Subscription software ($1.5B+ annual recurring revenue)

**Margin Impact**:
- Equipment Operating Margin: 15-16%
- Precision Ag Margin: 40%+
- Company avg rising to 20%+ (from 18%)

## Agricultural Cycle Considerations

Deere is cyclical—influenced by:
- Crop prices (corn, soybeans, wheat)
- Farm income levels
- Weather patterns
- Trade policy

**BUT**: Technology shift provides stability through cycles as farmers upgrade for efficiency.

## Investment Thesis

**Buy DE for**:
- Dividend growth potential (10-12%)
- Precision ag transformation
- Ultra-low payout ratio (22%)
- Subscription revenue growth

**Risks**:
- Agricultural commodity price sensitivity
- Farm income volatility
- Trade war impacts (China tariffs)
- Weather/climate variability

## Conclusion

Deere represents a dividend growth opportunity disguised as a cyclical industrial. The 1.2% yield won't attract income investors, but the 10-12% growth potential, 22% payout ratio, and transformation into a precision agriculture technology company create compelling long-term value. The subscription software model (40%+ margins) increasingly stabilizes earnings through agricultural cycles. Best for investors seeking dividend growth exposure to global food production trends with technology upside.
