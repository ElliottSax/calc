## Tax-Loss Harvesting Strategy

Tax-loss harvesting strategically sells securities at a loss to offset capital gains and reduce taxable income. This advanced strategy transforms market downturns into tax savings and wealth acceleration.

### How Tax-Loss Harvesting Works

When you sell investments at a loss, you can:
1. Offset realized capital gains dollar-for-dollar (unlimited)
2. Deduct remaining losses against ordinary income ($3,000/year)
3. Carry unused losses forward indefinitely
4. Immediately reinvest in similar (not substantially identical) securities

### Understanding the Wash-Sale Rule

You cannot immediately repurchase the same security within 30 days. However, you can shift to similar alternatives:

| Original Position | Wash-Sale Safe Alternatives |
|------------------|---------------------------|
| VTI | SWTSX, FSKAX, SCHB |
| VOO | SPLG, IVOO, IVV |
| VXUS | IXUS, IEFA, VEA |
| BND | SCHZ, VBTLX, AGG |
| SCHD | VYM, DGRO, DVY |

### Tax-Loss Harvesting Mathematics

**Tax saved = Harvested loss × Marginal tax rate**

For a high-income earner in 37% bracket:
- Harvest $50,000 loss = $18,500 immediate tax savings

### Real-World Example

A high-income earner with $400,000 portfolio:

**December market downturn:**
- VTI position loss: $35,000
- VOO position loss: $22,500
- SCHD position loss: $18,000
- **Total harvestable: $75,500**

**Concurrent capital gains:**
- Realized capital gains: $180,000
- After harvesting: $180,000 - $75,500 = $104,500 taxable
- **Immediate tax savings: $27,925 (37% × $75,500)**

**Reinvestment strategy:**
- Sell VTI at loss, immediately buy SWTSX (maintains full exposure)
- Sell VOO at loss, immediately buy SPLG (maintains full exposure)
- Portfolio composition unchanged, losses captured

### Q: When should I start withdrawing (4% rule)?

The 4% rule states: In retirement, withdraw 4% of your portfolio annually.

Example:
- Portfolio at retirement: $2,000,000
- Annual withdrawal: $2,000,000 × 0.04 = $80,000/year
- Historical success: 95% of 30-year retirements

Only start withdrawing when your portfolio reaches 25× your annual expenses.

### Q: Should I adjust my allocation as I age?

Yes. Use the simple rule: **Equity % = 110 - Your Age**

Examples:

- Age 55: 55% stocks, 45% bonds
- Age 65: 45% stocks, 55% bonds
- Age 75: 35% stocks, 65% bonds

This automatically becomes more conservative as you have fewer years to recover from downturns.
