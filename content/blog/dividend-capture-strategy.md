## Introduction
The dividend capture strategy is a popular investment approach that involves buying stocks or exchange-traded funds (ETFs) just before their ex-dividend dates and selling them immediately after. This strategy aims to capitalize on the dividend payout while minimizing the holding period, thereby reducing exposure to market volatility. As of 2026, many dividend-paying stocks and ETFs offer attractive yields, making this strategy an appealing option for income-seeking investors. For instance, the Vanguard Dividend Appreciation ETF (VDAIX) offers a dividend yield of 2.35% and a payout ratio of 45%, while the SPDR S&P 500 Dividend ETF (SDY) offers a dividend yield of 2.65% and a payout ratio of 50%.

## Strategy
To implement the dividend capture strategy, investors should follow these steps: (1) identify dividend-paying stocks and ETFs with attractive yields and payout ratios, (2) analyze the dividend payout schedules to determine the ex-dividend dates, (3) buy the stocks or ETFs just before the ex-dividend dates, and (4) sell the stocks or ETFs immediately after the ex-dividend dates. For example, if an investor wants to capture the dividend payout of the 3M (MMM) stock, which has an ex-dividend date of March 20, 2026, they should buy the stock on March 19, 2026, and sell it on March 21, 2026. By following this strategy, investors can capitalize on the dividend payout while minimizing their holding period and reducing their exposure to market volatility.
