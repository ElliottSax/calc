# How to Calculate Sortino Ratio for Small-Cap Funds in 2026

Small-cap funds deliver growth potential but exhibit substantial volatility. The Sortino Ratio reveals which small-cap funds protect downside most effectively during market stress. This comprehensive guide teaches you to calculate and interpret Sortino for small-cap investing.

## Understanding Small-Cap Volatility

Small-cap stocks and funds show amplified price swings compared to large-caps. Market stress, earnings surprises, and liquidity constraints create asymmetric downside risk. The Sortino Ratio specifically isolates downside movements without penalizing positive surprises.

**The Formula:**

**Sortino Ratio = (Return - Target Return) / Downside Deviation**

For small-cap investors, the target return typically matches the risk-free rate (4.5%) plus expected inflation (2-3%), totaling 6.5-7.5% annually.

## Small-Cap Volatility Patterns

Small-cap funds exhibit characteristic volatility:

**Monthly returns example:**
Jan: 8.2%, Feb: -6.4%, Mar: 5.1%, Apr: 3.8%, May: -7.2%, Jun: 6.5%, Jul: -3.1%, Aug: 4.9%, Sep: 9.3%, Oct: -8.7%, Nov: 7.2%, Dec: 5.6%

Average: 2.36% monthly
Target return: 7.0% annually / 12 = 0.583% monthly

Notice the pronounced swings both above and below target. The Sortino Ratio captures only the downside surprises.

## Calculating Downside Deviation for Small-Caps

**Identifying shortfalls (returns below 0.583% monthly):**

Jan: 8.2% (no shortfall), Feb: -6.4% (shortfall 6.983%), Mar: 5.1% (no), Apr: 3.8% (no), May: -7.2% (7.783%), Jun: 6.5% (no), Jul: -3.1% (3.683%), Aug: 4.9% (no), Sep: 9.3% (no), Oct: -8.7% (9.283%), Nov: 7.2% (no), Dec: 5.6% (no)

Squared shortfalls: 48.76 + 60.58 + 13.56 + 86.17 = 209.07
Average: 209.07 / 12 = 17.42
Downside deviation: √17.42 = 4.17% monthly

Annualized downside deviation: 4.17% × √12 = 14.44%

## FAQ

**Q: Why do small-cap funds show lower Sortino than large-caps?**
A: Small-caps exhibit higher downside deviation due to lower liquidity, higher business risk, and greater sensitivity to economic cycles. Lower Sortino reflects this reality.

**Q: What target return should I use for small-cap Sortino?**
A: Use 6.5-7.5% annually (risk-free rate plus inflation). This represents returns reasonably achievable with disciplined diversification.

**Q: Should I avoid small-caps with negative Sortino ratios?**
A: Not necessarily. Negative Sortino indicates returns below target, but may reflect temporary weakness. Compare to rolling averages and sector benchmarks.

**Q: How does sector concentration affect small-cap Sortino?**
A: Concentrated tech-heavy small-cap funds show higher Sortino during bull markets but worse downside during corrections. Diversified small-cap funds show more stable Sortino.

**Q: Can I use Sortino to time small-cap allocation?**
A: Yes. Decrease allocation when Sortino deteriorates significantly; increase when Sortino exceeds sector average plus one standard deviation.
