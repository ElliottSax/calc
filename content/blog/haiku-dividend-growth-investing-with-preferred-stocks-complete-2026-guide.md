## Understanding Preferred Stocks

Preferred stocks occupy the middle ground between bonds and common stocks:
- Fixed dividend yields (4-7%)
- Senior to common stock in bankruptcy (greater safety)
- Subordinate to bonds (more risk than bonds)
- Limited price appreciation potential
- Higher income than bonds or common stocks

Preferred stocks provide consistent income with less volatility than common stocks.

## Preferred Stock Characteristics

### Dividend Income

Preferred dividends are typically:
- Fixed and stable (rarely increase)
- Cumulative (unpaid dividends accumulate)
- Paid quarterly
- Tax-advantaged (treated as qualified dividends at 15%)

A preferred stock yielding 5% provides $50 annually per $1,000 invested.

### Price Behavior

Preferred stock prices move inversely to interest rates:
- Rates rise: Preferred prices fall (existing high yields less valuable)
- Rates fall: Preferred prices rise (fixed yields more valuable)

Preferred stocks behave more like bonds than common stocks.

### Call Risk

Many preferred stocks are callable:
- Issuer can redeem at par value after fixed date
- If redeemed, you lose price appreciation
- Limits upside in falling rate environment

Understand call dates and call prices.

## Preferred Stock Risks

### Interest Rate Risk

Rising interest rates reduce preferred prices:

**Example:**
- Buy PFF at 5.8% yield = $100/share
- Interest rates rise; PFF yield now 6.5%
- Market reprices PFF to $89/share
- You have $11,000 loss if forced to sell

This interest rate risk is significant in rising rate environment.

### Call Risk

If called (redeemed at par):
- Preferred purchased at premium: Lose difference
- Lose future dividend opportunity

**Example:**
- Buy preferred at $105 yielding 5.2%
- After 5 years, called at $100 par
- Loss: $500 per share = 10% haircut
- Offsets 5 years of gains

### Credit Risk

Preferred stock issuer defaults:
- Preferred holders still lose (senior to common, but behind bonds)
- Diversified ETF (PFF) mitigates this risk
- Individual preferred stocks carry real default risk

PFF diversification across 600+ holdings minimizes credit risk.

## FAQ

**Should preferred stocks replace bonds in my portfolio?**
Partially. Preferred stocks offer 1.5-2% more yield with similar volatility. Use 50/50 preferred/bonds for optimal balance.

**Are preferred stocks good for retirement accounts?**
Yes, excellent for Roth IRAs. Tax-free compounding of 5.5%+ yield is powerful.

**What's the downside to preferred stocks compared to dividend growth?**
No price appreciation (0% vs 5% for SCHD). Over 30 years, dividend growth stocks substantially outperform.

**Can preferred stock prices go negative?**
No. You can only lose up to your initial investment. Price floor is roughly par value minus any credit risk.

**Should I buy individual preferred stocks or PFF ETF?**
PFF ETF is superior. Diversification across 600+ holdings eliminates single-issuer credit risk.
