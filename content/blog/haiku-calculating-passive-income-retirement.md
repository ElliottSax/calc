# Calculating Passive Income for Retirement

Retirement planning hinges on a single critical calculation: determining how much passive income your portfolio generates. This practical guide reveals the formulas, worksheets, and strategies to calculate exactly how much retirement income you can generate from your investments.

## The Retirement Income Calculation

The foundation of retirement planning is simple arithmetic:

**Annual Passive Income = Portfolio Value × Average Yield**

Examples:
- $500,000 portfolio × 3% yield = $15,000 annual income
- $1,000,000 portfolio × 4% yield = $40,000 annual income
- $2,000,000 portfolio × 3.5% yield = $70,000 annual income

This formula assumes you're generating income from dividends and interest rather than selling shares.

## Calculating Required Portfolio Size

If you know your retirement income need, calculate backwards:

**Required Portfolio = Desired Annual Income ÷ Expected Yield**

Examples for $50,000 annual retirement income:
- At 2% yield: $50,000 ÷ 0.02 = $2,500,000 required
- At 3% yield: $50,000 ÷ 0.03 = $1,667,000 required
- At 4% yield: $50,000 ÷ 0.04 = $1,250,000 required
- At 5% yield: $50,000 ÷ 0.05 = $1,000,000 required

Higher yield requirements reduce needed capital but increase risk.

## Blended Yield Calculation

Most retirement portfolios combine multiple asset types with different yields:

**Blended Yield Formula:**

**Blended Yield = (Asset 1 Value × Asset 1 Yield%) + (Asset 2 Value × Asset 2 Yield%) ÷ Total Portfolio Value**

**Example Portfolio ($500,000):**

| Asset | Value | Yield | Contribution |
|-------|-------|-------|-------------|
| SCHD ETF | $150,000 | 3.4% | 1.02% |
| VYM ETF | $100,000 | 2.8% | 0.56% |
| JNJ Stock | $75,000 | 2.9% | 0.44% |
| VZ Stock | $75,000 | 6.4% | 1.92% |
| Bond ETF | $100,000 | 4.5% | 0.90% |
| **Total** | **$500,000** | **—** | **4.84%** |

**Annual Passive Income: $500,000 × 4.84% = $24,200**

## Calculating Capital + Income Strategy

Many retirees combine dividend income with modest capital withdrawals:

**Formula: Total Annual Income = Dividend Income + (Capital Withdrawal %)**

**Example: $750,000 portfolio**
- Dividend income available: 3.5% = $26,250/year
- Desired retirement income: $35,000/year
- Capital withdrawal needed: $35,000 - $26,250 = $8,750
- Capital withdrawal percentage: $8,750 ÷ $750,000 = 1.17%
- Total withdrawal rate: 3.5% + 1.17% = 4.67%

This strategy is sustainable if:
- Stock market returns average 7-8% annually
- You don't exceed 4.5% total withdrawal rate
- You're flexible in down market years

## Tax-Advantaged Retirement Income Calculation

Tax efficiency dramatically impacts retirement income:

**Scenario: $1,000,000 portfolio, $40,000 annual need**

**Taxable Account Strategy:**
- Dividend income: $40,000 (3.4% yield on diversified portfolio)
- Federal tax (qualified dividends): 15% = $6,000
- State tax (5%): $2,000
- Total tax: $8,000
- Net retirement income: $32,000

**Tax-Deferred Account Strategy:**
- Generate $40,000 from pre-tax sources
- IRA withdrawal: $40,000 (taxed as ordinary income, but no current dividend tax)
- Federal tax (24% bracket): $9,600
- State tax (5%): $2,000
- Total tax: $11,600
- Net retirement income: $28,400

**Roth IRA Strategy:**
- Generate $40,000 from tax-free sources
- Roth distribution: $40,000
- Federal tax: $0
- State tax: $0
- Total tax: $0
- Net retirement income: $40,000

Location matters significantly for tax-efficient retirement income.

## Social Security Integration

Calculate retirement income including Social Security:

**Total Retirement Income = Portfolio Income + Social Security**

**Example:**

Many retirees need far less portfolio capital because Social Security covers base expenses.

## Calculating Sustainable Withdrawal Rates

The "4% rule" suggests withdrawing 4% of your portfolio in year 1, then adjusting for inflation:

**Application:**
- $1,000,000 portfolio
- Year 1 withdrawal: $1,000,000 × 0.04 = $40,000
- Year 2 withdrawal: $40,000 × 1.03 = $41,200
- Year 3 withdrawal: $41,200 × 1.03 = $42,436
- Continue increasing by inflation

This historically succeeds in 90%+ of market scenarios over 30-year retirements.

**More Conservative 3% Rule:**
- More sustainable in uncertain environments
- $1,000,000 × 0.03 = $30,000 year 1
- Better for early retirees with 40+ year horizons

## FAQs

**Q: What's a safe retirement income percentage from portfolio?**
A: The 4% rule (4% first year, adjusted for inflation) succeeds 90%+ of the time. More conservative investors use 3-3.5%.

**Q: Should I calculate retirement income before or after taxes?**
A: Calculate both. Gross income is portfolio-generated amount; net is what remains after taxes.

**Q: How often should I recalculate retirement income?**
A: Annually. Market changes affect portfolio value and yields; update projections based on actual results.

**Q: What if I retire before age 65?**
A: You'll need a longer runway for portfolio to last. Reduce initial withdrawal rate to 3% or use bond bridge strategy until Social Security starts.

**Q: Can dividend yields change during retirement?**
A: Yes. Companies cut or increase dividends based on earnings. Plan conservatively using historical yields, not peak yields.

**Q: Should I include growth stocks in retirement income portfolio?**
A: Yes, 20-30% in growth stocks provides inflation protection. Pure income portfolios struggle with 20+ year retirements.

**Q: How do I calculate retirement income with both pensions and portfolio?**
A: Add all income sources. Pension + portfolio income should exceed your needs comfortably.
