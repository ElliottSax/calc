# 2026 Guide: Buy and Hold for Wealth Preservation

Wealth preservation through buy-and-hold means creating a portfolio at retirement and not touching it for 20-30 years except for withdrawals.

## The Preservation Buy-and-Hold Difference

Accumulation buy-and-hold: Add money regularly, rebalance annually, reinvest everything.

Preservation buy-and-hold: Create portfolio, withdraw money, rebalance rarely, hold most positions until death.

### Formula: Preservation Portfolio Target Allocation

```
Preservation Portfolio = (Annual Expenses × 25) × Conservative Allocation
Conservative = 45% Stocks, 35% Bonds, 20% Alternatives/Cash
```

**Worked Example:**

Annual expenses: $80,000
FI number: $80,000 × 25 = $2,000,000

**Allocation for preservation:**
- Stocks (45%): $900,000
  - VTI: $400,000
  - VXUS: $200,000
  - SCHD: $300,000
- Bonds (35%): $700,000
  - BND: $500,000
  - VGSH: $200,000 (short-term Treasuries)
- Alternatives/Cash (20%): $400,000
  - Dividend aristocrats: $200,000 (JNJ, MCD, PG, KO)
  - Money market/Cash: $200,000

**Annual income from this allocation:**
- VTI dividend: $400K × 1.4% = $5,600
- VXUS dividend: $200K × 2.2% = $4,400
- SCHD dividend: $300K × 3.5% = $10,500
- Bond interest: $700K × 4.5% = $31,500
- Dividend stocks: $200K × 2.7% = $5,400
- Money market: $200K × 5% = $10,000
- **Total: $67,400 annually**

This covers $67,400 of the $80,000 need, with $12,600 coming from principal decline (sustainable).

**Step 4: Set up automatic withdrawal**

**Completed. Now you're done. For 30 years.**

### The 30-Year Preservation Phase (No Action Required)

**Year 1-10:** Portfolio generates returns, withdrawals reduce balance slightly

**Year 11-20:** Portfolio may grow despite withdrawals (if returns exceed withdrawal rate)

**Year 21-30:** Portfolio continues, supplementing investment returns with principal for withdrawals

**That's it.** No rebalancing, no trading, no adjustments. Just automatic withdrawals and compounding.

## Annual Preservation Buy-and-Hold Checklist

### Once Per Year (30 minutes)

1. **Verify portfolio balance** (unchanged or growing)
   - If >30% decline, don't panic. Market recovers.

2. **Confirm withdrawals posted**
   - Have you received monthly/quarterly $6,667/$20,000?

3. **Check dividend reinvestment**
   - Are dividends automatically reinvested?
   - Or redirected to fund withdrawals?

4. **Calculate YTD return**
   - Portfolio performing per expectations (5-6%)?

5. **No other action required**
   - Don't rebalance
   - Don't trade
   - Don't adjust
   - Don't watch

### NEVER Do During Preservation:

- Don't check portfolio monthly (creates anxiety)
- Don't adjust allocations (ignore news)
- Don't try to "improve" returns (underperformance likely)
- Don't stop dividend reinvestment (need growth)
- Don't panic during crashes (they always recover)

## Common Preservation Buy-and-Hold Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Rebalancing Too Often**

Problem: Selling winners, realizing capital gains, paying taxes

Solution: Rebalance every 5 years only (or never)

**Mistake 2: Selling During Crashes**

Problem: Lock in losses, miss recovery

Solution: Predecide you will never sell during crashes. Stick to plan.

**Mistake 3: Chasing Returns**

Problem: Selling bonds to "get more growth," shifting to 80% stocks

Solution: Stick to original allocation. Bonds are there for stability, not returns.

**Mistake 4: Adjusting Withdrawal Rate Down**

Problem: Market drops 30%, panic, reduce spending

Solution: Actually increase withdrawal rate slightly during bear markets to force buying low

**Mistake 5: Not Accounting for Inflation**

Problem: $80,000 withdrawal in Year 1 becomes inadequate by Year 15

Solution: Increase withdrawal 3% annually for inflation
