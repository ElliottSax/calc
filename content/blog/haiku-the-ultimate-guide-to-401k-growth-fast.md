## Employer Match Optimization

Not all employer matches are equal. Strategic selection maximizes free money:

**Match Type 1: 100% of first 5%**
- You contribute 5% of $70,000 salary = $3,500
- Employer adds 100% = $3,500
- Total: $7,000 (free $3,500)

**Match Type 2: 50% of first 6%**
- You contribute 6% of $70,000 = $4,200
- Employer adds 50% = $2,100

**Conclusion:** Type 1 offers higher employer contribution ($3,500 vs $2,100).

**Profit-Sharing Opportunity:**
Some plans allow additional profit-sharing contributions up to 25% of compensation.

Example: $70,000 salary
- Profit-sharing allowance: 25% × $70,000 = $17,500
- Your contribution: $23,500
- Profit-sharing: $17,500 (if company contributes)
- **Total employer contribution: $17,500 (free money!)**

Verify whether your employer offers profit-sharing and structure bonuses to capture this advantage.

A: Over 20+ year periods, yes. Stocks average 10% historically; bonds 4-5%. Balanced allocation (75/25 stocks/bonds) averages 7.75%. During any specific year, returns vary dramatically.

**Q: Should I max contributions if I have debt?**
A: Generally yes if employer match is available (guaranteed 50-100% return). After capturing match, balance between debt reduction and additional 401k contributions.

**Q: Can I contribute more than $23,500?**

**Q: What happens to rapid contributions during job changes?**

**Q: Should I invest aggressively if I'm reaching rapid growth goals?**
A: Yes, until 10 years before retirement. After 10 years pre-retirement, gradually reduce equity allocation to 50-60% for stability.
