## Introduction

High-income earners ($200k+ annually, $5M+ net worth) can access sophisticated strategies generating 2-5%+ annual alpha through options strategies, direct stock picking, hedge fund allocation, and tax-efficient structuring unavailable to typical investors.

## Advanced Alpha Sources for High-Income Investors

### 1. Covered Call Strategies (1-2% alpha)

Sell covered calls against dividend positions:
- Dividend yield: 4%
- Call premium: 1.5-2% annually
- Total return: 5.5-6% vs. 4% passively
- Alpha: +1-2%

Example: $1M portfolio in SCHD ($4% dividend)
- Dividend income: $40,000
- Call premium from 30% of holdings: $15,000
- Total income: $55,000 ($5.5% yield)
- Alpha vs. dividend-only: +1.5%

### 2. Direct Stock Selection (1-3% alpha)

High-income investors with time/expertise can outpick:
- Beat SCHD by selecting better dividend stocks
- 70% of active managers underperform
- 30% consistently outperform by 1-3%
- Realistic alpha: 1-1.5% net of fees

### 3. Hedge Fund / Private Equity Allocation (2-4% alpha)

Accredited investors can access:
- Hedge funds: 2-3% alpha (net of fees)
- Private equity: 3-4% alpha (net of fees)
- Emerging funds: 4-5% alpha potential
- Requirement: $5M+ net worth

Example: $5M portfolio with 20% hedge fund allocation
- Hedge fund: $1M earning 8% = $80,000
- Bonds/stocks: $4M earning 8% = $320,000
- Total return: $400,000 (8% if all in bonds/stocks)
- Hedge premium: +0.4% alpha

### 4. Tax Arbitrage (1-2% alpha)

High earners maximize tax inefficiency gaps:
- Aggressive loss harvesting: $50,000+ annual losses
- Tax benefit: $15,000-$20,000 annually (37% marginal rate)
- On $5M portfolio: +0.3-0.4% alpha
- Plus: Traditional vs. Roth optimization (2-3% additional value)

### 5. Options Strategies (1-3% alpha)

Sophisticated investors trade options:
- Selling puts (cash-secured): +1-2% annual yield
- Spreads (iron condors): +0.5-1% monthly
- Realistic alpha: +1-2% net

## Risk Considerations

While alpha can reach 2-5%, risks increase:
- Covered calls cap upside (trade-off accepted)
- Direct stock picking introduces idiosyncratic risk
- Hedge funds can underperform (need careful selection)
- Options strategies require expertise (wrong execution destroys value)

Risk management: Start small (5-10% allocations), expand with success.

## FAQ

Q: Is 2-5% alpha realistic for sophisticated investors?
A: Yes. Historical data shows hedge funds average 2-3% net alpha. Direct stock picking with discipline achieves 1-2% alpha. Combined strategies → 2-5% possible.

Q: What's the minimum portfolio size for these strategies?
A: $2-3M minimum. Covered calls work at any size; hedge funds require $5M+ (accredited investor status).

Q: Should I attempt all strategies or specialize?
A: Start with 2-3 core strategies:
- Covered calls (moderate complexity, 1-2% alpha)
- Tax optimization (low complexity, 1-2% alpha value)
- Direct picking (high complexity, 1-1.5% alpha)

Master 2-3 before adding others.

Q: What happens if alpha strategies fail?
A: Expected risks:
- Covered calls miss upside (intentional trade-off)
- Hedge funds underperform (choose managers carefully)
- Options strategies can lose (use proper position sizing)

Mitigate: Use position sizing (10-25% per strategy), diversify approaches.
