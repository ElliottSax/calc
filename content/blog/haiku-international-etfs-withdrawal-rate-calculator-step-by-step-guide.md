## International Withdrawal Rate Considerations

International ETF withdrawal rates differ from domestic funds due to currency risk, tax withholding, and geopolitical factors. Conservative withdrawal rates (3-3.5%) suit international portfolios better than domestic 4% rule.

| Asset Class | Withdrawal Rate | Success Rate |

## Dynamic Withdrawal Adjustment

International investors should adjust withdrawals based on portfolio volatility:

**Guardrails approach for VXUS:**

- Increase annual withdrawal by 10%

If portfolio declines 25%:
- Decrease annual withdrawal by 10%

Otherwise:
- Adjust for inflation only

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Should international withdrawals be higher or lower than domestic?**
A: Lower. Use 3-3.5% for international versus 3.5-4% for domestic.

**Q2: How should I handle currency fluctuations in withdrawals?**
A: Use guardrails approach—adjust when portfolio changes 25%+ due to currency moves.

**Q3: Can I withdraw from international dividends only?**
A: Only if sufficient. VXUS at 2.1% provides $10,500 per $500,000, less than typical withdrawal needs.

**Q4: How do foreign taxes affect withdrawal sustainability?**
A: Significantly. International withdrawals net 15-20% less due to foreign withholding taxes.

**Q5: Should I use currency-hedged international ETFs for withdrawals?**
A: Yes. Reduces withdrawal volatility from currency swings, though at 0.3-0.5% annual cost.
