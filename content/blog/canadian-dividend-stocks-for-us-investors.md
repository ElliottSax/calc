## Introduction
As a US investor, diversifying your portfolio with international stocks can be a great way to reduce risk and increase potential returns. One of the most attractive markets for US investors is Canada, with its stable economy, strong banking system, and rich natural resources. Canadian dividend stocks, in particular, offer a compelling opportunity for income-seeking investors. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of investing in Canadian dividend stocks, analyze some top performers, and provide a strategy for US investors looking to add these stocks to their portfolio.

## Analysis
Canadian dividend stocks offer a unique combination of stability, growth, and income. Many of these companies have a long history of paying consistent dividends, making them attractive to investors seeking regular income. Additionally, the Canadian economy is closely tied to the US economy, making it easier for US investors to understand and navigate. Some of the top Canadian dividend stocks include:

* Enbridge Inc. (ENB), a leading energy infrastructure company with a current yield of 4.8% and a payout ratio of 70%. Enbridge has a strong track record of dividend growth, with a 5-year compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 10.3%.
* Royal Bank of Canada (RY), one of the largest banks in Canada, with a current yield of 3.9% and a payout ratio of 45%. Royal Bank has a long history of dividend payments, with a 5-year CAGR of 7.1%.
* Telus Corporation (TU), a leading telecommunications company, with a current yield of 4.2% and a payout ratio of 75%. Telus has a strong track record of dividend growth, with a 5-year CAGR of 8.5%.
* Canadian National Railway Company (CNI), a major railway company, with a current yield of 1.9% and a payout ratio of 35%. Canadian National has a long history of dividend payments, with a 5-year CAGR of 10.1%.
* Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD), another major bank in Canada, with a current yield of 3.7% and a payout ratio of 45%. Toronto-Dominion has a strong track record of dividend growth, with a 5-year CAGR of 8.1%.

These stocks offer a great combination of income, growth, and stability, making them attractive to US investors.

## Comparison
When comparing Canadian dividend stocks to their US counterparts, there are several key differences to consider. One of the main advantages of Canadian dividend stocks is their higher yields. Many Canadian companies have a long history of paying consistent dividends, which has driven up their yields over time. Additionally, the Canadian economy is closely tied to the US economy, making it easier for US investors to understand and navigate. However, there are also some potential drawbacks to consider. For example, Canadian stocks may be more volatile than US stocks, and the Canadian dollar may fluctuate against the US dollar, affecting the value of your investments.

## Strategy
So, how can US investors add Canadian dividend stocks to their portfolio? One approach is to invest directly in individual stocks, such as those listed above. This approach allows you to carefully select the stocks that best fit your investment goals and risk tolerance. Another approach is to invest in a Canadian dividend-focused exchange-traded fund (ETF) or mutual fund. These funds provide diversified exposure to a range of Canadian dividend stocks, making it easier to invest in the market as a whole. Some popular Canadian dividend ETFs include the Vanguard FTSE Canada All Cap Index ETF (VUN) and the iShares S&P/TSX Canadian Dividend Aristocrats Index ETF (CDZ).

When investing in Canadian dividend stocks, it's also important to consider the tax implications. As a US investor, you may be subject to withholding tax on dividend payments from Canadian companies. However, this tax can be reduced or eliminated through tax treaties between the US and Canada. It's also important to consider the currency risk associated with investing in Canadian stocks. The Canadian dollar may fluctuate against the US dollar, affecting the value of your investments. To mitigate this risk, you can consider investing in a Canadian dividend ETF or mutual fund that hedges against currency fluctuations.

## Conclusion
In conclusion, Canadian dividend stocks offer a compelling opportunity for US investors seeking income, growth, and stability. With their high yields, strong track records of dividend payments, and stable economy, these stocks are an attractive addition to any portfolio. With careful consideration of the tax implications and currency risk, US investors can build a diversified portfolio that includes the best of Canada's dividend stocks. Whether you're a seasoned investor or just starting out, Canadian dividend stocks are definitely worth considering as you build your investment portfolio.
