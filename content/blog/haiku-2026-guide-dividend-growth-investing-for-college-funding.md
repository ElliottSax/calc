## Dividend Growth Impact on College Funding

Dividend growth is often underestimated in college fund planning. Growing dividends transform static income into dynamic income.

### Formula: Future Dividend Income Projection

```
Future Dividend = Current Dividend × (1 + Growth Rate)^Years
```

**Worked Example:**

SCHD position in college fund: $100,000
Current dividend: $3,500 (3.5% yield)
Expected dividend growth: 10% annually

Year 1 dividend: $3,500
Year 5 dividend: $3,500 × (1.10)^4 = $5,124
Year 10 dividend: $3,500 × (1.10)^9 = $8,273
Year 18 dividend: $3,500 × (1.10)^17 = $16,922

**By Year 18, the same $100,000 SCHD position generates $16,922 annually in dividends.**

This creates a powerful college funding secondary stream: capital appreciation PLUS dividend income.

## Dividend Reinvestment Strategy

For college funds, dividend reinvestment (DRIP) is powerful.

### Formula: Dividend Reinvestment Impact

```
Future Value with Reinvestment = Dividend × (1 + Growth Rate)^Years + Compounded Reinvestments
```

**Worked Example:**

$100,000 SCHD position, 3.5% initial dividend, 10% annual dividend growth

**Without DRIP (reinvested manually as capital):**
- Year 1 dividend: $3,500 (reinvest as capital)
- Year 5 dividend: $5,124
- Year 10 dividend: $8,273
- Cumulative dividends reinvested: $43,000
- Total value: $100,000 + $43,000 = $143,000

**With automatic DRIP (dividends buy more shares):**
- Year 1: Buy $3,500 more shares (ownership: 103.5% of original)
- Year 5: Dividends compounding on compounding, ownership expanded
- Year 10: Significantly more shares due to compound reinvestment
- Total value: $158,000 (higher due to compounding)

**DRIP advantage: $15,000 additional wealth over 10 years**

## College Fund Dividend Growth Rebalancing

Unlike total market index funds, dividend growth portfolios need periodic rebalancing to maintain allocation.

### Annual Rebalancing Check

**Target allocation:**
- SCHD: 40%
- Aristocrats (JNJ, MCD, KO, PG): 30%
- VTI: 15%
- Cash: 5%

**After market movement (Year 5, $40,200 portfolio):**
- SCHD: $18,200 (45% vs. target 40%, overweight)
- Aristocrats: $12,100 (30%, at target)
- VTI: $6,500 (16%, slightly overweight)
- Cash: $3,400 (8%, overweight)

**Rebalancing action:**
- Sell $2,000 SCHD
- Sell $700 VTI
- Buy $900 bonds/money market cash

**Result:** Returns to target allocation

**Why rebalance?** Dividend-growth stocks often outperform total market, drifting allocation away from target. Annual rebalancing forces discipline.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I use dividend stocks or index funds for college?**
A: Combination works well. SCHD + index funds provides dividend growth + broad diversification.

**Q: What if dividend gets cut?**
A: Unlikely with aristocrats. If it happens, that company is removed from portfolio. Very rare.

**Q: Should I reinvest dividends or withdraw them?**
A: For college funds, reinvest. Dividends buy more shares, compounding growth.

**Q: Is 3.5% dividend yield enough for college funding?**
A: Yes. Capital appreciation (7% average) provides bulk of growth. Dividends amplify through reinvestment.

**Q: When should I shift to bonds?**
A: Year 16-17 (2 years before college), gradually shift 30% to bonds/cash for stability.
