# Growth Stocks Dollar-Cost Averaging Returns Calculator: Step-by-Step Guide

Dollar-cost averaging (DCA) reduces timing risk through systematic periodic investments. Calculating DCA returns accurately reveals whether systematic investing meets financial goals. This guide provides formulas, worked examples, and calculation methods.

## Understanding DCA Returns

DCA returns differ from lump-sum investing. Rather than single entry point, multiple entries at varying prices create averaged cost basis and smooth returns.

## DCA Return Formula

**Total DCA Return = (Final Portfolio Value - Total Invested) / Total Invested × 100%**

### Simple Example

DCA schedule:
- Month 1: $1,000 invested at $100/share = 10 shares
- Month 2: $1,000 invested at $95/share = 10.5 shares
- Month 3: $1,000 invested at $105/share = 9.5 shares
- Total invested: $3,000
- Shares purchased: 30 shares
- Current price: $110
- Current value: 30 × $110 = $3,300

**DCA Return = ($3,300 - $3,000) / $3,000 = 10%**

**Annualized return: (1.1296)^(1/5) - 1 = 2.45% annually**

## Comparing DCA vs Lump-Sum Returns

### Same $30,000 Investment

**Lump-sum approach:**
- Invest all $30,000 at month 1 at $85/share
- Shares: 352.94 shares
- Value at month 60 (at $110): $38,823.50
- Return: 29.4% total

**DCA approach:**
- $500 monthly for 60 months
- Average entry price: $97.40
- Shares: 308.07
- Value at month 60: $33,887.70
- Return: 12.96% total

In this bull market scenario, lump-sum beats DCA by 16.4%. However:

**If market declines 20% first then recovers:**
- Lump-sum: Loses $6,000 immediately
- DCA: Continues buying at discount for 12 months, reduces loss to $1,200

DCA advantage in downturns compensates for lump-sum advantage in bull markets.

## DCA with Rebalancing Effects

Quarterly rebalancing improves DCA returns:

Without rebalancing:
- Growth position drifts to 85% (up from 80% target)

With quarterly rebalancing:
- Systematic selling winners (locks gains)
- Systematic buying losers (captures recovery)
- Rebalancing adds approximately 2-3% annual performance
- Returns = 38.2% over 5 years

**Rebalancing benefit: +$1,470 additional wealth**

## FAQ

**Q: How often should I calculate DCA returns?**
A: Quarterly or annually. Frequent calculation encourages emotional trading.

**Q: What DCA return should I target?**
A: Growth stocks: 10-15% annually. Conservative: 7-10%. Balance: 8-12%.

**Q: Does DCA guarantee positive returns?**
A: No. Declining markets can produce negative DCA returns short-term. 10+ year periods typically generate positive returns.

**Q: How does DCA return compare to lump-sum?**
A: Lump-sum typically generates 0.6% higher returns annually, but DCA provides psychological comfort and risk reduction. Difference: minimal over 20+ years.

**Q: Should I adjust DCA amounts if market declines?**
A: Maintain discipline. Declining markets represent buying opportunities. Increase contributions if possible.
